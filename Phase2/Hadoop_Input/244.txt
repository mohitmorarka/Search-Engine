         <td class="colA" style="background-image: none; padding-bottom: 1em !important;" colspan="2"> <p>UCR’s central e-mail system is web-enabled and designed to meet the need of UCR faculty, students, and staff to securely and easily access UCR e-mail from anywhere in the world. UCR e-mail also provides campus users with important tools aimed at lessening the annoyance associated with spam and helping to address the growing threat from computer viruses that are spread via e-mail messages.</p> <p>Every UCR faculty and staff member is eligible for a free e-mail account, which is established automatically once a <a href="http://www.cnc.ucr.edu/esai/index.php?content=ucrnetid.html">UCR Net ID</a> has been obtained. Student e-mail accounts are automatically created when the Statement of Intent to Register is submitted. Once your account is established, you can access your e-mail whether you are on campus or off.</p> </td> 
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td class="colB"> <h3>For Faculty &amp; Staff</h3> <p>Microsoft Exchange-based e-mail is available for faculty and staff.</p> <h4>Web-based E-mail</h4> <p>Web-based access to Exchange is available via<a class="standout3" href="https://post.ucr.edu/owa"> Outlook Web Access</a></p> <h4>More Information</h4> <p>More information, as well as instructions to connect to Exchange on your computer or mobile device, is available at the <a class="standout3" href="/exchange/">Exchange support website</a></p> <p>For general e-mail questions and best practices, see <a class="standout3" href="/e-mail/">E-Mail @ UCR</a></p> </td> 
         <td class="colC"> <h3>For Students</h3> <p>Student e-mail is provided by Google-based R'Mail.</p> <h4>Web-based E-mail</h4> <p>Students may log in to R'Mail via the <a class="standout3" href="http://rweb.ucr.edu">R'Web student portal</a>&nbsp;</p> <p>Students may also log in directly to <a class="standout3" href="http://rmail.ucr.edu">R'Mail</a></p> <h4>More Information</h4> <p>More information, as well as instructions to connect to Exchange on your computer or mobile device, is available at the <a class="standout3" href="/rmail/">R'mail support website</a></p> </td>

     <h3>Enterprise Application &amp; Development<br /></h3> 
     <p>The Enterprise Application &amp; Development group provides enterprise-wide, computerized administrative systems to the UCR campus. Applications that are used by large numbers of staff across campus generally fall within our scope of responsibility.</p> 
     <p>Our deployed systems include the UCR Financial System (UCRFS) and all of its related utilities, the campus Purchasing system, various G/L feeder systems such as Physical Plant, Communications, Mail Services and Web Recharge, as well as a host of other tools and decision support systems including UCRFStotals, SuperDOPE, and the Enterprise Reporting System (ERS).</p>

      <h1>Sentry</h1> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">Large Scale Drawings by Joe Biel</h2> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">Sweeney Art Gallery</h2> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">Dec 21, 2013 - Mar 22, 2014</h2> 
      <h3 class="spaced-out">Reception - Sat, Feb 1, 2014, 6pm - 9pm<br /></h3> 
      <br /> 
      <br /> 
      <p> UCR ARTSblock presents <em>Sentry: Works by Joe Biel</em>, a solo museum exhibition featuring a series of large-scale, two-dimensional drawings on paper and a monumental drawing installation specifically produced for the Culver Center of the Arts. Working primarily with watercolor, graphite and colored pencils, Biel's figurative drawings are loosely composed in panoramic landscapes with latent narratives that tamper with the complexity and irony of human existence. In addition to the works on paper, visitors of the museum will be able to view Biel's new work in progress- a site-specific drawing that will encompass the grand atrium walls of the Culver Center. This large-scale installation drawing, <em>Sentry</em>, will be completed by the opening date of the exhibition on December 21, 2013. <br /><br /> Joe Biel received an MFA in Painting from the University of Michigan and is currently on the faculty as Associate Professor in Studio Art at California State University, Fullerton. His work has been exhibited in galleries nationally and internationally at <em>LA Louver Gallery, Roberts and Tilton Gallery, Acuna-Hansen Gallery</em> and <em>Angles Gallery</em> in Los Angeles, <em>Goff + Rosenthal Gallery</em> in New York, <em>Greg Kucera Gallery</em> in Seattle, and <em>Galerie Kuckei/Kuckei</em> in Berlin. He has been included in group exhibitions at the Otis College of Art and Design and the Torrance Museum of Art in Los Angeles, the De Young Museum in San Francisco, the Laguna Art Museum in Laguna, CA, the Aldrich Contemporary Art Museum in Ridgefield, CT and Diverseworks in Houston, TX. He has been awarded residencies at the Bemis Center for Contemporary Art in Omaha, NE, at the 18th St Arts Center in Santa Monica and at the Ballingllen Foundation in Ballycastle, Ireland. He was the recipient of a Pollock-Krasner Foundation award in 2003 and in 2008. He lives and works in Los Angeles. <br /><br /> <i>SENTRY: Large Scale Drawings</i> by Joe Biel&nbsp;was organized by UCR ARTSblock and curated by Jennifer Frias, Associate Curator, Sweeney Art Gallery, University of California, Riverside. Support provided by UCR's College of Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences (CHASS), Gluck Fellows Program of the Arts at UC Riverside, University of California Institute of Research for the Arts (UCIRA) and the City of Riverside.<font face="Helvetica"><span style="color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-size: 13px; line-height: normal;">&nbsp;</span></font><br /><br /> Artist website<br /> <a href="http://www.joebiel.com">http://www.joebiel.com</a> <br /><br /> Image credit line: <br /> Joe Biel, <em>Pax Romana</em>, 2006, watercolor, colored pencil and graphite on paper, courtesy of the artist. </p>
