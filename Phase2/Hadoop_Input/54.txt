     <h2 class="intro"> Strength to Succeed</h2> 
     <h3 class="verd intro"> Whether you’re a veteran, a transfer student, or a student with a <br /> unique need, you have total support at UCR.</h3> 
     <div class="genericBody version3"> 
      <div class="clearfix" style="margin-bottom: 30px;"> 
       <div class="grid_4 alpha"> 
        <img alt="Two female students, back-to-back" width="219" height="317" src="/Images/women_support_services.jpg" /> 
       </div> 
       <div class="grid_8 omega"> 
        <br /> 
        <h3> Choose the Right Path </h3> 
        <h4><a href="http://firstyear.ucr.edu/learning.html">First Year Learning Communities</a><a href="#"> </a> </h4> 
        <p> Meet fellow freshman taking similar coursework, form study groups and support one another’s success. Learn more about First Year Learning Communities: </p> 
        <ul> 
         <li><a href="http://firstyear.ucr.edu/bourns.html">First Year: Bourns College of Engineering</a> </li> 
         <li><a href="http://firstyear.ucr.edu/chass.html">First Year: College of Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences</a> </li> 
         <li><a href="http://firstyear.ucr.edu/cnas.html">First Year: College of Natural and Agricultural Sciences</a> </li> 
         <li><a href="http://firstyear.ucr.edu/honorsprogram.html">First Year: University Honors Program</a> </li> 
         <li><a href="http://firstyear.ucr.edu/academic.html">First Year: Medical Scholars</a></li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
      <div class="clearfix" style="margin-bottom: 30px;"> 
       <div class="grid_4 alpha"> 
        <h3> Access Everything</h3> 
        <h4> <a href="http://www.specialservices.ucr.edu/disabilities/Pages/default.aspx">Services for Students with Disabilities</a></h4> 
        <p style="line-height: 150%"> Fully participate in all aspects of campus life and enjoy equal access to educational, cultural and social programs.<br /> </p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="grid_4"> 
        <h3> Maximize Benefits</h3> 
        <h4> <a href="http://www.specialservices.ucr.edu/veterans/Pages/default.aspx">Services for Veterans and their Families</a></h4> 
        <p style="line-height: 150%"> Obtain the&nbsp;educational benefits you’ve proudly earned for yourself and eligible family members.<br /> </p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="grid_4 omega"> 
        <h3> Raise Your Grades</h3> 
        <h4> <a href="http://arc.ucr.edu/">The Academic Resource Center</a> </h4> 
        <p style="line-height: 150%"> Benefit from free tutoring provided by educators and advanced students who believe in your ability to succeed.</p> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
      <div class="clearfix" style="margin-bottom: 30px;"> 
       <div class="grid_8 alpha"> 
        <h3>Connect and Lead </h3> 
        <h4> <a href="https://www.ucr.edu/students/ethnic_gender.html">Ethnic and Gender Programs</a><a href="#"></a></h4> 
        <p> Access support services, find community, participate in cultural events and hone leadership skills with UCR’s Ethnic and Gender programs. </p> 
        <ul> 
         <li><a href="http://asp.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">African Student Programs</a> </li> 
         <li><a href="http://apsp.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">Asian Pacific Student Programs</a> </li> 
         <li><a href="http://csp.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">Chicano Student Programs</a> </li> 
         <li><a href="http://out.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">Lesbian Gay Bisexual Transgender Resource Center</a> </li> 
         <li><a href="http://nasp.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">Native American Student Programs</a></li> 
         <li><a href="http://wrc.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">Women’s Resource Center</a></li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <div class="grid_4 omega"> 
        <img alt="Two male students, hand on shoulder" width="221" height="318" src="/Images/men_support_services.jpg" /> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
      <div class="grid_8 omega"> 
       <h3> Improve Your Wellbeing</h3> 
       <h4> <a href="http://well.ucr.edu/About/Pages/WellPartnerInformation.aspx">The Student Wellness Partners</a><a href="#"></a></h4> 
       <p> Soothe stress, get fit, and feel well inside and out with campus resources that serve your healthy mind and body. </p> 
       <ul style="margin-top: -12px;"> 
        <li><a href="http://campushealth.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">Campus Health Center&nbsp;</a></li>

        <li><a href="http://housing.ucr.edu">Housing, Dining &amp; Residential Services</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://internationalcenter.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">International Student Resource Center</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://www.recreation.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">Student Recreation Center&nbsp;</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://well.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">The Well</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div>

       <p>RIVERSIDE, Calif. – A vision of a greener future was on display at the UC Riverside Alumni and Visitor Center last week as the university, the City of Riverside and Riverside Public Utilities (RPU) partnered to host the second Green Leadership Summit on Feb. 26, 2014.</p> 
       <p>The more than 80 guests in attendance saw presentations and displays of Riverside’s current sustainability practices, methods of motivating recent UCR students to stay and work locally following graduation, current sustainability successes at UCR and RPU, and ways to improve green practices throughout the region. In attendance were representatives from UCR, RPU, the city of Riverside, and local businesses and non-profits. Dignitaries included UCR Chancellor Kim Wilcox, Riverside Mayor Rusty Bailey, RPU Assistant General Manager Michael Bacich, former mayor and current UCR professor Ron Loveridge, UCR Director of Sustainability John Cook and UCR Director of CE-CERT Matt Barth.</p> 
       <p>Speakers from UCR discussed the university’s commitment to sustainability. Wilcox discussed future goals for the campus, which included the reduction of use of potable (drinking-quality) water for campus operations, increased application of UCR research in areas of global warming, air quality, agriculture, plant biology, solar energy and transportation and the achievement of climate neutrality, where the campus would have no net-climate-impact from carbon or other greenhouse gases, by 2025.</p> 
       <p>At the event’s conclusion, Wilcox and Bailey signed a collaborative agreement to work together to ensure sustainability for the current and future residents of Riverside. The agreement provides for two-to-four paid internships for UCR undergraduate and graduate students to assist the city on sustainability projects. These internships will aid the city in further advancing sustainability while providing encouragement for students to stay in Riverside after graduation.</p> 
       <p>“As a member of the UCR community, it’s hard not to feel proud of all that we have accomplished as a university and as a community. But, it’s also very humbling to think of the work that still needs to be done,” Wilcox said.</p> 
       <p>Mayor Rusty Bailey showed support for efforts to date and promised support for sustainability projects moving forward.&nbsp;“We’re continuing the momentum of previous generations and mayors with the mission of sustainability. I’m proud to carry the torch and take a step forward to continue our work.&nbsp;We’re in growth mode and we need to do things the right way.”</p> 
       <p>The signed agreement drew significant praise from attendees.</p> 
       <p>“If the saying is true, that it takes a village to raise a child, then it is also true that it takes a university to allow that child to flourish,” Cook said. “This partnership allows UCR to utilize its research to better help the community. That’s the beauty of partnership and it is a situation very unique to UCR. No other UC has this kind of relationship with their city.”</p> 
       <p>Echoing Cook’s hopefulness, Barth said he hopes that the collaboration will allow UCR to become an even greater asset to the city and the community.</p> 
       <p>Bailey also mentioned the <a href="http://coolclimate.berkeley.edu/challenge" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://coolclimate.berkeley.edu/challenge']);">CoolCalifornia City Challenge</a> as a resource for individuals who want to make a difference in sustainability now. The challenge, whose sponsors include Energy Upgrade California and the University of California Renewable and Appropriate Energy Laboratory, encourages residents to reduce their carbon footprints, and earn prize money to help support sustainability program development.</p> 
       <p>For more information about the city’s green initiatives, visit <a title="Green Riverside" href="http://www.greenriverside.com.">greenriverside.com.</a></p>
