      <p>RIVERSIDE, Calif. — Gladys M. Eaton, whose husband’s collection of science fiction books formed the basis of UC Riverside’s Eaton Collection of Science Fiction and Fantasy, died Jan. 22. She was 92.</p> 
      <p>A memorial service will be held March 8 in the Oakland Museum in Oakland, Calif.</p> 
      <p>Mrs. Eaton met J. Lloyd Eaton, a pulmonologist, while working as an office manager in a medical group in Oakland. The two married in 1956. She was active in charitable and nonprofit organizations, among them the Women’s Auxiliary of the Alameda-Contra Costa Medical Association, the Alameda County Heart Association and the Christmas Seal Drive in Alameda County. She was an avid photographer, served as a docent at the Oakland Museum and on the board of the UC Botanical Gardens, and supported scholarship programs of the Oakland Art Association.</p> 
      <p>Her husband, J. Lloyd Eaton, was a pioneering science fiction fan in Northern California whose collection of some 7,500 books — many of them dedicated to him by their authors — was acquired by UC Riverside in 1969, a year after his death.</p> 
      <p>In an article published in the Oakland Art Association newsletter in August 2007, Gladys Eaton recalled her efforts to catalog her husband’s collection while they were married. The Eaton Collection of Science Fiction and Fantasy originated with those volumes and has grown to more than 300,000 items. It is the largest publicly accessible collection of its kind in the world and draws scholars from North America and around the globe.</p> 
      <p>“Mrs. Eaton was very devoted to Lloyd and has been very generous to UCR,” said Melissa Conway, head of Special Collections &amp; University Archives at UCR. “She was a steadfast supporter of the Eaton Collection and Eaton Conference. I enjoyed our many years of friendship and will miss her very much.”</p> 
      <p>Mrs. Eaton was preceded in death by her husband, sister Bernice Buessing and brother Quentin Ronnebaum. She is survived by her brother Werner Ronnebaum of Seneca, Kan., Lloyd Eaton’s children and grandchildren, and many nieces and nephews.</p>

      <h5>Teaching at the UC Washington Center</h5> 
      <p><br /><strong>Academic Senate Faculty Positions Open Call for Academic Year 2015-16</strong></p> 
      <p>The University of California's Washington Center (UCDC) seeks to fill openings for UC Academic Senate Faculty to teach in its consolidated UCDC undergraduate programs for the AY 2015-16.&nbsp; The UCDC program provides opportunities for undergraduates from nine UC campuses to study and participate in public service, science policy and arts internships in Washington, DC for a quarter or semester.</p> 
      <p>We will accept applications for the academic year or for one term.&nbsp; The usual academic year teaching load is three quarter and one semester seminars of 25 students or less.&nbsp; There are themed seminars in areas such as Congress, the Presidency, and environment.&nbsp; Research seminars link research and writing assignments to the students' internships.</p> 
      <p>Faculty interested in residence for less than the academic year may apply by submitting a proposal to teach an elective.&nbsp; Their home departments will be compensated by funds to cover the lecturer salary for one full-time lecture course per term.</p> 
      <p>Anyone interested in the Academic Senate Faculty positions at UCDC for AY 2015-16 should email a letter of application, CV and course proposal to the Executive Director of the UC Washington Center, Melanie DuPuis, at melanie.dupuis@ucdc.edu.&nbsp; Applications will be accepted until all positions are filled.</p>

      <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial;">06/24/08 (6:30 am): </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial;">Power has been restored to all buildings except the Arts buildings. This building is closed for classes, events, and faculty/staff work until further notice. </span></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">&nbsp;</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial;">06/23/08 (5:30 pm): Power outages have been attributed to a plumbing problem, which have been isolated by the Physical Plant department. Utility services are expected to be restored by tomorrow 06/24/08 (8:00 am). Faculty, staff, and students should anticipate normal work and class schedules. The campus is advised to check this website for any last minute updates.</span></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">&nbsp;</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial;">06/23/08 (4:00 pm):&nbsp;At approximately 1:00 pm this afternoon, a 10&quot; water line in the steam tunnels had burst and caused flooding in several buildings (Hinderaker, Fine Arts, and Physical Education). Power outages spread to other buildings in the same region of the campus. Building occupants were safely evacuated and advised to leave the campus. People were directed to refrain from using elevators (even in unaffected buildings) due to the unpredictable electrical service. Affected buildings included: </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial;">Anderson Graduate School of Management, CHASS North, Fine Arts, Humanities, HMNSS, Hinderaker Hall, Kenn Hall, Physical Education, Rivera Library, Surge, Univ</span><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial;">ersity Lecture Hall, and Watkins Hall. Classes and events scheduled in these buildings were cancelled for the rest of the evening.</span></p>
