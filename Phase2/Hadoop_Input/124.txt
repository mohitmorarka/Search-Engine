          <h3>BE BIG</h3> 
          <p align="justify"><a href="/education/graduate/"><img class="img_L_borderless" src="/images/be_big.png" alt="Be BIG" width="192" height="137" /></a>The Bioengineering Interdepartmental Graduate (<a href="/education/graduate/">BIG</a>) program is exceptional with an additional 30 faculty across UCR, enhancing research opportunities for their students. A network of research centers, including the newly established Center for Bioengineering Research (<a href="/research/cbr/">CBR</a>), provides extensive support for their research programs with core capabilities in genomics, bioimaging, bioinformatics, proteomics, nanotechnology, analytical chemistry and stem cell technology. And BIG is one of the few biomedical engineering programs where the graduate students can take&nbsp;<a href="/education/graduate/courses.html#medical">non-clinical medical school courses</a>&nbsp;alongside of the medical students.</p> 
          <p align="justify">All of this culminates into a truly exceptional educational and research program to produce the best graduates for future advancements in Bioengineering, Biomedical Engineering and Biotechnology. To learn more,&nbsp;<a href="mailto:bioeng@engr.ucr.edu">contact bioeng@engr.ucr.edu</a>.</p>

        <div id="living_the_promise"> 
         <div class="texttab-column"> 
          <h3>LIVING THE PROMISE</h3> 
          <h4>Bioengineering</h4> 
          <p align="justify">Antibiotics. Artificial joints. Pacemakers, implants and heart valves. These are but a few of the extraordinary medical breakthroughs brought to us over the last several decades by the rapidly evolving science of bioengineering.</p> 
          <p align="justify">Today, UCR’s uniquely interdisciplinary bioengineering program combines the expertise of biologists, neuroscientists, nanotechnologists, physiologists, mathematicians, geneticists and others to push the boundaries of this dynamic field. From the discovery of powerful new drugs and diagnostic tools to the development of novel biocompatible materials that will revolutionize 21st century medicine, our researchers and graduates collaborate with pharmaceutical companies, medical device manufacturers and other organizations to put the power of groundbreaking ideas to work in the real world.</p>

       <p>Janet Napolitano was named the 20<sup>th</sup> president of the University of California on July 18, 2013, and took office on Sept. 30, 2013. She leads a university system with 10 campuses, five medical centers, three affiliated national laboratories, and a statewide agriculture and natural resources program. The UC system has more than 234,000 students, about 208,000 faculty and staff, more than 1.6 million living alumni and an annual operating budget of more than $24 billion.&nbsp;<a href="about/index.html">Read more</a></p>

      <h3>Correctly Format Your Syllabus</h3>
      <br /> 
      <h4>Attention Faculty and Preparers:</h4> 
      <p>Whether creating, restoring or changing a course, faculty must supply preparers with an appropriately-formatted syllabus.</p> 
      <h4>Format</h4> 
      <p>Outline the course by week, date, or topic and include weighted grading criteria. Required reading should be explained with a reading list linked to weeks, dates or topics. </p> 
      <h4>If you are:</h4> 
      <ul class="orange-bullets"> 
       <li>Creating a new course</li> 
       <li>Restoring an old course</li> 
       <li>Changing or adding consultation hours for an existing course</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p>You must explain how consultation hours are implemented and monitored.</p> 
      <h4>If you are:</h4> 
      <ul class="orange-bullets"> 
       <li>Creating a new course</li> 
       <li>Restoring an old course</li> 
       <li>Changing or adding noncontact hours for an existing course</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p>You must describe course activities that do not involve faculty contact and explain how they are evaluated.</p> 
      <h4>Distance Education Technologies</h4> 
      <p>If your course uses distance education technologies, please describe the instructional arrangements.</p>
