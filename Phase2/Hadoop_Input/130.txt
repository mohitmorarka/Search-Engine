              // http://stackoverflow.com/questions/27745/getting-parts-of-a-url-regex)
              var uri_regex = /^((http[s]?|ftp):\/)?\/?([^:\/\s]+)(:([^\/]*))?((\/\w+)*\/)([\w\-\.]+[^#?\s]+)(\?([^#]*))?(#(.*))?$/;

         <h2> UC DELIVERS </h2> 
         <div class="spotlightstory"> 
          <div class="spotpic" style="width:175px"> 
           <img src="/CFFileServlet/_cf_image/_cfimg3675491982263792655.PNG" alt="Factors and Practices that Influence Livestock Distribution" class="none" /> 
          </div> 
          <div class="spottext">
            Reducing livestock impacts on water quality, aquatic and riparian habitat, and biodiversity are continuing goals for livestock producers, natural resource managers, and conservation groups. These livestock impacts are frequently due to problems with livestock distribution. While fences are usually an effective tool for controlling livestock distribution and reducing impacts on riparian zones or other critical areas, manipulation of grazing patterns can also effectively reduce adverse impacts from livestock. These practices can also facilitate the use of grazing to manipulate vegetation to meet management goals. It is crucial that livestock producers, land managers, community watershed groups, environmental interest groups and policy makers understand the factors that influence where animals graze, rest, and drink, and how livestock can be predictably and effectively redistributed so that they do not produce undesirable effects in grazed watersheds.

Close the form to return to the database.</li> 
          <li><b><u>Click SAVE WORK</u></b> to make a <u>working copy</u> of the course and to update the Action field. <p> <img border="0" alt="" src="../../images/course-mgt/chapter11two.jpg" /><br /> </p> </li> 
          <li>Modify or complete the First Effective Quarter and Year, Justification, Syllabus, and Approvals fields, and the fields onthe Other Courses/Programs page if necessary, but do not complete the Cross-listed field until step 8 below. To modify or complete a field, do the following: 
           <ol style="list-style:lower-alpha;"> 
            <li><u>Right-click</u> in the field and click Modify.<br /> <b>NOTE: </b>Clicking on History displays the history of a field (all previous versions). The Compare option is available only on longer fields and is designed for use only with courses that have been returned to you by the Reviewer or an approver (see <a href="returned-courses.aspx">&quot;Returned Courses Guide&quot;</a>). <p> <img border="0" alt="" src="../../images/course-mgt/chapter11two1.jpg" /></p> <p> <br /> </p> </li>

            <li>Enter the Field Justification if one displays and check spelling, if necessary.</li> 
            <li>Click OK to exit the field. </li> 
           </ol> </li>
