       <div id="ctl00_PlaceHolderMain_ctl01__ControlWrapper_RichHtmlField" style="display: inline"> 
        <h4 class="section_title2">Bienvenidos a nuestra familia de UCR!</h4> 
        <p>We celebrate the traditions of Chicano/Latino culture while sharing the spirit of La Raza with our campus and community. To our Chicano/Latino students, we are one big familia, and like a family, we fully support your academic, cultural and social success.</p> 
        <h5>WE:</h5> 
        <h6>SUPPORT</h6> 
        <p>We create a positive environment for Chicano/Latino students at UCR by offering supportive <a title="Programs and Services" href="../programs/Pages/default.aspx">services and programs</a> that encourage academic success and retention. We provide service referral when necessary, and serve as a resource for advocacy, as well. </p> 
        <h6>SHARE</h6> 
        <p>We educate our campus and community about Chicano/Latino culture through our exciting <a title="Events" href="../quepasaraza/Pages/default.aspx">events</a>, active <a title="Student Organizations" href="../studentinvolvement/Pages/chicano.aspx">student organizations</a>, and rewarding <a title="Outreach Programs" href="../studentinvolvement/Pages/outreach.aspx">outreach programs</a>, too.</p> 
        <h6>CONNECT</h6> 
        <p>We collaborate with other <a title="Our Partners" href="../ourfamilia/Pages/ourpartners.aspx">UCR departments with shared goals</a>; encourage <a title="Our Faculty" href="../ourfamilia/Pages/ourfaculty.aspx">faculty and staff</a> mentorship; reach out to our local community; and maintain a helpful campus, community and <a title="Our Alumni" href="../alumni/pages/default.aspx">alumni network</a> that help our students grow.</p> 
        <h6>INSPIRE</h6> 
        <p>Our outstanding <a title="Our Faculty" href="../ourfamilia/Pages/ourfaculty.aspx">faculty</a>, <a title="Our Staff" href="../ourfamilia/Pages/ourstaff.aspx">staff</a> and <a title="Student Organizations" href="../studentinvolvement/Pages/chicano.aspx">students</a> inspire future members of <a title="Our Familia" href="../ourfamilia/pages/default.aspx">our familia</a> and demonstrate true orgullo y coraz&oacute;n. </p>

     <div class="clear section"></div> 
     <p>CE-CERT congratulates Ji (Jill) Luo and Qiu (Apple) Jin for both receiving the 2013 Women’s Transportation Scholarship! Jill and Apple each received a $3,000 Helene M. Overly Scholarship!&nbsp;The Helene M. Overly Memorial Scholarship was established in 1981 to encourage women to pursue career paths in transportation. The scholarship is awarded to women pursuing graduate studies in transportation or a related field.&nbsp;</p> 
     <p><img class="borderless" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" src="/images/news/applejillwts.jpg" alt="apple jill" width="506" height="361" /></p> 
     <p><strong>Jill Luo</strong>&nbsp;<em>(left)</em> is pursuing her Ph.D. in Chemical and Environmental Engineering here at UCR - working under Dr. Matthew Barth at CE-CERT. Jill's research is focused on transportation-related emissions and air quality impacts as part of the &quot;Transportation Systems Research&quot; program at UCR, and her pollutant-minimizing algorithm research has been published by the 2013 Intelligent Vehicle Conference Proceedings. &nbsp;</p> 
     <p><strong>Qui &quot;Apple&quot; Jin</strong>&nbsp;<em>(right)</em> is pursuing her Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering here at UCR - working under Dr. Matthew Barth at CE-CERT. Apple's profilic involvement in transportation research has included developing new innovative traffic signal algorithms that are not only more efficient in improving mobility, but are also designed to specifically to reduce pollutant emissions and save fuel.&nbsp;</p> 
     <p><strong>Background information about the Women’s Transportation Scholarship: </strong>Founded in 1977, the Women’s Transportation Seminar (<a title="" href="http://www.wtsinternational.org/" target="_blank">WTS</a>) is an international organization dedicated to the professional advancement of women in transportation.&nbsp; The Inland Empire Chapter (<a title="" href="http://www.wtsie.org/" target="_blank">WTS-IE</a>) was formed in 2005, and is one of 46 Chapters worldwide. &nbsp;&nbsp;With over 200 Chapter members, one of WTS-IE’s primary goals is to provide scholarships for women pursuing careers in transportation. Each year, women with permanent residence or who are enrolled at a College or University in the Inland Empire are invited to apply for a scholarship. This includes high school seniors, who plan to enroll at a college or university during the following fall.&nbsp;</p> 
     <p>In memory of Helene M. Overly, the first Executive Secretary of WTS, the Board of Directors voted to name the annual WTS graduate scholarship, to honor Helene for her dedicated service to WTS. Helene became a member in 1981. She brought with her 15 years of experience in public service, tremendous organizational abilities, and a spirit of determination that helped WTS nearly double its membership in two years.&nbsp;</p> 
     <p>To learn more information about the women the scholarships are named after: <strong>Susan Van&nbsp;Note and Helene M. Overly Memorial Scholarships</strong>&nbsp;-&nbsp; go to this&nbsp;<a title="Scholarship Honorees" href="http://wtsie.org/scholarships/scholarship-honorees/">link</a>.</p> 
     <p>Women in Transportation website:&nbsp;<a href="http://wtsie.org/">http://wtsie.org/</a></p>
