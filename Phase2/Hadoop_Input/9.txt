     <h3>Cutting Edge Research that Reaches the World</h3> 
     <p> Through <a href="http://or.ucr.edu/">Research and Economic Development</a> and the dedicated work of faculty and student researchers in the pursuit of new knowledge, UC Riverside is fulfilling its role as the research university of Inland Southern California. </p> 
     <p> The university's research heritage, our resources and our location make it an ideal place for exploring air, water, energy, biodiversity, sustainability, land use, the habitat/agriculture interface and much more. </p> 
     <p> What we do and study here makes a difference to people every day: Every orange, lemon, lime and tangerine found on your grocer’s shelf has a genetic connection to the trees at our Citrus Research Collection. Our scientists have earned worldwide recognition for their expertise and breakthrough findings in the areas of plant sciences, environmental and natural resources and pest management. </p> 
     <p> Our recent research innovations include: </p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Engine technology that allows cars to run on a mix of hydrogen and natural gas.</li> 
      <li>New varieties of citrus and asparagus.</li> 
      <li>An efficient and clean method of turning waste materials into a sulfur-free diesel fuel.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <div class="nota-bene"> 
      <h3><em>We’re Making the World a Better Place, One Discovery at a Time.</em></h3> 
      <ul> 
       <li>In 1980, we released our first patented citrus variety, the Oroblanco grapefruit. Other citrus breeding program releases include the Melogold grapefruit and the Gold Nugget mandarin.</li> 
       <li>Plant physiologist Charles Coggins registered gibberellic acid for use in California citrus groves to delay fruit maturation. The ultimate result of his work was the extension of the citrus-growing season in California from four months to nine. 
        <div class="figure"> 
         <img src="/research/images/main_images/research_citrus.jpg" alt="Picture of Citrus Fruit" width="413" height="134" /> 
         <p>For over 100 years, UCR has been instrumental in the improvement of citrus production.</p> 
        </div> </li> 
       <li>In 1967, biochemist and molecular biologist Anthony Norman was one of two U.S. scientists working independently who isolated and described the chemical nature of a substance the public knew as vitamin D, but was, in fact, a steroid hormone. Several life-saving drug treatments were developed as a result of research by the Norman lab. </li> 
       <li>William Frankenberger, professor of soil microbiology and biochemistry, developed patented technologies using fungi and other microbial agents that naturally detoxify water contaminated with selenium, perchlorate, and nitrate.</li> 
       <li>Our high-energy physicists were part of an international effort that provided the world with evidence of the top quark — a fundamental particle which was first theorized about in the 1960s but never before observed.</li> 
       <li>Chemist Guy Bertrand and his research team were the first to create a stable singlet diradical, a development announced worldwide. The unique compound could lead to a new generation of non-metallic magnetic devices for medical imaging or electronics.</li> 
       <li>Plant pathologist Noel Keen and biochemist Fran Jurnak identified a previously unknown protein structure, the parallel beta-helix as part of Keen’s pioneering work in plant pathology. </li> 
       <li>Plant geneticist Michael Clegg led a research group that was the first to recover and decode ancient plant DNA genetic material from a magnolia leaf 20 million years old. </li> 
       <li>Geophysicist Harry Green II and graduate student Pamela Burnley provided the first explanation for “deep-focus earthquakes,” which occur 300 kilometers or more beneath the earth’s surface.</li> 
      </ul>

     <h3>Cutting Edge Research that Reaches the World</h3> 
     <p> Through <a href="http://or.ucr.edu/">Research and Economic Development</a> and the dedicated work of faculty and student researchers in the pursuit of new knowledge, UC Riverside is fulfilling its role as the research university of Inland Southern California. </p> 
     <p> The university's research heritage, our resources and our location make it an ideal place for exploring air, water, energy, biodiversity, sustainability, land use, the habitat/agriculture interface and much more. </p> 
     <p> What we do and study here makes a difference to people every day: Every orange, lemon, lime and tangerine found on your grocer’s shelf has a genetic connection to the trees at our Citrus Research Collection. Our scientists have earned worldwide recognition for their expertise and breakthrough findings in the areas of plant sciences, environmental and natural resources and pest management. </p> 
     <p> Our recent research innovations include: </p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Engine technology that allows cars to run on a mix of hydrogen and natural gas.</li> 
      <li>New varieties of citrus and asparagus.</li> 
      <li>An efficient and clean method of turning waste materials into a sulfur-free diesel fuel.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <div class="nota-bene"> 
      <h3><em>We’re Making the World a Better Place, One Discovery at a Time.</em></h3> 
      <ul> 
       <li>In 1980, we released our first patented citrus variety, the Oroblanco grapefruit. Other citrus breeding program releases include the Melogold grapefruit and the Gold Nugget mandarin.</li> 
       <li>Plant physiologist Charles Coggins registered gibberellic acid for use in California citrus groves to delay fruit maturation. The ultimate result of his work was the extension of the citrus-growing season in California from four months to nine. 
        <div class="figure"> 
         <img src="/research/images/main_images/research_citrus.jpg" alt="Picture of Citrus Fruit" width="413" height="134" /> 
         <p>For over 100 years, UCR has been instrumental in the improvement of citrus production.</p> 
        </div> </li> 
       <li>In 1967, biochemist and molecular biologist Anthony Norman was one of two U.S. scientists working independently who isolated and described the chemical nature of a substance the public knew as vitamin D, but was, in fact, a steroid hormone. Several life-saving drug treatments were developed as a result of research by the Norman lab. </li> 
       <li>William Frankenberger, professor of soil microbiology and biochemistry, developed patented technologies using fungi and other microbial agents that naturally detoxify water contaminated with selenium, perchlorate, and nitrate.</li> 
       <li>Our high-energy physicists were part of an international effort that provided the world with evidence of the top quark — a fundamental particle which was first theorized about in the 1960s but never before observed.</li> 
       <li>Chemist Guy Bertrand and his research team were the first to create a stable singlet diradical, a development announced worldwide. The unique compound could lead to a new generation of non-metallic magnetic devices for medical imaging or electronics.</li> 
       <li>Plant pathologist Noel Keen and biochemist Fran Jurnak identified a previously unknown protein structure, the parallel beta-helix as part of Keen’s pioneering work in plant pathology. </li> 
       <li>Plant geneticist Michael Clegg led a research group that was the first to recover and decode ancient plant DNA genetic material from a magnolia leaf 20 million years old. </li> 
       <li>Geophysicist Harry Green II and graduate student Pamela Burnley provided the first explanation for “deep-focus earthquakes,” which occur 300 kilometers or more beneath the earth’s surface.</li> 
      </ul>

     <h3>Partnerships Promoting Student Wellness</h3> 
     <p>UCR is an environment for students, staff and faculty that promotes a vibrant, healthy and balanced lifestyle. We have several integrated wellness programs that foster a culture of health and wellness and promote the importance of maintaining an optimal quality of life.</p>
     <!-- /com.omniupdate.div --> 
     <div class="boxB"> 
      <!-- com.omniupdate.div label="maincontent2" group="Everyone" button="707" -->

      <h4><a class="standout2" href="http://campushealth.ucr.edu/Services">Campus Health Center</a></h4> 
      <p><img class="img_L" src="/students/images/healthcenter_thumb.jpg" alt="Campus Health Center" width="93" height="46" />UCR’s Campus Health Center provides convenient, affordable and easily accessible medical care to students. All registered students are eligible to use the center. Students with the UC Student Health Insurance Plan have no out-of-pocket expenses for doctor’s visits. Students with private insurance will have a $20 fee for doctor’s visits. Lab work, X-rays, procedures and pharmaceuticals carry a range of fees. For appointments, call (951) 827-3031.</p> 
      <p><strong>Services include:</strong></p>
      <!-- /com.omniupdate.div --> 
      <div class="column2"> 
       <!-- com.omniupdate.div label="maincontent3" group="Everyone" button="707" -->

        <li>Emergency </li> 
        <li>After-Hours Care </li> 
        <li>Primary Care </li> 
        <li>Walk-in Clinic </li>

        <li>Allergy Clinic </li>

      <h5>Walk-in Clinic Hours</h5> 
      <p>8 a.m.–4:30 p.m. Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Friday<br />9 a.m.–4:30 p.m. Thursday</p>

      <h4 class="section"><a class="standout2" href="http://counseling.ucr.edu/">Counseling Center</a></h4> 
      <p><img class="img_L" src="/students/images/counseling_thumb.jpg" alt="Counseling Center" width="93" height="46" />The <a href="http://counseling.ucr.edu/">Counseling Center</a> provides counseling services to currently enrolled undergraduate and graduate students, in addition to consultation and programming services to the broader university community. </p> 
      <p>Counselors are available by phone 24 hours, seven-days-a-week, at (951) 827-5531.</p> 
      <h4 class="section"><a class="standout2" href="http://www.recreation.ucr.edu/">Student Recreation Programs</a></h4> 
      <p><img class="img_L" src="/students/images/recreation_thumb.jpg" alt="Student Recreation Program" width="93" height="46" />The UCR Student Recreation Center is home to intramural and club sports, recreational clubs and classes, as well as Outdoor Excursions. It features weight and cardio facilities, racquetball, volleyball and basketball, while the outdoor complex includes tennis courts, a ropes course, a roller hockey rink, two sand volleyball courts and two basketball courts. </p> 
      <p>The facilities are free to UCR students with a current ID. Non-students may purchase quarterly memberships or day passes.</p> 
      <h4 class="section"><a class="standout2" href="http://well.ucr.edu/">The WELL</a></h4> 
      <p><img class="img_L" src="/students/images/well_thumbnail.jpg" alt="The Well" /><a href="http://well.ucr.edu/">The WELL</a> is the center for UCR’s wellness programs that support holistic student health and well-being and is part of the campus and UC system-wide Mental Health/Healthier Campus Climate Initiative. Located in the Highlander Union Building (HUB 248), it features peer educators, mentors and professionals who provide students with resources and support in physical, social, emotional, cultural and spiritual wellness. For more information, call (951) 827-9355 (WELL) .</p>

       <h3>Mission of the UCR Medical School</h3> 
       <p>The mission of the UCR School of Medicine is to improve the health of the people of California and, especially, to serve Inland Southern California by training a diverse workforce of physicians and by developing innovative research and health care delivery programs that will improve the health of the medically underserved in the region and become models to be emulated throughout the state and nation.</p> 
      </div> 
      <div class="l_lower_section"> 
       <h3 class="section">Message to Prospective Students</h3> 
       <p>We are proud to introduce California’s newest medical school, which recently received preliminary accreditation from the Liaison Committee on Medical Education and will enroll its inaugural class of M.D. students in August 2013. Building on a long partnership in medical education with the David Geffen School of Medicine at UCLA, the UCR School of Medicine is committed to expanding and diversifying the physician workforce in Inland Southern California and improving the health of people living in the region.</p> 
       <p>The foundation for this new medical school is an excellent integrated two-year medical curriculum which was developed in concert with UCLA as part of the UCR/UCLA Thomas Haider Program in Biomedical Sciences. This program provided the first two years of M.D. education at UCR for over 30 years; students then completed their M.D. training at UCLA. More than 700 practicing physicians have graduated from this joint program. The new UCR School of Medicine will continue the tradition of providing a unique portal into medical school for UCR students, with up to 24 of the 50 seats filled by students who attend UCR for at least six consecutive quarters and complete their bachelor’s degree at UCR.</p> 
       <p><strong>We invite you to explore our website and learn more about the UCR School of Medicine.</strong></p> 
       <div class="column2"> 
        <p>Neal L. Schiller, Ph.D.<br /> Senior Associate Dean for Student Affairs<br /> Professor of Biomedical Sciences</p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="column2R">

       <h3 class="section">A New Medical School for Inland Southern California</h3> 
       <p><img class="img_L" src="/images/future_students_newmedical.jpg" alt=" " width="199" height="161" />If you aspire to be a patient-centered physician, if your passion is serving those in your community who are less fortunate, the UCR School of Medicine may be the pathway to achieving your dream.</p> 
       <p>The inaugural class of medical students entering in August 2013 will become the sixth medical school in the world renowned University of California. This medical school is distinctive in its focus on serving the needs of the Inland Southern California region —&nbsp;a rapidly growing and ethnically diverse region that is home to 4.2 million people.</p> 
       <p>With an initial class size of 50, UCR medical students will benefit from an intimate educational experience that features:</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li>A organ-based, integrated approach to the science of human biology and disease,</li> 
        <li>Instruction in clinical skills from the very beginning,</li> 
        <li>A distinctive, longitudinal clinical experience that begins in Year 2, and</li> 
        <li>Mentorship from a team of student-centered biomedical science faculty and community-based&nbsp;clinical faculty.</li> 
       </ul> 
       <p>The UCR School of Medicine is now accepting applications for its inaugural class.</p>
