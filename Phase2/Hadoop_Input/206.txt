      <h3>What is College Portrait?</h3> 
      <p>The College Portrait is a nation-wide effort to assist students and parents in comparing and selecting a college. Using a standard layout, the College Portrait enables students to easily compare facts about different colleges. UC Riverside's College Portrait can be viewed online through this Web site or <a title="PDF file" href="/pdf/college_portrait_2011.pdf">downloaded as a PDF</a>. Also included is a <a href="/terms.html">page of terms</a> used in the College Portrait to help you understand the data presented. Review UCR's College Portrait, and compare us to other colleges. We think you will be impressed.</p> 
      <h3>College Portrait answers your questions!</h3> 
      <p>Below are some of the most frequently asked questions:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li><em>How much does it cost to go to UCR?</em><br />Going to UCR is a bargain. To review the costs and financial aid opportunities, visit the <a href="/financial.html">Costs of Attendance and Financial Aid</a> page.</li> 
       <li><em>How does UCR rank compared to other universities?</em><br />UCR is one of the top ranked schools in the nation. Among many other accolades, UC Riverside was ranked 9th among &quot;up and coming&quot; universities by U.S. News and World Report, and 41st&nbsp;among national public universities in U.S. News’ 2011 edition of America’s Best Colleges. &nbsp;<a href="http://www.ucr.edu/about/rankingsandfacts.html">Visit our Rankings and Quick Facts pages </a>to learn more.</li> 
       <li><em>Can I graduate in four years?</em><br />Yes, you can. The majority of UCR's students graduate in four years with the average being four years and one quarter. <a href="/success_progress.html">See UCR's &quot;Time to Degree&quot; statistics</a>.</li> 
       <li><em>How large are your classes?</em><br />At UCR, undergraduates will experience classes ranging from as big as 570 to as small as 3 or 4. Overall, most (70%) classes have enrollments less than 30 students. <a href="/dist_faculty.html#environment">Learn more about class sizes. </a></li> 
       <li><em>What are the most popular majors?</em><br />The most popular major at UCR is business administration, followed by psychology, and biology. <a href="http://my.ucr.edu/academic/Pages/MajorsandPrograms.aspx">See a complete list of majors</a> at MyUCR.</li> 
       <li><em>Isn't UCR a commuter school?</em><br />Some undergraduates choose to commute. However, over 75% of freshmen live on campus. <a href="/student_housing.html">Learn more about student housing opportunities.</a></li> 
      </ul> 
      <h3>Make your own portrait at MyUCR</h3> 
      <p>If you need more information, please visit <a href="http://my.ucr.edu/">MyUCR</a>, the Web site for future UCR students. It&nbsp; provides a rich <a href="http://my.ucr.edu/Explore/Pages/default.aspx">&quot;Explore&quot; </a>section as well as detailed answers to questions about admissions, financial aid, housing, and many other aspects of college life.</p> 
      <p>Make your own portrait as you watch an online video with actual UCR students, explore <a href="http://my.ucr.edu/Explore/Pages/Viewbook.aspx">111 Facts about UCR in our online viewbook</a>, or take a student-led campus tour. The picture you'll get is one you just might want to be a part of.</p>
