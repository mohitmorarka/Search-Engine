UCR uses average student expenses to estimate the cost of attendance for students in various categories. Your actual costs may vary.</p>

             <h3>Learn About Basic Costs</h3> 
             <p>Basic costs include University of California&nbsp;tuition and&nbsp;student sevices&nbsp;fees and campus-based fees for activities and facilities. Students who are not California residents must pay non-resident tuition and fees. The amount of your tuition and fees is based on the following factors: Undergraduate or graduate status, professional student status (i.e., M.B.A. or medical school), and California residency status. Find more info on <a title="Tuition and Fees" href="http://classes.ucr.edu/fees/schedule.htm" target="_blank">tuition and fees</a>. </p> 
             <p>Students enrolled in Summer Session will be charged on a per course basis according to the fee schedules published in the <a title="UCR Summer Session Catalog" href="http://summer.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Summer Session Catalog</a>.</p>
            </div>
            <div id="tab17" class="verticalslider_contents">
             <h3>Live in UCR Housing</h3> 
             <p>UCR offers <a title="On-Campus Housing" href="http://housing.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">on-campus housing</a> in residence halls and campus apartments. Apartments are available for undergraduate and graduate students, and family student housing is available for married students and students with children. Students in the residence halls have a housing contract that includes room and board. Some campus apartments include the cost of utilities in monthly lease rates. For more information on campus housing, contact the Housing Office at (951) 827-6350. </p>
            </div>
            <div id="tab18" class="verticalslider_contents">
             <h3>Pay Your Fees</h3> 
             <p>The Student Business Services Office sends all students a monthly Statement of Account, which includes all fees due and any other outstanding institutional charges. Tuition and fees may be paid by mail or in-person at UCR's Main Cashier's Office, located in the Student Services Building. Tuition and fees must be paid by the <a title="Fee Payment Deadlines" href="http://classes.ucr.edu/enrollment/calendar.htm" target="_blank">fee payment deadlines</a> in order to avoid a $50 late fee and loss of classes for the quarter. </p> 
             <h4>For 2014-15, the deadline to pay fees each quarter is as follows: </h4> 
             <ul> 
              <li>Fall: September 15, 2014 </li> 
              <li>Winter: December 15, 2014</li> 
              <li>Spring: March 13, 2015</li>
             </ul> 
             <p><strong>Other charges</strong> are due within 30 days&nbsp;of the date they appear on the monthly Statement of Account. </p> 
             <p><strong>Institutional charges</strong> may be paid in cash, with a check, by eCheck or by <a title="GROWL" href="https://ucribm.ucr.edu/Paws/Growl.html" target="_blank">credit card via GROWL</a>. American Express, MasterCard, and Discover credit cards are accepted for payment to student accounts via the Web. (Visa cards are not accepted.) A nonrefundable convenience fee is assessed for using this payment method. The Cashiers Office is not able to take any credit card payments at its counter. </p>
            </div>
            <div id="tab19" class="verticalslider_contents">
             <h3>Defer Payments</h3> 
             <p>A Deferred Payment Plan (DPP) is available to students who wish to pay fees and tuition in three monthly installments each quarter. The DPP program may cover the difference between fees and tuition, and any grants, scholarships, and waivers&nbsp;credited towards fees for the quarter. DPP is not available to cover UCR Extension&nbsp;courses. There is an application fee for participation in this program. For more information on the Deferred Payment Program and an application, visit <a title="Student Business Services" href="http://www.sbs.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Student Business Services</a>, part of the Highlander One-Stop Shop (HOSS) located on the first floor of the Student Services Building.</p>
            </div>
            <div id="tab20" class="verticalslider_contents">
             <h3>Set a Budget</h3> 
             <p>One of the important things to learn while you are in school is how to manage your money. Establishing a budget permits you to identify what your monthly or quarterly income will be and what your anticipated expenses are. If your expenses are greater than your resources, you will need to look at options for cutting your costs or supplementing your income. Suggestions may include getting a part-time job during the school year or a summer job, sharing expenses with one or more roommates, finding an apartment with lower rent, conserving on utilities, limiting the amount that you eat out, and spending less on clothes, personal costs, and entertainment. Plan a <a title="Budgeting Worksheet" href="/SiteCollectionDocuments/budgeting_worksheet.pdf" target="_blank">budget</a> to ensure you have enough money to pay for tuition/fees, housing, food and other personal expenses. </p>
            </div>

     <h5> <em>AGSM Reads Across America</em></h5> 
     <p> <img alt="AGSM Read Across America" src="http://soba.ucr.edu/images/news/175.jpg" style="width: 322px; height: 210px; margin: 5px; float: right;" /><em>(February 28, 2014)</em></p> 
     <p class="p1"> On February 28, staff and students from UC Riverside's A. Gary Anderson Graduate School of Management (AGSM) participated in National Education Association's (NEA) <em>Read Across America Day</em>. AGSM traveled to Monroe Elementary School in Riverside, CA to read to children and teach the importance of literacy and language.</p> 
     <p class="p1"> &quot;We're so grateful for such a high turnout! It really means a lot that AGSM was able to bring so many people,&quot; says Morgan Baeza, teacher from Monroe Elementary School. In attendance from AGSM was Irene Song, Rich Reyes, Andrew Monroe, Yi Sun, Wang Yang, Keyang Huang, Connie Kuo, Michelle Hsueh, and Jung Wang. Also joining AGSM was Ramona High School,&nbsp; California Baptist University, and the California Highway Patrol. Of the experience Song says, &quot;It was so much fun to be able to come out here and make a difference to the kids. When you see their eyes light-up, you can tell that they like it too.&quot;</p> 
     <p class="p1"> <em>Read Across America Day</em> is the NEA's signature event. This year's event is the 17th anniversary since the inception of the program and the first-time that AGSM has participated.</p>

    <h3>Common Questions</h3> 
    <p><span style="color: #444444; font-size: 12px; line-height: 18px;">We’ve developed the information below to assist applicants with the most common questions regarding the UCR JOBS application system. Please review the information carefully.</span></p> 
    <p>&nbsp;<span class="wordtotrans">Para ver la traduccion de estas</span> <span class="wordtotrans">preguntas</span> <span class="wordtotrans">en</span> <span class="wordtotrans">espa&ntilde;ol,</span> <span class="wordtotrans">por</span> <span class="wordtotrans">favor</span> <span class="wordtotrans">haga</span> <span class="wordtotrans">clic</span> <span class="wordtotrans"><a href="/helpspanish.html">aqu&iacute;</a>.<br /></span></p> 
    <h4>Do I need to set up a new account if I already had a previous account on the old UCR JOBS site:&nbsp;hr.ucr.edu/jobs?</h4> 
    <p>Yes.&nbsp; A new account will need to be created in order to apply for current positions on the new UCR JOBS website.</p> 
    <h4>What happens to the applications I submitted on the&nbsp;old UCR job site: hr.ucr.edu/jobs?</h4> 
    <p>If you applied to a position in the previous UCR JOBS system, you do not need to reapply in the new system.&nbsp; Your previous application will be considered. However, you will need to create a new account in order to apply for other positions on this website.</p> 
    <h4>Why is there a red &quot;X&quot;&nbsp; in the left-hand navigation when I fill out the application?</h4> 
    <p>All required fields (indicated by red italics) must be completed before the application can be submitted. As each section of the application is completed, the red “X” for that section in the left-hand navigation will become a green check mark.</p> 
    <p>The majority of support inquiries come from users who have not completed one or more required fields. Please confirm that all application sections indicate a green check mark, and review any that contain a red “X” to identify and complete all required fields.</p> 
    <h4>What web browser should I be using?</h4> 
    <p>UCR’s application system supports a variety of browser and operating system combinations. The following browsers and operating systems are supported:</p> 
    <ul> 
     <li>On Windows XP/Vista/7: Internet Explorer 7 (XP only), 8, Firefox 3 &amp; 4</li> 
     <li>On Apple OS X: Firefox 3 &amp; 4</li> 
     <li>On Ubuntu Linux: Firefox 3 &amp; 4</li> 
     <li>Please note that while UCR does not officially support Internet Explorer 9, in general applicants have been able to complete the application using this browser.</li> 
     <li>UCR does not support Google Chrome.</li> 
    </ul> 
    <h4>What document formats do you accept for resumes and cover letters?</h4> 
    <p>Applicants have the ability upload cover letters and/or resumes specifically tailored for individual positions being applied for, and to store these documents for later use in the applicant’s Document Repository. The following document types are allowed for upload:</p> 
    <ul> 
     <li>Microsoft Word 2003/2007/2010 (.DOC/.DOCX)</li> 
     <li>Adobe Acrobat (.PDF)&nbsp; You may download a free copy of <a href="http://get.adobe.com/reader/">Adobe Reader.</a></li> 
     <li>Applicants also have the ability to cut/copy and paste these documents into the application system. Please note, however, that document formatting will not be preserved when the cut/copy &amp; paste method is utilized.</li> 
     <li>UCR JOBS allows you to upload and use multiple resumes and cover letters to complement your employment application. You may access your document library by logging into your account and selecting the blue “Documents” box.</li> 
    </ul> 
    <h4>How do I check the status of my application?</h4> 
    <p>To check the status of positions you have applied to, login to your account and select the green “Apps in Progress” box.</p> 
    <h4>How do I find out about new job opportunities?</h4> 
    <p>Register for an account and join our email list: <a href="http://irecruitportal.ucr.edu/irecruit/!Controller?action=jobs_webui.show_page&amp;page=jobs_register&amp;module=jobs" target="_blank">Receive emailed employment opportunities</a></p> 
    <h4>What if I need an accommodation in application process?</h4> 
    <p>UCR will provide reasonable accommodations for applicants with a disability, as necessary.&nbsp; Please contact the campus Human Resources office of assistance at: applicationhelp@ucr.edu.&nbsp; Please enter the following as the email subject: &quot;Accommodation needed&quot;.</p> 
    <h4>I have a limitation seeing, hearing, speaking or moving.&nbsp; How can I communicate with the Human Resources office?</h4> 
    <p>You may contact the California Telephone Access Program by dialing 711 from any phone in California or by visiting: <a href="http://ddtp.cpuc.ca.gov" target="_blank">http://ddtp.cpuc.ca.gov</a>/.&nbsp; A Communications Assistant can help you contact the Human Resources office at 951.827.JOBS.</p> 
    <h4>How can I find out more about UC, UCR and employment happenings?</h4> 
    <p><a href="/blog/">Read our Blog.</a></p> 
    <h4>What other ways can I keep up to date with UCR Careers?</h4> 
    <p>Follow us on Twitter: <a href="http://twitter.com/#/UCRCareers">@UCRCareers</a></p> 
    <h4>How do I access my UCR JOBS account?</h4> 
    <p>To access your UCR JOBS account, visit: <a href="http://irecruitportal.ucr.edu/irecruit/!Controller?action=jobs_webui.show_page&amp;page=jobs_account_overview&amp;module=jobs">My Account</a></p>
