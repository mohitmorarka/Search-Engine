      <h1>The Permanent Collection</h1> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">Artifacts from UCR/CMP's Permanent Collection</h2> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">California Museum of Photography</h2> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out"></h2> 
      <h3 class="spaced-out"></h3> 
      <br /> 
      <br /> 
      <p>In its diversity, UCR/CMP collection is the largest, most comprehensive photographic center in the West. This resource is utilized as four inter-linked sub collections: the Bingham Technology Collection, University Print Collection, the UCR/CMP Study Center Library Collection, and the Digital Virtual Collection. The museum's collection provides a foundation for exhibitions and programs examining broad associations between photographic works, the instruments of photography, and society's evolving relationship with the photograph. On view are a sampling of these great collections, which give us a glimpse not only photographic methods and artistry but also the full range of practices that contribute to our growing understanding of visual language and image making.</p>

      <p>In collaboration with the colleges and individual faculty, the UWP offers targeted workshops designed to help students address specific assignments.&nbsp; Our most effective offerings are those that faculty members require their students to attend.&nbsp; Our TAs from a range of disciplines consult with the interested faculty member, attend relevant sessions of the course, and design the workshops to be as productive as possible for that particular class.&nbsp; The workshops can be accompanied by tutorials for individual students who need additional instruction.&nbsp;&nbsp; Instruction and sign-ups take place in&nbsp;HMNSS 1103.<br /><br />The overall goal of the workshops is to support UCR faculty who are making their courses more rigorous and rewarding by assigning writing.&nbsp; We believe that good writing assignments, supported by instructional support from the UWP and the faculty member making the assignment,&nbsp; are crucial factors for improving academic engagement.&nbsp; They challenge students to learn while offering assistance that strengthens their capacity to learn.</p>
