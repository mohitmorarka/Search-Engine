					function rgb2hex(hex) {
						if( hex.substr(0, 1) === '#' ) {
							// ie already supplies a rgb value, just return it
							return hex.replace('#', '');
						} else {
							var digits = /(.*?)rgb\((\d+), (\d+), (\d+)\)/.exec(hex);
							var red = parseInt(digits[2]);
							var green = parseInt(digits[3]);
							var blue = parseInt(digits[4]);
							var rgb = blue | (green << 8) | (red << 16);
							return digits[1] + '' + pad(rgb.toString(16),6);
						}
					};

     <p>Computing &amp; Communications (C&amp;C) has deployed a new, easy-to-use tool that allows UCR faculty to create guest wireless accounts on their computer, tablet, or smartphone. UCR faculty can now create up to ten accounts at a time, and the duration of these accounts may be extended on an as needed basis.</p> 
     <p>In addition to the new tool, C&amp;C has also deployed a &quot;wireless conference support&quot; capability that allows individuals to create up to 100 wireless accounts to support larger groups of visitors. Campus Systems Access Administrators (SAAs) can create two wireless administrators per department to support these requests.</p> 
     <p>If your guest comes from an eduroam-enabled campus, your guest may already have access. Please visit the <a href="/eduroam">eduroam</a> site for more information.</p>

Hoddle, Ph.D.</a></h3> <p><em>Biological Control Specialist and Principal Investigator&nbsp;</em></p> <p>Dr. Hoddle has headed the research in this laboratory since 1997 and is primarily involved in the identification of pest problems wf biological control could be a successful approach. The location, release and evaluation of natural enemy impacts on population growth features strongly in his research. The evaluation of biological control agents are conducted primarily in the field and, when necessary, aspects of both pest and natural enemy biology and behavior are studied in the laboratory.</p> </td>

Irvin, Ph.D.</a></h3> <p><em>Biological Control Specialist and Research Scholar</em></p> <p>Dr. Irvin joined the Hoddle laboratory in 2001 as a postdoctoral scholar. She has a heavy focus on researching the glassy-winged sharpshooter, <em>Homalodisca vitripennis</em> (Germar), and its mymarid wasp biological control agents <em>Gonatocerus ashmeadi</em>, <em>G. fasciatus</em>, <em>G. triguttatus</em> and <em>G. tuberculifemur</em>.&nbsp;Dr. Irvin has since been promoted to Assistant Specialist and in 2007 was awarded a Western Region Sustainable Agriculture Research and Education (Western SARE) grant to investigate the use of nectar cover crops for sustainable pest control in California vineyards.&nbsp;</p> </td>

     <h3>About the Eaton Collection of Science Fiction &amp; Fantasy</h3> 
     <p>The Eaton Collection is the largest publicly-accessible collection of science fiction, fantasy, horror and utopian literature in the world. It consists of hardback and paperback books, pulp magazines, fanzines, film and visual material, comic books, and ephemera. It is housed in the <strong>UCR Libraries' Special Collections &amp; Archives</strong>, located on the fourth floor of the Tom&aacute;s Rivera Library.</p> 
     <div class="l_black_box"> 
      <h4>History of the Collection</h4> 
      <p>The Eaton Collection originated with the personal library of <a href="/jlloydeaton.html"><strong>Dr. J. Lloyd Eaton</strong></a>, an Oakland, California physician and book collector. Eaton's collection consisted of about 7,500 hardback editions of science fiction, fantasy and horror from the Nineteenth to the mid-Twentieth centuries. The collection was acquired in 1969 with the intention of creating an outstanding resource for the research and study of science fiction and fantasy.</p> 
      <p>Today the collection has grown to over 300,000 items and is a major resource for the study of speculative fiction or <em>&quot;</em>SF.&quot; It is visited by scholars from around the world both for its American and international holdings, from&nbsp; the 1517 edition of <strong>Thomas More</strong>'s Utopia to the most recent sf publications. Among its archival collections are the papers of <strong>Richard Adams</strong>, <strong>Gregory Benford</strong>, <strong>David Brin</strong>, <strong>Michael Cassutt</strong>, <strong>Robert L. Forward</strong>, and <strong>Anne McCaffrey</strong>,&nbsp;just to name a few.</p> 
      <div class="l_overflow_block"> 
       <h4 style="text-transform: none !important;">The Eaton Conference</h4> 
       <p>From the outset, the intention of UCR Libraries was to make the Eaton Collection an outstanding resource for research and study. As part of its scholarly mission, the Eaton Collection began hosting an <strong>Eaton Conference</strong> in 1979.</p> 
       <p>Over the years, the Eaton Conference has attracted a number of famous writers, including <strong>Brian Aldiss</strong>, <strong>Gregory Benford</strong>, <strong>Ray Bradbury, David Brin, Samuel R. Delany</strong>, <strong>Harlan Ellison</strong>, <strong>Frederik Pohl, Kim Stanley Robinson, Robert Silverberg</strong>, <strong>Theodore Sturgeon</strong> and <strong>Roger Zelazny</strong>. It has attracted an equally distinguished group of critics and scientists including <strong>Harold Bloom</strong>, <strong>Leslie Fiedler</strong>, <strong>Fredric Jameson</strong>, <strong>Harry Levin</strong>, <strong>Marvin Minsky</strong> and <strong>Robert Scholes</strong>.</p> 
       <p>The conference has produced more than 20 volumes of critical essays, published by various university presses.</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li><a href="http://eatonconference.ucr.edu/">To learn more about the Eaton Conference, please visit our conference website. </a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <p>The collection is also a compendium of five centuries of publishing history. The history of decades of science fiction illustration can also be researched from the covers of pulp paperbacks and dust jackets of hard cover books.</p> 
      <p>From 1979 to 2004, <strong><a class="external" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Edgar_Slusser">George Slusser</a></strong> held a joint position as Eaton curator and professor of comparative literature. During his tenure the collection grew in printed titles and other materials, including the acquisition of the <a href="/fanzines.html">major fanzine collections</a>.</p> 
      <p>A prolific scholar in the field, Slusser taught the first courses in science fiction studies at UCR and originated the Eaton Conference, which he chaired for more than 20 years. <a href="http://www.fiatlux.ucr.edu/cgi-bin/display.cgi?id=422">It was Slusser's dream for UCR to become a center of science fiction studies, with the collection he helped build as the nucleus.</a> Establishing UCR as a center for science fiction studies is still part of the long-range vision at UCR.</p> 
      <p>Courses in science fiction studies are now taught by leading SF scholar <a href="http://facultydirectory.ucr.edu/cgi-bin/pub/public_individual.pl?faculty=3489"><strong>Rob Latham</strong></a> and SF author Nalo Hopkinson. Latham also serves as <em>ex officio</em> liaison to the Eaton Collection and co-chair of the biennial Eaton Conference with <strong>Melissa Conway</strong>, the UCR Libraries' Head of Special Collections &amp; Archives.</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li><a class="external" href="http://articles.latimes.com/print/2007/jun/21/local/me-scifi21">Read a profile of Slusser and the Eaton Collection from the June 21, 2007 <em>Los Angeles Times</em>.</a></li> 
       <li><a class="external" href="http://www.celebratingresearch.org/libraries/riverside/eaton.shtml">Read a profile of the Eaton Collection and its history from <em>Celebrating Research</em></a>.</li> 
      </ul>
