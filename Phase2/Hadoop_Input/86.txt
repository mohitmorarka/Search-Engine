      <div id="omnicontent"> 
       <p>The University of California founded the Citrus Research Center and <a href="http://www.ucr.edu/research/centers/aes.html" title="Agricultural Experiment Station">Agricultural Experiment Station (CRC-AES)</a> in Riverside, CA in 1907. The CRC-AES is world renowned for research on citrus including the development of citrus varieties and rootstocks, as well as disease and insect management, postharvest handling methods, and cultural practices for improved citrus production. The <a href="http://www.citrusvariety.ucr.edu/" title="Citrus Variety Collection">Citrus Variety Collection</a> is the world's largest collection of citrus and citrus relatives and is part of CRC-AES. Over the years, the CRC-AES has expanded its scope and encompasses research from applied to basic sciences on a full range of perennial tree and vine crops, annual field and vegetable crops, turf, ornamental and landscape plants.</p> 
       <p><strong>Mission of Agricultural Operations:</strong><em> To operate UCR experiment stations in the support of field research and greenhouses for the faculty and staff of the <a href="http://cnas.ucr.edu" title="College of Natural and Agricultural Sciences">College of Natural and Agricultural Sciences</a> and the <a href="http://www.ucr.edu/research/centers/aes.html" title="Agricultural Experiment Station">Agricultural Experiment Station</a>. Within that mission, the Department provides and maintains research facilities, provides appropriate services, performs field-plot care, and acts as custodian of the land.</em></p> 
       <p>The Citrus Experiment station is managed by the Department of Agricultural Operations and is known as &quot;Ag Ops&quot;. Ag Ops is a support Department of the <a href="http://cnas.ucr.edu" title="College of Natural and Agricultural Sciences">College of Natural and Agricultural Sciences (CNAS)</a>. Ag Ops serves as a point of integration for applied and basic research under field, greenhouse, and screenhouse conditions and offers the opportunity to collaborate across disciplines. Essentially, it is the field laboratory of CNAS. We host research projects addressing issues in agriculture, biotechnology, horticulture, plant pathology, entomology, nematology, food systems, natural resources, environmental sciences and social sciences. We have federally funded IR-4 research on agricultural pesticides as well as house the USDA Germplasm Repository for Citrus and Dates. There are active programs involving community participants, research faculty from other UC campuses, county-based <a href="http://ucanr.org/" title="UC Cooperative Extension">UC Cooperative Extension Advisors</a>, as well as collaborations with other institutions outside of California. On average, there are over 100 faculty served by Ag Ops.</p> 
       <p>Ag Ops is the 'showcase' into the CNAS and campus research in agriculture and environmental sciences, with numerous national and international visitors, tours and field days conducted, thus having a strong outreach value for stakeholders in applied research, but also to the broader local community and for area schools and colleges (K-12 etc.). R'Garden, community garden and the future outreach center and outdoor classroom, invites students, faculty, youth and community partners to engage in sciences, welcoming them into a dynamic learning environment and campus.</p> 
       <p>Ag Ops manages and operates greenhouses, screenhouses, and two field stations. The 480 acres <a href="../crcaes/" target="_top">CRC-AES</a> is located <a href="http://campusmap.ucr.edu/campusMap.php?loc=AGOP" title="Campus Map">adjacent to campus</a> and the 540 acres <a href="../cvars/" target="_top">Coachella Valley Agricultural Research Station (CVARS)</a> is located 90 miles southeast of campus. The two stations represent different agro-ecologies representative of the area: CRC-AES in traditional citrus/subtropicals production and CVARS representative of desert agricultural production systems. Ag Ops is responsible for establishing and maintaining allocated research plot areas and also the land not in use for UCR academic departments and researchers.The research conducted at the stations serves as the basis for new, improved plant varieties for agricultural and urban landscapes, as well as new, more sustainable agricultural practices to enhance crop productivity. Over 50 different crops are grown annually for research; among the commodities under study are citrus, turfgrass, grapes, avocados, asparagus, date palms, vegetables, corn, alfalfa, cowpeas, small grains, ornamentals, and cover crops. Additionally, specialty crops, native plants, and weeds are often grown to meet the needs of specific projects. The facilities are poised for future growth through the integration of research and extension in agricultural, environmental sciences, social science and medical sciences.</p> 
       <p>Ag Ops also is a vital in extension activities for the campus and the Riverside community. The farm is vital to collaborations in outreach through <a href="http://ucanr.org/" title="UC Cooperative Extension">Cooperative Extension</a> emphasizing the Riverside community, southern California and drawing clientele from throughout the state. We are large part of the continuum from basic and applied research, extending information throughout the world.</p> 
       <p>Hours open: 8:00 am to noon and 1:00 pm to 5:00 pm, Monday through Friday<br />Closed on <a href="../holidays/">campus holidays</a>.</p>

      <div class="span4 hero-unit"> 
       <p>We support the recruitment, retention and advancement of a diverse, world-class faculty across UC.</p> 
      </div> 
      <div class="span4">
       <p>Academic Personnel develops programs, initiatives and policies to support faculty recruitment, retention and advancement. We have universitywide responsibility for academic appointments and compensation and serve as steward for the university’s Academic Personnel policies covering all academic appointments: faculty, research and health science clinical faculty, lecturers, librarians, graduate students and postdoctoral scholars. We oversee systemwide faculty diversity programs and academic affirmative action.&nbsp;<a href="about-us.html">View our mission</a></p>

      <h3>Understanding Refunds</h3> 
      <p>If you cancel your enrollment or withdraw from UCR, you may be eligible for tuition and fee refunds. If you withdraw from <em>some</em> of your classes, you are not eligible for a refund. The exception to this is if you are enrolled in a graduate self-supporting program charging per unit or per course fees. Use the tables below to help determine your anticipated refund. </p> 
      <span class="warning">Important:</span> 
      <ul class="orange-bullets"> 
       <li>Financial aid plays a complex role in refunds. <a href="http://financialaid.ucr.edu/contact/Pages/default.aspx" target="_blank">Your financial aid counselor</a> can best help you understand your particular refund situation</li> 
       <li><a href="/registrar/academic-calendar/default.aspx">See the calendar</a> for the first official day of instruction</li> 
       <li><a href="/registrar/registering-for-classes/cancellation-withdrawal-absence-readmission.aspx">Learn more about the withdrawal process</a>.</li> 
       <li>Past refund tables may be found in the <a href="/registrar/archives/default.aspx">Archives section.</a></li> 
      </ul>

      <h3>Non-Refundable Fees</h3> 
      <p> Most fees are eligible for refund, including <a href="/registrar/tuition-and-fees/quarterly-fees.aspx">tuition and campus-based fees</a>. There are a few fees that become <strong>nonrefundable</strong> after a certain amount of time passes (unless special requirements are met). These include:</p> 
      <div class="accordion"> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Course Materials and Services Fees</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p> Because academic departments must purchase supplies, materials and services prior to instruction, <a href="/registrar/tuition-and-fees/course-materials-and-services-fee.aspx">course materials and services fees</a> are nonrefundable after the first day of instruction. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Health Insurance Premium</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p> For all students, the health insurance premium is nonrefundable after the first day of instruction, unless the student enters the Armed Forces and requests a prorated refund from the <a href="http://campushealth.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx" target="_blank">Campus Health Center.</a> </p> 
        <p>Contact Student Health Insurance at (951) 827-5683 for details about insurance benefits after the date of withdrawal. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Student Technology Course Materials Fee</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p> The Student Technology Course Materials Fee is nonrefundable after Friday of the third week of instruction.</p> 
       </div>
