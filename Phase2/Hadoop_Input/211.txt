      <h3>Anti-Virus and Anti-Spyware @ UCR</h3> 
      <p>UCR provides <a href="/security/sophos.html">anti-virus software</a> from Sophos, which also now includes anti-spyware protection.&nbsp; C&amp;C suggests using an additional anti-spyware package in conjunction with Sophos and recommends Windows Security Essentials from Microsoft. The campus has purchased a site license for Sophos and it is available at no cost to all staff, faculty and students.&nbsp; There are no license fees for Windows Security Essentials as it is part of the Windows 7 and 8 operating system.</p> 
      <p>Windows XP users will need to <a href="http://www.microsoft.com/security/pc-security/malware-removal.aspx" target="_blank">install Windows Malicious Software Removal Tool</a>; however, Windows Vista and Windows 7 operating systems have Microsoft Security Essentials pre-installed under the Windows Action Center.</p> 
      <p>If you encounter any difficulties downloading or utilizing Sophos or Windows Security, please contact the Computing Help Desk (<a href="mailto:helpdesk@ucr.edu">helpdesk@ucr.edu</a> or dial x23555).</p>

      <h3>What is the Academic Senate?</h3> 
      <p>The Academic Senate is the voice of the faculty in the University of California. It represents the Faculty in the &quot;shared governance&quot; of UC. This responsibility is delegated by the Regents and shared with the University administration, both at the campus level and systemwide. The University of California Academic Senate is one of the most highly developed and influential faculty governments in any university. It is the one organization through which the faculty, as a whole or on any of the campuses, can express its views on an issue.&nbsp; The senate guides the university’s educational course and is responsible for:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>Determining academic policy.</li> 
       <li>Setting conditions for admission and granting of degrees.</li> 
       <li>Authorizing and supervising curricula and courses.</li> 
       <li>Advising on faculty appointments, promotions and budgets.</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p>The senate works in concert with the university administration, which is responsible for the finances and organization of the institution. These roles overlap and are frequently interdependent.</p> 
      <p><a href="/about/officers.html">Meet the officers of the Academic Senate </a></p> 
      <p><a href="/about/staff.html">Meet the staff of the Academic Senate. </a></p> 
      <h3>How the Senate Works</h3> 
      <p>The Academic Senate was established upon the creation of the university to create a model where faculty were both managers and teachers within the institution. The regents have reaffirmed and strengthened the decisive role of the senate in both the daily management and long-term planning of the institution. The senate has a dual role of authority in the educational aspects of the university and advice to the administrators in the overall operation of the university.</p> 
      <p><a href="/about/duties_structure.html">Read about the Duties and Structure of the Academic Senate </a></p> 
      <p>The organization, duties, powers and privileges of the Academic Senate are detailed in Regents Standing Order 105. <a href="/about/organization_powers.html"> </a></p> 
      <p><a href="/about/organization_powers.html">Read the full text of the standing order.</a></p>

     <h3><img class="img_L" title="Christina Hoddle" src="/images/christina_hoddle_ucr.jpg" alt="Christina Hoddle" width="150" height="182" />Christina Hoddle<br /><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;">Assistant Specialist<br /></span><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;">Phone: (951) 827-4360<br /></span><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;">Fax: (951) 827-3086<br /></span><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: x-small;"><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;"><a href="mailto:christina.hoddle@ucr.edu">christina.hoddle@ucr.edu</a></span></span></h3> 
     <p>Christina has collaborated with the Hoddle laboratory since 2004. Christina has dedicated her research efforts towards biological control and invasive species with an emphasis on crop pests. She has been involved in a variety of international projects sponsored by various granting agencies, private institutions, and commodity boards. An example of these finished and on-going projects include: hunting for bean thrips in Australia (Funded: Citrus Research Board-CRB), identification and production of pheromone for avocado seed moth (<em>Stenoma catenifer</em>) in Guatemala and Peru (Funded: CA Avocado Commission), searching for Asian citrus psyllid parasitoids in Pakistan (Funded: CDFA/CRB), monitoring <em>Rodolia cardinalis</em> populations on the Galapagos Islands and assessing the impact of this biocontrol agent on the cottony cushion scale, <em>Icerya purchasi</em> (Funded: Various Donors), red palm weevil (<em>Rhynchophorus ferrugineus</em> and <em>R. vulneratus</em>) flight activity in Saudi Arabia (Funded: King Faisal University) and genetic analyses throughout southeast Asia (Funded: CDFA).</p> 
     <p>Since December 2011, she has been in charge of releasing and monitoring <em>Tamarixia radiata</em>, a biological control agent of Asian citrus psyllid (<em>Diaphorina citri</em>) in urban areas of Southern California. This ongoing project has proven to be extremely successful and parasites have been detected 5-7 miles beyond their release points. These efforts will continue through 2013 with funding from the CDFA and the Citrus Research Board.</p>
