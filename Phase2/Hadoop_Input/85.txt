           Where will I live?
          </blockquote>

          <blockquote>
           Can I afford it?
          </blockquote> 
          <div> 
           <p>You can afford UCR! Not only are we a <a href="http://colleges.usnews.rankingsandreviews.com/best-colleges/rankings/national-universities/best-value">Top 25 Best Value School, </a>we rank higher than <em>any other UC campus</em>. Eighty-three percent of our undergraduate students receive financial aid. Of those students, 70% have <em>all of their system-wide fees</em> covered with scholarships and grants. </p> 
          </div> 
          <p class="readMore clearfix"> <a href="/PayingForCollege/costEstimator">See How Affordable it is to Attend UC Riverside</a> <a href="/PayingForCollege/financialAidScholarships">Learn More About Scholarships, Grants and Loans</a> </p>

          <blockquote>
           Will I get accepted? 
          </blockquote> 
          <div> 
           <p>UC Riverside seeks extraordinary students and admission is competitive. We consider your academic record, test scores and the challenge of your coursework. We give special consideration to first-generation college students and those from low-income families. We also offer programs that guarantee your admission. </p>

       <h1 class="page-header">Academic Affairs</h1> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <!-- Example row of columns --> 
     <div class="row-fluid"> 
      <div class="span4 hero-unit"> 
       <p>We are a systemwide resource for academic excellence in research, teaching and public service.</p> 
      </div> 
      <div class="span4">
       <p>The Office of the Provost builds on the energy and academic strength of UC’s campuses to enhance the system’s educational impact. We help recruit and retain outstanding professors and provide them with the support they need to succeed. We promote excellence through programs that help prepare, recruit and support undergraduate, graduate and professional students. We manage programs that help position UC and California as engines of economic, social and cultural development. And we ensure that UC remains on the cutting edge of innovation in teaching and learning by supporting new academic initiatives.</p>

      <h3>The Part-Time Fee Waiver<br /> </h3> 
     </div> 
     <div class="span-11"> 
      <p>If you attend UCR part-time, you may be entitled to reduced tuition. To qualify, undergraduates must meet certain requirements and <a href="http://student08.ucr.edu/Em/myforms/Forms.aspx" target="_blank">submit an Undergraduate Petition for Unit/Fee Reduction</a>. For graduate students, the process is slightly different. Contact the <a href="http://graduate.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Graduate Division</a> for <a href="http://graduate.ucr.edu/pub_forms.html" target="_blank">forms</a> and details. </p> 
      <p> If approved, you will pay one-half of your applicable tuition. If you qualify for nonresident status, you will also pay one-half of your nonresident supplemental tuition fee. You will still need to pay all campus-based fees (including the Student Services Fee) in full. </p> 
      <div class="accordion"> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Who can apply for a part-time fee waiver?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <ul class="orange-bullets wide padLeft40"> 
         <li>Undergraduate students with a dean-approved course load of 10 units or fewer may apply.</li> 
         <li>Graduate students with six units or fewer may apply.</li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> When does a part-time fee waiver have to be submitted?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <ul class="orange-bullets wide padLeft40"> 
         <li><strong>Each quarter</strong> you intend to enroll in 10 units or fewer.</li> 
         <li>Before <strong>NOON</strong> on the Friday of the third week of instruction. <a href="/registrar/academic-calendar/default.aspx">See the calendar</a> for details. </li> 
         <li>Please note: we do not process waivers until your enrollment is reduced to 10 units or fewer. </li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Can international students apply for part-time fee waiver? </h4> 
       <div> 
        <ul class="orange-bullets wide padLeft40"> 
         <li>Federal regulations require international students with F-1 or J-1 immigration status to maintain a full course load of 12 units each quarter.</li> 
         <li>Dropping below 12 units without consulting an international student advisor at the <a href="http://internationalcenter.ucr.edu" target="_blank">International Student Resource Center (ISRC)</a> jeopardizes legal status.</li> 
         <li>Federal regulations allow for a drop below 12 units in very limited circumstances. If you are an international student considering part-time status, it is <strong>essential</strong> that you talk to an advisor at the ISRC before dropping classes or filing a part-time waiver.</li> 
        </ul> 
        <p>&nbsp;</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Does a part-time fee waiver impact financial aid? </h4> 
       <div> 
        <ul class="orange-bullets wide padLeft40"> 
         <li>Your award may be reduced if you receive a part-time fee reduction. You could also face a unit deficit affecting future financial aid eligibility.</li> 
         <li><strong>We strongly recommend</strong> consulting <a href="http://financialaid.ucr.edu/contact/Pages/default.aspx" target="_blank">your financial aid counselor</a> before reducing your course load in order to determine the impact part-time status could have on your aid.</li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Are employees eligible for a part-time fee waiver?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>No, employees who are utilizing the Employee Reduced Fee Enrollment Policy are not eligible for the part-time fee waiver. Please visit <a href="http://humanresources.ucr.edu/education/benefits.html" target="_blank">Human Resources</a> for more information on reduced-fee eligibility for employees. </p> 
       </div>
