      <p>The Academic Senate and its Committee on Research, administer three grant programs for UCR Faculty members and researchers.</p> 
      <p>The Committee on Research is made up of at least seven members of the Senate, and is responsible for reviewing applications from eligible faculty members for support from intramural research funds, research travel funds or other research monies. Learn more about the <a href="/committee/index.html?do=info&amp;id=20">Committee on Research</a>.</p> 
      <h3>Academic Senate Grants</h3> 
      <h4 class="section">Omnibus Funds</h4> 
      <p>Academic Senate research funds can be used for a variety of reasons, including:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>Travel to scholarly meetings of learned societies (TSM)</li> 
       <li>Research and Travel Award (RTA)</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p class="indent"><a href="/research_grants/omnibus_guidelines.html">Application Guidelines for 2013-14</a></p>

      <p class="indent"><a href="/research_grants/omnibus_review.html">Grant Review Process</a> (Committee on Research members only)</p> 
      <h4 class="section">Regents Faculty Fellowships and Faculty Development Awards</h4> 
      <p>Regents Faculty Fellowships (RFF) afford selected junior faculty with an opportunity for educational enrichment through significant research, advanced or independent study, or improvement of teaching effectiveness.</p> 
      <p>Faculty Development Awards (FDA) are for deserving assistant professors who are approaching tenure to assist in their professional development.</p> 
      <p class="indent"><a href="/research_grants/rfffd_recipients_all.html">Previous Year Recipients </a></p>

      <h4 class="section">Committee on Research (COR) Fellowships</h4> 
      <p>Committee on Research (COR) Fellowships assist faculty to develop new major research projects that can be funded extramurally. Recipients must submit an extramural grant application within two years of receiving a COR&nbsp; Fellowship.</p>
