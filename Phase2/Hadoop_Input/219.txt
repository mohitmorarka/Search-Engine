      <h3>Multimedia Equipment</h3> 
      <p>Multimedia Technologies delivers equipment to the campus and vicinity. Smart Classroom equipment can be rented for non-academic events. Equipment is available to departments and registered campus organizations. <a href="/multimedia/rates.html">Multimedia Equipment &amp; Rates</a><br /><br />Our technicians will deliver, set up, and instruct you in the operation of our equipment. Some events require a technician to supervise the operation of the equipment.</p> 
      <h3>Ordering Equipment<br /><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; font-weight: bold; line-height: 18px;"><br />Campus Departments<br /></span><span style="color: #444444; font-size: 12px; line-height: 18px;">Work orders can be submitted via <a href="/media2010">Media2010</a>. Transactor Access is needed to enter and edit requests in Media2010. This access level is authorized by your departmental Systems Access Administrator (SAA) using the Enterprise Access Control System (EACS).<br /><br /></span><span style="color: #444444; font-size: 12px; line-height: 18px;">There are no equipment charges for Academically Scheduled Courses.&nbsp;<br /></span><span style="color: #444444; font-size: 12px; line-height: 18px;">If you cannot gain access to Media2010, please complete and fax a <a href="/multimedia/files/medform2010.pdf">Multimedia Services Form</a> to x27282.&nbsp;</span></h3> 
      <h5>Student Groups &amp; Campus Organizations<br /><span style="color: #444444; font-size: 12px; font-weight: normal;">Work orders can be submitted in person at the Multimedia Front Office (Sproul Hall Basement), Monday through Friday, 8AM - 5PM. Payment must be made prior to the event, in the form of check or money order.</span></h5> 
      <h3> Cancellation and Late Fees</h3> You may cancel an equipment or service request prior to the setup of your event. Please call 827-3045 to cancel equipment. Academic orders cancelled upon delivery incur a $10.00 cancellation fee. 
      <p>Orders placed less than 3 business days before the event incur a $25.00 late fee.&nbsp;</p>

     <p>Dr. Hoddle has headed the research in this laboratory since 1997 and is primarily involved in the identification of pest problems where biological control could be a successful approach. The location, release and evaluation of natural enemy impacts on population growth features strongly in his research. The evaluation of biological control agents are conducted primarily in the field and, when necessary, aspects of both pest and natural enemy biology and behavior are studied in the laboratory. &nbsp;He is also the director of the <a href="http://cisr.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Center for Invasive Species Research</a>.</p>

     <h3>Research Interests</h3> 
     <p>Biological control is the intentional use of host specific natural enemies (predators, parasitoids, and pathogens) by humans to suppress population growth of noxious plants and animals to levels which are no longer damaging. Many of our current agricultural pest problems are amenable to biological control, and when successful, natural enemies provide enduring, environmentally benign, pest control. Biological control is also being used in conservation efforts to restore natural areas invaded by exotic organisms, especially weeds. The emphasis of my work is to identify pest problems where biological control could be successful, locate and release natural enemies, and then evaluate natural enemy impact on pest population growth. Evaluations of biological control agents are conducted primarily in the field, and when necessary, aspects of both pest and natural enemy biology and behavior are studied in the laboratory. I am particularly interested in determining; 1) the magnitude of reduction in pest population growth caused by natural enemies, 2) the mechanisms by which pests and natural enemies co-exist at low densities, 3) the number of natural enemy species that are needed to give control, 4) inter-specific competition between natural enemies which utilize the same host, and 5) the economics of biological control when compared to pesticides.</p> 
     <br /> 
     <p><a href="/hoddle/harrysmithfund.html"><span style="color: #2668b2; font-size: 18px; line-height: 24px; font-weight: 300;"><img class="img_R" title="Harry Scott Smith" src="/images/harrysmith_thumb.jpg" alt="Harry Scott Smith" width="100" height="163" /></span></a><span style="color: #2668b2; font-size: 18px; line-height: 24px; font-weight: 300;">The Harry Scott Smith Scholarship for Graduate Biological Control Students</span></p>

      <h3>A Leading Southern California MBA Program</h3> 
      <p>The motto of the MBA program at the A. Gary Anderson Graduate School of Management (AGSM) is &quot;Leading Thinkers, Leading Growth,&quot; a reflection of our mission to produce leaders whose ability to think about the challenges they face will provide them with skills to lead growth in both our region and the world.&nbsp;Accredited by the&nbsp;<a href="http://www.aacsb.edu/">Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB)</a>, and with fellowships and other financial assistance packages available on a competitive basis,&nbsp;there are many great reasons to earn your MBA at UCR.</p> 
      <h4>University of California Excellence</h4> 
      <p>Graduating from the MBA program at AGSM provides students with a world-recognized University of California degree. UCR is ranked among the most diverse campuses in the nation with a long tradition of welcoming and supporting international scholars.</p> 
      <h4>World-Class Faculty</h4> 
      <p>The faculty at AGSM features preeminent scholars and practitioners in their fields. Our distinguished faculty engages and encourages students through their own academic success and research.&nbsp;Educated at leading universities and serving on the editorial boards of leading journals in business administration, our faculty are also recognized experts in the fields of intellectual property management, e-commerce and marketing, entrepreneurship, finance, accounting and supply chain management.</p> 
      <h4>The Right Location: Southern California</h4> 
      <p>Our MBA program is situated in the living laboratory of Inland Southern California, one of the fastest growing and changing regions in the world. This environment is an ideal location where students and faculty can perform cutting-edge research and experience first-hand how business works. For example, our Sloan Center for Internet Retailing is the world's leading university research center devoted to the study of e-commerce marketing.</p>

         <h4>UCPath Center - Offering Exciting&nbsp;Opportunities</h4> 
         <p>The University of California has launched the UCPath Initiative to <br />create cost savings and administrative efficiencies by providing one <br />single, university-wide system.&nbsp; As part of this initiative, a new <br />UC-wide shared services center (UCPath Center) is being designed that <br />will process routine transactions related to payroll, benefits, leave <br />management and workforce administration. Located in Riverside, <br />California, the UCPath center will provide services across the <br />University of California's 10 campuses and 5 medical centers.&nbsp;</p> 
         <p>UC employees are encouraged to explore newly opened career opportunities at the UCPath Center:&nbsp;</p> 
         <ul> 
          <li><a href="https://www.candidatecare.com/srccsh/RTi.home?r=2000017267010&amp;d=ucop.candidatecare.com">UCPath Center - Senior HRIS Support Analyst</a></li> 
         </ul> 
         <p>The University is committed to supporting the UC Community by giving priority consideration to current UC employees seeking employment opportunities at the center.&nbsp; Applications are being accepted at the <a href="http://jobs.universityofcalifornia.edu/?action=ucpsearch">university systemwide job board</a>.</p> 
         <p>Visit the&nbsp;<a href="http://ucpath.universityofcalifornia.edu/">UCPath website</a>&nbsp;to learn more about UCPath and UCPath Center opportunities.&nbsp; Questions about UCPath Center jobs and recruiting can be sent to <a href="mailto:UCPathJobs@universityofcalifornia.edu">UCPathJobs@universityofcalifornia.edu</a>.</p> 
         <p>&nbsp;</p> 
         <h3><span style="color: #003366;">Campus Security and Crime Report</span></h3> 
         <p>The &quot;Jeanne CLERY Disclosure of Campus Security Policy and Campus Crime Statistics Act of 1998,&quot; commonly referred to as the &quot;CLERY Act,&quot; requires institutions of higher education receiving federal financial aid to report specified crime statistics on college campuses and to provide other safety and crime information to members of the campus community.&nbsp; The report is available on the UCR PD website at <a href="http://police.ucr.edu/clery/clery.html">http://police.ucr.edu/clery/clery.html</a>.&nbsp; If you would like a printout containing this information, you can contact a representative of the UCR Police Department at 3500 Canyon Crest Drive, Riverside, CA&nbsp; 92521 or by phone at (951) 827-5222.</p>
