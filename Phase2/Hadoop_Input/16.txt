     <h3>The Best Place to Work in Inland Southern California</h3> 
     <p>UCR staff members are a part of a unique and supportive community that supports the university's mission of empowering future generations by offering&nbsp; our talented students the support, resources and inspiration to explore and contribute. </p> 
     <p>But what makes UC Riverside a great place to work? Is it our location close to the mountains, the desert and the beach?&nbsp; The outstanding benefits? The vibrant campus atmosphere? Being a part of a university growing in national prominence? Working in a community of accessible excellence?&nbsp;</p> 
     <p> It's all of these... and more.&nbsp;</p> 
     <div class="nota-bene"> 
      <h4><img id="bang2" class="img_R_borderless" src="/staff/images/red_bang.png" alt="!" width="76" height="76" />Ready to Apply?</h4> 
      <p>To apply for a job at UC Riverside, visit the <a href="http://hr.ucr.edu/">Office of Human Resources website</a>.</p> 
      <ul class="unIndentedList"> 
       <li><a class="standout2" href="http://hr.ucr.edu/jobs/JobsBrowse.aspx?@strPositionListBy=Summary">Search the available positions</a></li> 
       <li><a class="standout2" href="http://hr.ucr.edu/jobs/UserProfileInsertEmail.aspx">Create a profile</a></li> 
       <li><a class="standout2" href="http://hr.ucr.edu/jobs/DocFAQ.aspx">Read our frequently asked questions</a></li> 
       <li><a class="standout2" href="http://hr.ucr.edu/jobs/DocDivCompBenefits.aspx">Learn about the benefits afforded to UCR employees</a></li> 
      </ul> 
     </div> 
     <ul class="striped big_links"> 
      <li><a href="/about/impacts.html"> <strong>Making a Difference in our Region</strong> UCR's economic impact on the state of California exceeds $1 billion. Spending by the university, its students, and visitors helped generate more than 11,000 full-time jobs statewide. Locally, UCR supports the local economy via its more than 1,000 faculty members, 5,500 staff members and 20,000 students.</a></li> 
      <li><a href="/about/region.html"> <strong>Riverside: A Great Place to Work and Live</strong>Riverside is an exciting and vibrant community. The City of Riverside has won numerous other awards for excellence in city governance and public services, including recognition of efforts to create innovative broadband applications, creating green, sustainable communities. Learn more about the region we call home.</a></li> 
      <li><a href="http://diversity.ucr.edu/"><strong>Embracing Diversity</strong> UC Riverside's multicultural, ethnically diverse, and socially safe environment provides our students, staff and faculty with a successful and positive educational climate . Learn more about the culture of diversity at UCR and the programs that forge its foundation.</a></li> 
      <li><a href="http://staffassembly.ucr.edu/"><strong>The Voice of the UCR's Staff</strong> The UCR Staff Assembly promotes the interests and well-being of all staff career employees. Overseen by an executive board, the Staff Assembly works in conjunction with the Office of the Chancellor to support the university's mission, sponsors social events and outings and supports activities that enrich the personal and professional lives of UCR staff.</a></li> 
      <li><a href="http://cdc.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx"><strong>Childcare at the UCR Child Development Center</strong> UCR faculty members are eligible to enroll their children in the UCR Child Development Center. The curriculum at the center follows guidelines set by the California Department of Education and the National Association for the Education of Young Children. All of the centers programs are accredited by the National Academy of Early Childhood Programs.</a></li> 
     </ul>

     <h3>The Best Place to Work in Inland Southern California</h3> 
     <p>UCR staff members are a part of a unique and supportive community that supports the university's mission of empowering future generations by offering&nbsp; our talented students the support, resources and inspiration to explore and contribute. </p> 
     <p>But what makes UC Riverside a great place to work? Is it our location close to the mountains, the desert and the beach?&nbsp; The outstanding benefits? The vibrant campus atmosphere? Being a part of a university growing in national prominence? Working in a community of accessible excellence?&nbsp;</p> 
     <p> It's all of these... and more.&nbsp;</p> 
     <div class="nota-bene"> 
      <h4><img id="bang2" class="img_R_borderless" src="/staff/images/red_bang.png" alt="!" width="76" height="76" />Ready to Apply?</h4> 
      <p>To apply for a job at UC Riverside, visit the <a href="http://hr.ucr.edu/">Office of Human Resources website</a>.</p>

       <li><a class="standout2" href="http://hr.ucr.edu/jobs/DocDivCompBenefits.aspx">Learn about the benefits afforded to UCR employees</a></li> 
      </ul> 
     </div> 
     <ul class="striped big_links"> 
      <li><a href="/about/impacts.html"> <strong>Making a Difference in our Region</strong> UCR's economic impact on the state of California exceeds $1 billion. Spending by the university, its students, and visitors helped generate more than 11,000 full-time jobs statewide. Locally, UCR supports the local economy via its more than 1,000 faculty members, 5,500 staff members and 20,000 students.</a></li> 
      <li><a href="/about/region.html"> <strong>Riverside: A Great Place to Work and Live</strong>Riverside is an exciting and vibrant community. The City of Riverside has won numerous other awards for excellence in city governance and public services, including recognition of efforts to create innovative broadband applications, creating green, sustainable communities. Learn more about the region we call home.</a></li> 
      <li><a href="http://diversity.ucr.edu/"><strong>Embracing Diversity</strong> UC Riverside's multicultural, ethnically diverse, and socially safe environment provides our students, staff and faculty with a successful and positive educational climate . Learn more about the culture of diversity at UCR and the programs that forge its foundation.</a></li> 
      <li><a href="http://staffassembly.ucr.edu/"><strong>The Voice of the UCR's Staff</strong> The UCR Staff Assembly promotes the interests and well-being of all staff career employees. Overseen by an executive board, the Staff Assembly works in conjunction with the Office of the Chancellor to support the university's mission, sponsors social events and outings and supports activities that enrich the personal and professional lives of UCR staff.</a></li> 
      <li><a href="http://cdc.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx"><strong>Childcare at the UCR Child Development Center</strong> UCR faculty members are eligible to enroll their children in the UCR Child Development Center. The curriculum at the center follows guidelines set by the California Department of Education and the National Association for the Education of Young Children. All of the centers programs are accredited by the National Academy of Early Childhood Programs.</a></li>

       <div id="ctl00_PlaceHolderMain_ctl01__ControlWrapper_RichHtmlField" style="display:inline">
        <h3>Recognizing Excellence </h3> 
        <p class="openuptext-top">Scholarships are awarded on the basis of academic achievement, talent, and/or athletic ability. UCR, along with outside organizations, offer a variety of scholarships to offset college costs. </p> 
        <p class="openuptext-top"><strong>What You Should Know:</strong> Scholarships do NOT have to be repaid. Outside Scholarships replace loan or Federal Work-Study whenever possible. However, regulations may require adjustments, for example,&nbsp;to Cal Grant awards for students whose total awards exceed their Cal Grant need figure.</p>

            <div class="accordion1_4_2" xmlns:x="http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema" xmlns:d="http://schemas.microsoft.com/sharepoint/dsp" xmlns:asp="http://schemas.microsoft.com/ASPNET/20" xmlns:__designer="http://schemas.microsoft.com/WebParts/v2/DataView/designer" xmlns:sharepoint="Microsoft.SharePoint.WebControls" xmlns:ddwrt2="urn:frontpage:internal">
             <h3><a id="accdn1" href="#accdn1">UCR Foundation Scholarships, Fellowships, and Awards</a></h3>
             <div>
              <p>See all the <a href="http://scholarships.ucr.edu/">scholarships</a> at UCR for prospective /incoming/transfer students and current undergraduate and graduate students. Check out some of the leading merit scholarships available for each type of student below.</p>
             </div>
             <h3><a id="accdn2" href="#accdn2">Entering Students</a></h3>
             <div>
              <p>As an entering student, you apply for scholarships on the University of California Application for Undergraduate Admission and Scholarships during November. UCR Scholarships are awarded to entering students based on a record of high academic achievement and/or leadership skills. </p> 
              <h5>Regents Scholarship</h5> 
              <p>The most prestigious scholarship awarded to incoming UCR freshmen  Regent Scholars are notified of the award at the time of admission. Students are selected based on a review of their academic record by the faculty Committee on Scholarships and Honors. The Regents Scholarship is a renewable award and will be awarded to students for up to four years if all renewal criteria are met.</p> 
              <h5>Chancellor's Scholarship</h5> 
              <p>Awarded to incoming UCR freshmen with a distinguished high school academic record, the Chancellor's Scholarship provides an honorarium applied toward your fees. If you are offered this award, you can find more info regarding the terms and amount of your award on MyUCR .</p> 
              <h5>Highlander Excellence Scholarship</h5> 
              <p>Awarded to incoming UCR freshmen with a distinguished high school academic record, the Highlander Excellence Scholarship provides an honorarium towards college expenses. If you are offered this award, you can find more information regarding the terms and amount of your award on MyUCR. </p> 
              <h5>Alumni Scholarships</h5> 
              <p>The UCR Alumni Association offers non-renewable merit-based scholarships to entering California resident freshmen and re-entry students over the age of 25 who have had an interruption in their education of at least two years. Applicants must demonstrate leadership in extracurricular activities, community activities, employment or personal situations. The application process requires submission of an essay, and top candidates are selected for an interview.<br />Alumni scholarships include the <a href="http://alumni.ucr.edu/Page.aspx?pid=352">Alumni Freshman Scholarship Program</a> and the <a href="http://alumni.ucr.edu/Page.aspx?pid=357">Re-entry Scholarship program</a>.</p>
             </div>
             <h3><a id="accdn3" href="#accdn3">Continuing Students</a></h3>
             <div>
              <p>If you're a continuing student, scholarships are often awarded based on both financial need and academic excellence. </p> 
              <h5>Continuing Student Undergrad Scholarships</h5> 
              <p>Continuing Student Scholarship Applications are available <a title="" href="http://vcsaweb.ucr.edu/continuingStudentScholarship" target="_blank">online</a> in December each year and must be submitted by March 2 prior to the award year. These awards are based on a student's academic achievement and, except for honorary scholarships, on need. They generally range from $100 to $5000. All need-based awards are subject to compliance with UCR priority aid <a title="" href="/qualify/Pages/default.aspx">application deadlines</a>. Application requirements for continuing student UCR scholarships may vary.&nbsp;Award decisions are made by a scholarship selection committee during the summer or fall quarter. </p> 
              <p>View&nbsp;a complete list, including departmental awards,&nbsp;of&nbsp;all of&nbsp;<a title="" href="https://advancementservices.ucr.edu/Scholarship/FundList.aspx?ss=Continuingundergraduatestudents" target="_blank">UCR's continuing student scholarships</a>. </p>
             </div>
             <h3><a id="accdn4" href="#accdn4">All Students</a></h3>
             <div>
              <p>Many&nbsp;students choose to apply for departmental scholarships and those awarded by organizations outside of UCR.</p> 
              <h5>Middle Class Scholarships</h5> 
              <p>Starting in the 2014-15 academic year, the Middle Class Scholarship (MCS) program will provide scholarships to undergraduate students with family incomes up to $150,000, and who attend a University of California campus</p> 
              <p>Both Cal Grants and the Middle Class Scholarship are only available by filling out the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) atat <a href="http://www.fafsa.ed.gov/"><span style="color:rgb(57, 102, 191);font-family:'Arial','sans-serif';font-size:10pt;text-decoration:none;text-underline:none">www.fafsa.ed.gov</span></a><span style="color:black;font-family:'Arial','sans-serif';font-size:10pt"></span> or the CA Dream Act Application at <a href="http://www.caldreamact.org/"><span style="color:rgb(57, 102, 191);font-family:'Arial','sans-serif';font-size:10pt;text-decoration:none;text-underline:none">www.caldreamact.org</span></a><span style="color:black;font-family:'Arial','sans-serif';font-size:10pt">.</span></p> 
              <p>Remember, you won't be eligible for the money unless you fill out the application and meet the March 2, 2014 deadline.</p> 
              <h5>Outside Agency Scholarships </h5> 
              <p>Incoming and continuing UCR students are encouraged to check frequently&nbsp;for scholarship opportunities sent to UCR from outside agencies. See a list of these scholarships and find out <a title="Scholarships List" href="http://www.scholarshiplist.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">how to apply</a><strong>.</strong></p> 
              <h5>Departmental Scholarships</h5> 
              <p>A variety of scholarships are awarded by academic departments or college offices. For example, Chancellor's Performance awards are available through the departments of <a title="" href="http://dance.ucr.edu/degrees/awards_grants.html" target="_blank">Dance</a> , <a title="" href="http://www.art.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Art</a> , <a title="" href="http://music.ucr.edu/about.html" target="_blank">Music</a> , and <a title="" href="http://www.creativewriting.ucr.edu/contest_awards/index.html" target="_blank">Creative Writing</a>. </p> 
              <p>View&nbsp;a complete list of&nbsp;all of&nbsp;<a title="" href="https://advancementservices.ucr.edu/Scholarship/FundList.aspx?ss=Continuingundergraduatestudents" target="_blank">UCR's continuing student scholarships</a>. </p>
             </div>

     <h3>Investing Your Time in Your Community</h3> 
     <p>UC Riverside invites community members to play a part in campus life by participating in a number of programs and activities. An investment of your time can benefit both you and the community as a whole. </p> 
     <p>Become a part of something larger than yourself and reap the benefits from the shared knowledge and experience of others by volunteering at UCR.</p> 
     <h3>Where You Can Get Involved with UCR </h3> 
     <p>There are a variety of programs and organizations that can use your help. Here is a list of just a few.</p> 
     <ul class="striped big_links"> 
      <li><a href="http://gardens.ucr.edu/involved.html"><strong>The UCR Botanic Gardens</strong> The gardens relies on volunteers in most areas of its operations and activities. Volunteers are the backbone of all events and keep the gardens running smoothly on a day to day basis. Join and become active in the Friends of the UCR Botanic Gardens.</a></li> 
      <li> <a href="http://community.ucr.edu/citizens.html"><strong>Citizens University Committee</strong>The Citizens University Committee (CUC) creates awareness and fosters ties between UCR and the community. Monthly breakfast meetings between the CUC and Riverside community members address issues ranging from students social activities to campus expansion.</a></li> 
      <li> <a href="http://community.ucr.edu/affiliates.html"><strong>Affiliates of UCR</strong>The Affiliates of UC Riverside foster friendship and understanding between the City of Riverside and UCR. The membership consists of people from the community and campus. The group's scholarship fund helps to support students, awarding over $10,000 in scholarships annually.</a></li> 
      <li> <a href="http://www.athleticsassociation.ucr.edu/"><strong>Highlander Athletics Association</strong>The Highlander Athletics Association provide the critical financial support required for UCR to provide life changing educational experiences for more than 300 exceptional student-athletes. Join the Highlander Athletics Association today and make a difference in the life of a student!</a> </li> 
      <li> <a href="http://www.alumni.ucr.edu/Page.aspx?pid=781"><strong>UCR Advocacy Network</strong>The UCR Advocacy Network enhances public understanding and supports the University of California and UCR. Advocates help convey university-wide and campus-specific messages to ensure that the university's mission as well as its legislative and budgetary objective are given due consideration by governmental decision makers and the general public. </a></li> 
     </ul>

     <h3>Sharing our Discoveries With the World</h3> 
     <p>Taking a discovery from the lab and getting it to a place where it can help people requires a lot of work. UC Riverside maintains an active patenting and patent licensing program through <a href="http://or.ucr.edu/OTC.aspx">Research and Economic Developments Technology Commercialization</a> program, ensuring that the results of our research are made available for the publics use and benefit.</p> 
     <p>UCR has had many remarkable research success . Our inventions have run the gamut from research in plant varieties, plant genomics, biomedical sciences, materials science and engineering.</p> 
     <p>The UC system has used patents and licensing for more than 40 years to promote the development of research while also preventing that emerging research from straying outside the inventors control.</p> 
     <h4>The Process of Transitioning to Market</h4> 
     <p><img class="img_R_borderless" src="/industry/images/licensing_r.png" alt="r" width="146" height="140" /> Transitioning a product or technology from the lab to the marketplace involves several steps, all of which are designed to protect the rights of the person or persons whose breakthroughs will be in great demand. These steps include:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li><em><strong>Publication </strong></em> Proper publication and disclosure of an invention is crucial to ensuring patent rights to the invention, both domestically and abroad.</li> 
      <li><em><strong>Reporting </strong></em> University policy calls for disclosure of all inventions for which a patent could conceivably be necessary.</li> 
      <li><em><strong>Evaluations </strong></em> The university will help an inventor determine whether an invention is likely patentable, what its commercial potential may be and how much it likely will cost to protect the invention with patents in the U.S. and abroad.</li> 
      <li><em><strong>Patent applications</strong> </em> The University Licensing Office coordinates the process of seeking a patent. The inventor works closely with a patent attorney to complete the process and answer questions from a U.S. patent examiner.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>The process can take two years, but it is the safest way to protect an invention for the benefit of the inventor and the university.</p>
