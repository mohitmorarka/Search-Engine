     <h1 align="center"><strong>WELCOME TO THE WORLD OF WRITING</strong></h1> 
     <p>The UCR MFA in Creative Writing and Writing for the Performing Arts (CWPA) is one of the most diverse and vibrant multi-genre MFA writing programs in the country. Our MFA student and alumni poets and prose writers have published full-length collections, memoirs and novels, chapbooks, reviews, essays and articles with first-rate houses and journals. Our playwrights and screenwriters have had plays staged locally and around the country, have optioned, sold and been hired to write scripts, and received some of the most prestigious awards in their fields. Due to the multi-genre nature of our program, our students often succeed in more than one field: fiction writers have not only published fiction, but seen their plays produced or screenplays optioned; screenwriters have published poems or stories; playwrights have placed creative non-fiction pieces. As the media in which we work and the old genre boundaries increasingly intersect, overlap and are redefined, our students are uniquely prepared to take advantage of every opportunity. Our program is truly multi-genre, and about excellent writing in whatever form it takes. </p> 
     <p>To learn more about our program, our faculty and our students' achievements, please explore the following resources:</p>

      <h3>Anti-Spam Efforts at UCR</h3> 
      <p>Spam poses a serious threat to productivity and Computing and Communications is therefore committed to providing effective solutions aimed at reducing the amount of unsolicited and unwanted e-mail that arrives at the e-mail inboxes of UCR faculty, students and staff.</p> 
      <p>During the past several years, UCR has utilized <em>spam tagging </em>as its primary defense against e-mail spammers. Spam tagging works by checking all incoming mail and rating the mail with a number; when this number reaches a certain level (determined by the campus) the e-mail is tagged as spam. This tagging process makes it easy to create a <em>filter</em> allowing an e-mail client (e.g. Eudora, Outlook, Exchange) to automatically move these messages to a spam (or trash) folder.</p> 
      <p>Unfortunately, great care must be taken to select the point at which e-mail is tagged as spam. Setting the threshold <em>too low</em> results in &quot;false positives&quot; (legitimate e-mail tagged as spam). Setting the level <em>too high</em> allows spam to avoid the tagging process altogether. Moreover, spammers (those responsible for sending spam mail) have become increasingly effective disguising spam as legitimate e-mail, thus avoiding campus tagging efforts.</p> 
      <p>As a result, Computing and Communications (C&amp;C) has adopted additional measures aimed at further reducing the impact of spam at UCR. The latest initiative to control spam is SMTP authentication. SMTP authentication requires users connecting to UCR's e-mail servers to send mail from outside the campus network to possess a valid set of UCR credentials. Spammers, who frequently attempt to send mail with a fictitious @ucr.edu e-mail address, lack these credentials, and their e-mail is rejected by the system. UCR users on campus will be unaffected by these changes, but those who send mail from home, the road, or smartphones and tablets should <a href="/antispam/authhome.html">learn about changes they may need to make to their systems</a>. More information on all of UCR's antispam initiatives can be found by clicking on the left hand menu.</p>

     <h5 style="line-height: 18px;"> <em>SoBA Global Programs Team Returns from India and Turkey</em></h5> 
     <p> <em>(January 21, 2014)<img alt="" src="http://soba.ucr.edu/images/news/169.jpg" style="height: 333px; margin: 5px; float: right;" /></em></p> 
     <p style="text-align: justify;"> For many college students, winter break conjures up images of rest, relaxation, and great food. Whether catching up with friends locally or back at home with family, academics are the least of students' worries. However, a group of UCR Business students spent this year's winter break doing the exact opposite.</p> 
     <p style="text-align: justify;"> From December 11-19, 2013, the&nbsp;SoBA Global Programs held their second trip to Mumbai, India concluding in Istanbul, Turkey.&nbsp; The week-long intensive exposed the team to the massive city of Mumbai, India's financial capital and Turkey's&nbsp;largest city located both in Europe and Asia.&nbsp; Corporate and cultural site visits included:</p> 
     <p> <strong>India</strong></p> 
     <ul> 
      <li> Tata Consultancy, one of the world's leading IT consulting firms</li> 
      <li> National Stock Exchange of India, India's largest exchange</li> 
      <li> The American Consulate</li> 
      <li> The Gateway of India, one of India's most famous colonial landmarks</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p> <strong>Turkey</strong></p> 
     <ul> 
      <li> The Blue Mosque and&nbsp;Hagia Sophia, among the world's largest and most famous mosques</li> 
      <li> Basilica Cistern, the world's largest underground cistern built in the 6th century</li> 
      <li> The Grand Bazaar, one of the oldest and largest covered markets in the world dating from the 15th century</li> 
      <li> Alarko&nbsp;Headquarters, Turkey's largest conglomerate company</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p> University of Mumbai, one of India's oldest and most distinguished universities, served as the host university.&nbsp; Students heard customized lectures from Indian faculty and practitioners on topics ranging from the history of India and Mumbai, India's liberalization, privatization, and globalization initiatives, and the future of the Indian economy.&nbsp; While in Istanbul, students enjoyed lectures on the history and future of Turkey by&nbsp;UCR MBA alum,&nbsp;Ece Baykut, now a brand management expert working in Istanbul.&nbsp; The program concluded with a&nbsp;personalized presentation by the CEO of&nbsp;Alarko Holdings.&nbsp;</p> 
     <p> The&nbsp;SoBA Global Programs is now enrolling for the next trip&nbsp;during spring break to&nbsp;University of Oxford where students will live in residence at Mansfield College&nbsp;taking part in UCR's customized program, <em>Oxford Young Entrepreneurial Leadership Program.&nbsp; </em>Enjoy the video of the &quot;UCR in India&quot;&nbsp;trip and visit the links below to learn more about&nbsp;SoBA's exciting trips to the world's most important markets</p> 
     <p> <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Vlv-4NwqEYE?list=UUZCJwwpKDBQIvAaI_pBeymg" width="560"></iframe></p> 
     <ul> 
      <li> <a href="http://soba.ucr.edu/global/">UCR Global Programs</a> (Program information)</li>
