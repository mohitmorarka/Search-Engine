     <p><a href="javascript:jumpBillboard()"><img src="images/books/RezaAslan.jpg" name="billboard" width="320" height="240" align="left" class="box" /></a>The <strong>Department of Creative Writing</strong> at UCR offers the only Bachelor of Arts in Creative Writing in the University of California system and <a href="http://writingmfa.ucr.edu/">MFA in Creative Writing and Writing for the Performing Arts</a>. It is a growing and dynamic program made up entirely of established writers and poets. Courses at UCR are designed for all students in the language arts, and they emphasize developing each student's skills and talents. Through writing fiction, poetry, nonfiction and/or drama, students examine language and meaning both as practitioners and as readers as they develop and hone essential writing techniques.</p> 
     <p>Every writer needs to develop a critical sense to augment creative ability. For this reason, the Creative Writing Department offers two types of courses. Workshop courses are seminars that focus on writing and on the discussion of student work. Reading courses for writers focus on aspects of literature presented from a writer's point of view. Frequently they employ writing in imitation as one of several approaches to understanding the craft of writing. Upper division workshop courses are offered at the beginning, intermediate, and advanced levels in poetry, nonfiction, and fiction. Several reading courses link two genres such as fiction and poetry, and poetry and drama.</p>

      <h1>UCR ARTSblock</h1> 
      <br />
      <br /> 
      <p>University of California, Riverside, has recently brought together its three premier art institutions to create a new cultural complex: UCR ARTSblock. ARTSblock is composed of the California Museum of Photography, the Sweeney Art Gallery, and the Barbara and Art Culver Center of the Arts. Located on a single block in adjacent historical buildings in downtown Riverside, easily accessible to the public as well as the campus, ARTSblock's mission is to provide a cultural presence, educational resource, community center and intellectual meeting ground for the university and the community. <br /><br /> ARTSblock's programs investigate the art of our time, media arts, and the history and practice of photography through exhibitions, performances, screenings, workshops, artist residencies, permanent collections, and creative laboratories. ARTSblock presents significant national and international art and photography, and showcases UCR student artwork and regional artists. <br /><br /> ARTSblock's activities embody University of California, Riverside's commitment to broadly-based public education and cutting-edge research. ARTSblock offers innovative programs that engage diverse audiences, nourish the imagination and challenge assumptions.</p>

     <h5> <em>UCR School of Business Administration and California Steel Industries have begun a partnership that extends CSI employees a reduction in tuition.</em></h5> 
     <p> <img alt="CSI MOU" src="http://soba.ucr.edu/images/news/168.jpg" style="width: 500px; height: 333px; margin: 5px; float: right;" /><em>(December 13, 2013)</em></p> 
     <p> <span style="font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em;">The UCR School of Business Administration (SoBA) and California Steel Industries (CSI) have entered into an agreement that allows CSI employ</span>ees a reduction in tuition should they apply for the MPAc, MFin, or Flex MBA programs offered at the UCR business school.<span style="font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em;">&nbsp;</span></p> 
     <p> <span style="font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em;">The partnership between UCR SoBA and CSI</span><span style="font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em;"> was made official at a memorandum of understanding (MOU) signing ceremony held at the California Steel Industries facilities on December 11.</span></p> 
     <p> In attendance from CSI was Toshiyuki &quot;Ted&quot; Tomai (President / CEO), Rod Hoover (Human Resources Manager), Luz Rodriguez-Roldan (Assistant Manager, Human Resources), Harold Allin (Vice-President, Operations), and several UCR alumni.</p> 
     <p class="p1"> &quot;One of the best things that we can do to help the community is to provide the educational support to those that are interested in broadening their business acumen. We welcome this partnership with California Steel Industries wholeheartedly and hope to continue building this relationship,&quot; says Interim Dean Yunzeng Wang.</p>
