      <p> Bob Slater | Superintendent of Building Services<br /><a href="mailto:bobsl@ucr.edu">bobsl@ucr.edu</a></p> 
      <p> Supervising the following:<br />Administrative Assistant<br /> Building Services Mission<br /> <a href="/building/special.html">Special Event Services</a></p> 
      <ul> 
       <li> Relocation Services</li> 
       <li>Event Services</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p> Custodial Operations:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>&quot;Swing&quot; Shift</li> 
       <li>&quot;Graveyard&quot; Shift</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p> BUILDING AND SPECIAL SERVICES provides a variety of essential services for the campus community.&nbsp; These include, but are not limited to:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li> Relocation Service.&nbsp; Our efficient, well trained and prepared staff is eager to be of assistance to you in any/all moves, large or small.&nbsp; Estimates for such services are readily presented upon request.&nbsp; Should the magnitude of a move be too large or the time frame too limited we will attain an estimate from our service contractor for you, and will oversee the operation to maximize your ease of mind.</li> 
       <li> Event Preparation.&nbsp; In order to ensure our customers, the campus community, a more affordable means of planning and putting on events, we have purchased a large number of items used in such programs and have made them available for rent.&nbsp; A complete list of these items and their associated costs have been determined, to cover purchase costs, care, maintenance and replacement expense only. Our fast, efficient and dependable staff will handle the complete preparation of the event for you and will restore the site immediately thereafter.&nbsp; For a detailed estimate please contact us at ext. 4219.</li> 
       <li> Custodial Services. Routine custodial services are accomplished during the hours when the campus is least used, and service may vary between the &quot;swing&quot; shift and the &quot;graveyard&quot; shift. Our current arrangement provides for the daily service to all &quot;public&quot; areas as well as General Purpose classrooms, and twice per week service to all other areas. This service is available to all &quot;non-state&quot; funded activities as well, on a cost basis only, and includes: 
        <ul> 
         <li> Routine custodial service</li> 
         <li> Detailed Carpet Care</li> 
         <li> Detailed hard surface floor care</li> 
         <li> Complete room/building preparation</li> 
        </ul> </li> 
      </ul> 
      <p> Building and Special Services also provides Emergency Service to the campus on a seven day per week, twenty-four hour basis.&nbsp; Our staff are responsive to the campus needs during times of internal disasters, including floods, fires, earthquakes, etc.</p> 
      <p> We look forward to being of service to you.</p>

       <h4><span style="color: #000000;">Why is </span><strong><span style="color: #990000;">NOW</span></strong><span style="color: #000000;"> the time to prepare for a career in teaching science and mathematics?</span></h4> 
       <p>There is currently a critical shortfall of qualified science and mathematics teachers in California classrooms. Because of this shortage, prospective science and mathematics teachers are extremely competitive in securing positions soon after graduation.</p> 
       <p>They are able to:</p> 
       <ul class="star"> 
        <li>find internship jobs immediately upon graduation at salaries comparable to entry-level credentialed teachers; these intern opportunities often lead to permanent teaching positions</li> 
        <li>find job openings in the school district of their desire</li> 
        <li>become emerging leaders in the school districts and communities</li> 
        <li>benefit from the financial incentives, including loan-forgiveness programs, offered through the government<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ScienceMathInitiativeAtUcr"><img src="/images/facebook.jpg" alt="smi facebook" width="270" height="187" /></a></li> 
       </ul> 
       <p><a href="http://calteach.universityofcalifornia.edu/"><strong>UCR-SMI is part of the UC-system wide California Teach - Science and mathematics Initiative</strong></a></p> 
       <p><strong><strong><img src="/images/calteach-logo.gif" alt="uc-smi logo" width="240" height="68" /></strong></strong></p>

      <h3 class="accessibility">Undergraduate Programs: Where Do You Want to Be Tomorrow?</h3> 
      <p>With 65 undergraduate majors and programs to choose from, UC Riverside has a course of study that fits your interests and will help you reach your goals. <a href="/academics/undergradmajors.html">Explore our list of majors</a>. </p> 
      <h4><img id="student1" class="img_R_borderless" src="/academics/images/student1.jpg" alt="." width="139" height="185" />Resources to succeed in your education</h4> 
      <p>The <a href="http://www.ue.ucr.edu/">Office of Undergraduate Education</a> is committed to student success and the creation of a supportive educational environment at UCR. The office oversees the development of programs designed to support student learning, research and experiential opportunities. Visit our offices to learn about the support programs that can help you be more competitive in the job market and in the pursuit of graduate and professional education. </p>

      <h3 class="accessibility">Drive to Succeed With UCR Summer Sessions</h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/academics/summer.html">Summer Sessions program</a> provides the opportunity for students to attend summer classes at the university without being a full-time enrolled UCR student. </p> 
      <p>Classes may be open to: </p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>College and university students</li> 
       <li>High school graduates</li> 
       <li>High school students entering grades 10-12 in 2010 through the <a href="http://summeracademy.ucr.edu/">Summer Academy For Advanced High School Students</a></li> 
       <li>Adult learners</li> 
       <li><a href="http://summer.ucr.edu/international_students.html">International students</a></li>
