     <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="mainContent" -->Administrative Studies is housed in the&nbsp;
     <a href="http://www.chass.ucr.edu">College of Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences</a>&nbsp;and is offered through the departments of&nbsp;
     <a href="http://www.arthistory.ucr.edu" target="_blank">Art History</a>,&nbsp;

     <a href="http://www.sociology.ucr.edu" target="_blank">Sociology</a>. Descriptions of the five departments and detailed program information can be found on respective department websites.&nbsp;
     <br /> 
     <br /> The Administrative Studies component of the major provides an interdisciplinary education in administrative analytical skills and decision-making. The cooperating departments provide a thorough grounding in their respective disciplines. The combination gives students a well-rounded education that can lead directly into a variety of careers or to graduate studies.&nbsp;
     <br /> 
     <br /> Faculty for the Administrative Studies majors is drawn from the cooperating departments and the Business Administration program. They are renowned for both teaching and research. All majors lead to a Bachelor of Arts degree in either Art History, Economics, History, and Political Science, or Sociology. The Sociology Department offers a Bachelor of Science degree as well.

     <p>The South Coast Air Quality Management District, with co-funding from the<span style="color: black;"> California Energy Commission and Southern California Gas Company,</span> has awarded <span style="color: black;">$3,500,000 from the Clean Fuels Fund to Cummins Inc. for the development, integration and demonstration of ultra-low emissions natural gas engines for heavy-duty vehicles. CE-CERT is a partner on the project. </span>Cummins Inc. is taking the lead in the development of a new engine that is designed to run on natural gas rather than the current design approach where a diesel engine is modified to run on natural gas. The design will be based on the ISX-15G 15L spark ignited engine equipped with advanced three way catalyst (TWC) technology. CE-CERT’s role is to work with the team to optimize the integrated engine/chassis vehicle before placing the vehicles in a six month demonstration program and to measure the emissions with driving cycles that simulate in-use conditions. The testing will involve several weeks on UCR’s chassis emission dynamometer, with regulated and non-regulated emissions being measured; including, ammonia, ultra fines, and nitrous oxide. UCR will report on the fuel economy, criteria and greenhouse gas emissions.</p>
