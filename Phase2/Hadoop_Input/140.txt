      <h3>An Overview of CRAMS Users and Terms</h3> 
      <p> </p>
      <h4>Who uses CRAMS?</h4> 
      <p></p> 
      <div class="accordion marginBtm10"> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> College Approver</h4> 
       <div class="ui-accordion-content ui-helper-reset ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" role="tabpanel" style="display: none; height: 0px"> 
        <p><strong>College Approver</strong> – The college representative that coordinates courses for approval at the college executive committee meeting. He or she also records the approval signature and date for each course the committee approves.</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Committee on Courses Approver</h4> 
       <div class="ui-accordion-content ui-helper-reset ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" role="tabpanel" style="display: none; height: 0px"> 
        <p><strong>Committee on Courses Approver</strong> – The representative of the <a href="http://senate.ucr.edu/committee/?do=info&amp;id=8">Committee on Courses</a> that coordinates all undergraduate, graduate and professional courses for approval at the Committee on Courses meeting. He or she also records the approval signature and date for each course the council approves.</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Faculty Approver</h4> 
       <div class="ui-accordion-content ui-helper-reset ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" role="tabpanel" style="display: none; height: 0px"> 
        <p><strong>Faculty Approver </strong>– The faculty member on the college executive committee, Graduate Council, or Committee on Courses that reviews course proposals online via CRAMS. This faculty member provides feedback and approval before passing it along. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Graduate Approver</h4> 
       <div class="ui-accordion-content ui-helper-reset ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" role="tabpanel" style="display: none; height: 0px"> 
        <p><strong>Graduate Approver </strong>– The representative of the Graduate Division and Graduate Council that coordinates courses for approval at the Graduate Council meeting. He or she also records the approval signature and date for each course the council approves.</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Preparer</h4> 
       <div class="ui-accordion-content ui-helper-reset ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" role="tabpanel" style="display: none; height: 0px"> 
        <p><strong>Preparer</strong> – The person who prepares the proposal to create, restore or modify a course and enters it in CRAMS. <a href="/Content/pdfs/Find_a_Preparer.pdf">Find a preparer.</a></p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Reviewer</h4> 
       <div class="ui-accordion-content ui-helper-reset ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" role="tabpanel" style="display: none; height: 0px"> 
        <p><strong>Reviewer</strong> – The <a href="/registrar/help-desk/default.aspx">courses specialist</a> that reviews proposals, makes any editorial changes, ensures they meet requirements, and then passes them along to the college executive committee, Graduate Division or Committee on Courses for approval. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Superuser</h4> 
       <div class="ui-accordion-content ui-helper-reset ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" role="tabpanel" style="display: none; height: 0px"> 
        <p><strong>Superuser</strong> – The person that can grant preparers and approvers access to CRAMS. <a href="/crams/support-services/system-requirements.aspx">Find a superuser.</a></p> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
      <h4 class="padTop20">CRAMS Terms</h4> 
      <br /> 
      <strong class="sea-blue">WIPQ</strong> – Work-in-Progress Queue. Proposals saved but not yet submitted appear in this queue. New courses are green, modified courses are tan (or pink, depending on your monitor) and reviewed courses are red. You can access the WIPQ from the CRAMS Main Menu. 
      <p></p> 
      <strong class="sea-blue">Withdraw/Unwithdraw</strong> – If the department changes its mind and decides to withdraw a course submission before it has been approved, the course can be withdrawn at the preparer stage. If the department then decides to get the course approved, it can “unwithdraw” the course at the preparer stage and pick up where they left off. 
      <a class="noUnderline" href="/crams/course-management/withdraw-unwithdraw-a-course.aspx">Learn more</a>. 
      <p></p> 
      <strong class="sea-blue">Sweep/Unsweep</strong> –&nbsp;If the department changes its mind about a course&nbsp;after it has been approved&nbsp;and wants it unapproved, the reviewer can “sweep” the course. Think of it as sweeping a course under a rug. That way, if the department decides to reinstate the course, the reviewer can “unsweep” it, and retrieve it in its previously-approved form 
     </div>

     <h3>UCR: The Home of the Highlanders!</h3> 
     <p><img id="highlanders_logo" class="img_R_borderless" src="/athletics/images/ucr_athletics_logo.png" alt="UC Riverside Athletics Logo" width="173" height="178" />The proud history of Highlander athletics dates back to the university's inception in the mid-1950s. Today, UC Riverside’s Intercollegiate Athletics program features 17 sports competing at the NCAA Division I level in the Big West Conference.</p> 
     <h3>Pride in our Academic and Athletic Accomplishments</h3> 
     <p>The Highlanders compete in 17 sports at the NCAA Division I level, the highest level of collegiate competition in the country. We are proud members of the Big West Conference, a group of nine schools all based within the state of California. The Big West is the only conference in the nation that can claim this distinction.</p> 
     <p>UC Riverside has made a splash on the national scene with NCAA post-season appearances in the sports of Baseball, Women's Basketball, Women's Soccer, Men's Golf, Men's and Women's Cross Country and Men's and Women's Track and Field. To learn the latest news about <a href="http://www.gohighlanders.com">UCR Intercollegiate Athletics</a>, check out the website at <a class="external" title="extermal site: Highlander Athletics" href="http://www.gohighlanders.com">www.gohighlanders.com</a>.</p> 
     <div class="nota-bene"> 
      <img id="big_west" class="img_R_borderless_close" src="/athletics/images/intercollegiate_big_west.png" alt="Big West Conference" width="195" height="76" /> 
      <h4>The members of the Big West are:</h4> 
      <p>UC Riverside, UC Davis, UC Irvine, UC Santa Barbara, Hawaii, Cal Poly (San Luis Obispo, CA), Cal State Fullerton, Cal State Northridge, Long Beach State University</p> 
     </div> 
     <h3><img id="men_basketball" class="img_R_borderless_close" src="/athletics/images/intercollegiate_basketball.png" alt="Men's basketball" width="201" height="399" />It's more than just &quot;playing games&quot;</h3> 
     <p>The goals of Intercollegiate Athletics at UC Riverside are to create a program that:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Enhances the reputation of the university by striving for athletic and academic excellence.</li> 
      <li>Provides student-athletes with the opportunity for growth outside the classroom.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>The student-athlete's experiences on the practice field, the competition field and in the classroom combine to create a well-rounded individual with a dedication to integrity, teamwork, excellence, personal growth and individual respect.</p> 
     <p>Student-athletes at UCR are held to the same or higher academic standards than ordinary students, abiding by the rules and restrictions of the NCAA and Big West, in addition to the rules of UC Riverside. <strong>Highlander student-athletes often have higher GPA's than their non-athlete counterparts.</strong></p> 
     <div class="clear"></div> 
     <div class="column2"> 
      <h4><img class="border" src="/athletics/images/women_sports.jpg" alt="Women's Sports Image" width="226" height="50" /> <img src="/athletics/images/women_sports_title.gif" alt=" " width="103" height="20" /><span class="accessibility">Women's Sports</span></h4>

     <h3 class="section">Want to Come Root for Your Highlanders?</h3> 
     <ul> 
      <li><strong>All UCR undergraduate students are admitted for free to any home UCR sporting events</strong> with their student I.D. cards. Just show your card at the ticket booth to get in.</li> 
      <li>For non-students, tickets for UCR home events are available at the venue on the day of the game, online at <a class="external" href="http://www.gohighlanders.com">gohighlanders.com</a> or in advance from the UCR Athletics Ticket Office. For group sales, contact the Ticket Office at (951) 827-4653 or <a href="mailto:highlandertickets@ucr.edu?subject=web%20site%20inquiry">via e-mail.</a></li> 
      <li><a href="http://gohighlanders.com/calendar.aspx">Complete schedules for all</a> 17 UCR teams are available.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <img id="highlanders_mascot" class="img_L_borderless" src="/athletics/images/mascot_01.png" alt="UCR Mascot" width="182" height="245" /> 
     <h3>The UC Riverside Athletics Association:<br /> Helping UCR Athletics</h3> 
     <p>Made up of alumni, friends and family, the <a href="http://athleticsassociation.ucr.edu/">UC&nbsp;Riverside Athletics Association</a> is the booster organization for UCR Athletics. It encourages involvement in and financial support for UCR’s athletics program and the 300 young people who balance the rigors of “student” and “athlete.”</p> 
     <p><a class="button" href="https://advancementservices.ucr.edu/Givingform.aspx?DU=Athletics">Make a donation now</a></p>

      <h3>Executive Leadership</h3> 
      <p class="intro">Technical support, visioning, and guidance are provided by the Executive Director and the CIO as follows:</p> 
      <div class="boxA"> 
       <h4 class="inline">Charles Rowley is the Associate Vice Chancellor (AVC) and Chief Information Officer (CIO)</h4> 
       <p class="inline">and has primary responsibility for strategic planning and operational support related to UCR's campus-wide and system-wide information systems and voice and data networks.</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_people.html#chuckr">More about AVC Charles Rowley</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div class="boxB"> 
       <h4 class="inline">Bob Grant is the Executive Director and CTO</h4> 
       <p class="inline">and is responsible for providing the highest level of technical planning, consulting, and visioning support relating to the implementation and operation of systems, applications and campus computing (servers, networks, security, etc.) infrastructure.</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_people.html#bobg">More about Director Bob Grant</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <h3>Organizational Leadership</h3> 
      <p class="intro">Campus Information Services (CIS) is broadly organized as follows:</p> 
      <div class="boxC"> 
       <h4>Enterprise Application Development</h4> 
       <p>David Gracey is the Director of Enterprise Application Development and is responsible for a wide variety of software applications supporting UCR’s administrative and academic operating environments.</p> 
       <ul class="tight"> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_people.html#davidg">More about Director David Gracey</a></li> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_operations.html#ead">More about Enterprise Application Development</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div class="boxD"> 
       <h4>Academic Information Systems</h4> 
       <p>Mary Livaudais is the Director of Academic Information Systems provides leadership, technical expertise, and project management guidance to UCR's Student Information Systems (SIS) software team.</p> 
       <ul class="tight"> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_people.html#maryl">More about Director Mary Livaudais</a></li> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_operations.html#ais">More about Academic Information Systems&nbsp;</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div class="boxA"> 
       <h4>Database Administration Services</h4> 
       <p>Bob Ayers is the Director of Database Administration group and provides leadership, technical direction, and project management expertise to C&amp;C's Database Administration group.</p> 
       <ul class="tight"> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_people.html#boba">More about Director Bob Ayers</a></li> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_operations.html#dba">More about Database Administration Services</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div class="boxB"> 
       <h4>Educational Technology and Computing Services</h4> 
       <p>Israel Fletes is the Director of Educational Technology and Computing Support Services and is responsible for campus-wide desktop computing support, instructional technology services, classroom technology support, and other faculty instructional and research technology support initiatives.</p> 
       <ul class="tight"> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_people.html#israel">More about Director Israel Fletes</a></li> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_operations.html#css">More about Educational Technology &amp; Computing Services</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div class="boxA"> 
       <h4>Computing Infrastructure and Security</h4> 
       <p>Russ Harvey is the Director of Computing Infrastructure and Security and campus Information Security Officer, and he provides leadership relating to the development of long term infrastructure and security objectives as well as concomitant operational plans for acting on the long term vision.</p> 
       <ul class="tight"> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_people.html#russh">More about Director Russ Harvey</a></li> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_operations.html#cis">More about Computing Infrastructure &amp; Security</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div class="boxC"> 
       <h4>Communications Services</h4> 
       <p>Jill Hishmeh is the Director of Communication Services and provides leadership, direction, planning, and management to the Communications Services department encompassing all aspects of voice (telephone) and data (network) communications for the campus.</p> 
       <ul class="tight"> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_people.html#jillh">More about Director Jill Hishmeh</a></li> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_operations.html#comm">More about Communications Services</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div class="boxD"> 
       <h4>Policy, Planning, Finance, and Administration</h4> 
       <p>Shelley Gupta is the Chief Financial and Administrative Officer (CFAO) and manages all financial and administrative functions for Campus Information Services.</p> 
       <ul class="tight"> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_people.html#shelleyg">More about CFAO Shelley Gupta</a></li> 
        <li><a href="/about_mgmt_operations.html#ppfa">More about&nbsp;Policy, Planning, Finance, and Administration</a></li> 
       </ul>
