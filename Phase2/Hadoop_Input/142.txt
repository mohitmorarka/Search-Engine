     <p><img src="/about/images/main/counsel.jpg" alt="Students and professor at table" width="484" height="180" /></p> 
     <p><strong>The Office of Campus Counsel (OCC)</strong> provides counsel and legal advice to the chancellor and his cabinet as well as to the senior leadership at UCR.</p> 
     <p><strong>Interim Chief Campus Counsel David Bergquist</strong> provides legal advice and assistance on a wide range of matters affecting faculty, students and staff. Such matters include labor and employment, student affairs, faculty issues, research, academic affairs, land use, dispute resolution, compliance and regulations and policies of The Regents.</p> 
     <p>The chief campus counsel reports to <a href="http://chancellor.ucr.edu/">Chancellor Kim A. Wilcox</a>, as part of the Office of the Chancellor, and to <a class="external" href="http://ucop.edu/ogc/welcome.html">General Counsel and Vice President for Legal Affairs for The Regents of the University of California Charles F. Robinson</a>.</p> 
     <div class="l_xpander-box-faq">

      <h3>Frequently Asked Questions</h3> 
      <ul class="striped"> 
       <li> <strong>Where do I serve a subpoena, lawsuit, bankruptcy court notice, Earnings withholding notice, or Public Records Act and Information Practices Act request?</strong> 
        <div class="this-xpander-txt"> 
         <p><strong>Subpoenas for Documents*:</strong></p> 
         <p><strong>Payroll and Personnel Records:</strong> <br />Payroll Office/Accounting Services Office <br />1201 University Avenue, Suite 208 <br />Riverside, CA 92507 <br />Tel: (951) 827-3303</p> 
         <p><strong>Medical Records-Students:</strong> <br />UC Riverside Campus Health Center <br />900 University Avenue <br />Riverside, CA 92521 <br />Tel: (951) 827-3031</p> 
         <p><strong>Counseling Center:</strong> <br />UC Riverside Counseling Center <br />Veitch Student Center, North Wing <br />Riverside, CA 92521 <br />Tel: (951) 827-8255</p> 
         <p><strong>Student Records; other than Medical or Counseling</strong> <br />Office of the Registrar <br />2249 Student Services Bldg. <br />University of California, Riverside <br />Riverside, CA 92521-0118 <br />Tel: (951) 827-5977<br />*Subpoenas for Individuals to appear must be served on the individual</p> 
         <p><strong>Bankruptcy Court Notices:</strong><br />Student Business Services <br />900 University Avenue <br />Riverside, CA 92521</p> 
         <p><strong>Small Claims Service of Process:</strong><br />Risk Management<br />900 University Avenue<br />Surge Building<br />Riverside, CA 92521</p> 
         <p><strong>All Other Services of Process for Lawsuits (other than small claims actions):</strong><br />Office of General Counsel of the Regents<br />1111 Franklin Street, 8th Floor<br />Oakland, CA 94607-5200</p> 
         <p><strong>Public Records Act Requests and Information Practices Act Requests:</strong><br />Kristen Erving<br />Senior Paralegal Specialist<br />University of California, Riverside<br />900 University Avenue<br />3144 Hinderaker Hall<br />Riverside, CA 92521<br />Tel: (951) 827-5983<br />E-mail: <a href="mailto:PublicRecords@ucr.edu?subject=PRA%20request">PublicRecords@ucr.edu</a></p> 
         <p><strong>Earnings Withholding Orders:</strong><br />UC RIVERSIDE<br />Payroll and Personnel Records<br />Payroll Office/Accounting Services Office<br />1201 University Avenue, Suite 208<br />Riverside, CA 92507<br />Tel: (951) 827-3303</p> 
        </div> </li> 
       <li> <strong>What should I do if someone tries to serve me with a subpoena, lawsuit, bankruptcy court notice, earning withholding notice, or Public Records Act or Information Practices Act request?</strong> 
        <div class="this-xpander-txt"> 
         <p>A: If it is a service of process for a lawsuit (other than a small claims action), refuse to accept it and direct the server to the Office of General Counsel (<a href="http://www.ucop.edu/general-counsel/_files/servproc.pdf">http://www.ucop.edu/general-counsel/_files/servproc.pdf</a>).</p> 
         <p>A: If it is a service of process for a small claims court action, refuse to accept it and refer to: (<a href="http://risk.ucr.edu/">http://risk.ucr.edu/</a>)<br /><strong>Risk Management</strong><br />900 University Avenue<br />Surge Building<br />Riverside, CA 92521</p> 
         <p>A: If it is a subpoena and&nbsp;your office is not the proper recipient, refuse the subpoena and refer the server to the appropriate office.</p> 
         <p>A: If it is a Public Records Act request or Information Practices Act Request, accept it and immediately forward it to:</p> 
         <p><strong>Kristen Erving</strong><br />Senior Paralegal Specialist<br />University of California, Riverside<br />900 University Avenue<br />3144 Hinderaker Hall<br />Riverside, CA 92521<br />Tel: (951) 827-5983<br />E-mail: <a href="mailto:PublicRecords@ucr.edu?subject=PRA%20request">PublicRecords@ucr.edu</a></p> 
        </div></li> 
      </ul>

      <h3 style="margin-top: 20px;">CRAMS Frequently Asked Questions</h3> 
      <br /> 
      <div class="accordion"> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> What does &quot;Priority Enrollment&quot; in the Closing Notes mean? </h4> 
       <div> 
        <p> The Committee on Courses has determined that courses can be approved with an enrollment priority for a term of three years. After three years, the course will be able to be offered, but the priority enrollment aspect of it will not be allowed unless the course is submitted through the course proposal process and it is re-approved. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> What fields do I update for adding Priority Enrollment?</h4> 
       <div class="addBullet"> 
        <p>When updating a course for adding Priority Enrollment (without changing any other item in the prerequisite):</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li>Update the <u>Closing Notes</u> field with the following:&nbsp; “requesting priority enrollment”</li> 
         <li>Update the <u>Justification</u> section in the <u>Prerequisite</u> field with the following: “Adding enrollment priority statement. Please see programs affected section for specific enrollment priority statement per course specialist office.” If deleting a priority enrollment statement, update the justification in the prerequisite with: “Deleting priority enrollment.” It is important to fill in the justification so a change bar shows while everyone reviews the course (including the College Executive Committee and Committee on Courses).</li> 
         <li>Add the enrollment priority statement in the <u>Affected Programs</u> field. For example: “Enrollment priority during junior and senior enrollment is given to Business Economics majors, Economics majors and minors, Economics/Administrative Studies majors and Economics/Law and Society majors.”</li> 
         <li>The <u>Affected Programs</u> field also needs to include the other courses impacted by the proposal so that all departments will know which programs will be affected. You discover impacted courses by requesting reports. [REPORTS]</li> 
         <li>If the enrollment priority statement does not have all majors listed in the report, you will have a justification to list in the affected program tab immediately under the enrollment priority statement. For example: “After junior and senior enrollment period the class will be opened to the entire campus. Additionally, students in these programs have other course options to choose from besides ECON 104A; BA in Art History/Administrative Studies; BA in History/Administrative Studies; BA in Political Science/Administrative Studies; BA/BS in Sociology/Administrative Studies.”</li> 
         <li>If that is the case, you should send the report details in an email. For example: “Program search information: ECON 104A- BA in Art History/Administrative Studies; BA in Economics; BA in Business Economics; BA in Economics/Administrative Studies; BA in Economics/Law and Society; Minor in Economics; BA in History/Administrative Studies; BA in Political Science/Administrative Studies; BA/BS in Sociology/Administrative Studies.”</li> 
         <li>Update the course proposal as necessary with other updates.</li> 
         <li>Preview the course and make sure all information is in the required fields and there is a change bar on the <u>Prerequisite </u>field.</li> 
         <li>Submit the proposal in time for the posted deadlines as you would any other proposal.</li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> What does “Sunset Clause” mean?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>“Sunset Clause” is the term the Committee on Courses uses to state that Priority Enrollment for any course will lapse after three years. An updated course proposal will be needed if a department wants to continue to keep Priority Enrollment for the course for another term. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> What does “NOFY” stand for? </h4> 
       <div class="addBullet"> 
        <p>NOFY stands for “Not Offered for Four Years.”&nbsp; The Committee on Courses sends out a report every year in September* that shows courses that fall into one of the following categories:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li>Courses that were not offered for four years</li> 
         <li>Courses that were canceled or had zero enrollment every time they were offered</li> 
         <li>Regular session courses that were offered only during a summer session</li> 
        </ul> 
        <p>*Contact the <a href="http://senate.ucr.edu/">Academic Senate</a> Analyst if you have not received the yearly report. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> My course was cross-listed, but after I updated the description and removed one prerequisite, my course is no longer linked with the other course(s). How did it become unlinked? How do I re-link it to its partner?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>When updating a cross-listed course, the cross-listed course(s) will unlink (not show the cross-listed courses) as you update/save the course. </p> 
        <p>Before you submit the updated course, you need to check that the cross-listed course(s) are in the <strong><u>Cross-listed</u></strong> field of the <strong>Description/Grading/Etc</strong>. tab, under the <strong><u>Cross-listed with:</u></strong> field, in CRAMS.</p> 
        <p>You will need to re-link the cross-listed course(s). To re-link the cross-listed course(s) see <a href="/crams/course-management/cross-listed-courses.aspx" ..="" ourse-management="" ross-listed-courses.aspx="" "="">Dealing With Cross-Listed Courses</a> under <strong>Initiating Department (creates or updates SOC 100) starting at #2.</strong></p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> How do I know if I need to request a course search or/and program search report?</h4> 
       <div class="addBullet"> 
        <p>You must request reports if you are:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li>Deleting a course</li> 
         <li>Renumbering a course</li> 
         <li>Changing a course title</li> 
         <li>Changing the units</li> 
         <li>Changing a cross-listing</li> 
         <li>Changing a credit statement</li> 
         <li>Submitting a course proposal with an enrollment priority statement or restriction</li> 
        </ul> 
        <p>You can <a href="../CourseProposal/Reports.aspx">request reports through our online form</a>. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> I submitted my course but it’s cross-listed with another course that didn’t get submitted, what should I do so my course can move forward?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>Cross-listed courses are courses that are exactly the same. In CRAMS, use the <strong>Search/Modify</strong> feature to search for the cross-listed course that is cross-listed with your current course proposal; the course should be RED. See Location of Courses for help searching for a course. </p> 
        <p>EXAMPLES of what you might see in <u>Determining the Location of a Course Proposal</u> for cross-listed courses: </p> 
        <p>If your submitted course says “<em>WAITING”</em> This means the course is waiting for cross- listed partner(s) department approval</p> 
        <p>The cross-listed partner(s) will say&nbsp; “Saved - (New, Changed, Deleted).” Then the course is in the departments’ WIPQ of that course(s) department. It may be in a certain preparer’s WIPQ (click College/Department tab, in CRAMS, to see who may have it).</p> 
        <p>The primary course submitter’s responsibility is to contact the department preparer of all cross-listed course(s), to get the cross-listed course(s) approved and sent forward. When all cross-listed partner(s) have been approved by their department(s) and submitted, the course status will change to “Submitted – (New, Changed, or Deleted).” If the primary course or any other course(s) that are cross-listed say “<em>WAITING,” </em>that means the course proposal(s)&nbsp; are still “<em>WAITING</em>” for department approval and <strong><u>have not</u></strong>&nbsp;been sent on with department approval dates to the Registrar Office.</p> 
        <p>If the cross-listed course(s) are un-cross-listed and have not be “saved” in the WIPQ of their department WIPQ and the primary course has been submitted, then ask the Office of the Registrar to return the submitted course so you can re-cross-list the course(s). </p> 
        <p>If you are unable to find the cross-listed course(s) from using the <strong>Search/Modify</strong> <strong>Course</strong> <a href="../CourseManagement/SearchForACourse.aspx">instructions</a> and/or <a href="../LocationOfCourses/Default.aspx">Location of Courses</a>, contact the Office of the Registrar to help in your search.</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> Why do I need to request a report if I’m only changing the course title?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>Changing the title of a course affects inventory in SIS. Any proposal that affects inventory requires at the least a program search to keep the records straight and to keep a history. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> How do I insert the Committee on Courses extension approval in the Faculty Correspondence field?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>Using Microsoft Word, highlight the entire extension request, right click and select copy. Then open CRAMS <u>Faculty Correspondence</u> field by using Modify. Then, select the icon for paste in CRAMS. The file will be pasted into the field. Next select SC or File Save &amp; Close. Lastly, select the yellow Save Work button to save the information.</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> When is the latest that I can submit my course proposal and have it approved for the current term so I can offer it?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>Check <a href="http://senate.ucr.edu/committee/8/Submission%20Deadlines%20for%202014-15.pdf" target="_blank">Dates and Deadlines</a> for the course proposal deadlines. </p> 
        <p>Course(s) have a process and are “routed” differently please looking at <a href="../course-proposal/approval-routing.aspx">general routing process</a>, routing should be considered if you intend to request an extension (late submission request) </p> 
        <p>If you are past the deadline you will need a <a href="http://senate.ucr.edu/committee/8/request_for_extension_of_course_proposal_submission_deadline_form.html" target="_blank">late submission request</a>. Fill in the late submission request and send email to: the Course Specialist, and cc staff support- committee analyst, as well as the Chair of Committee on Courses.</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> Do I have to submit a new syllabus with the course proposal or can I just leave the old one in?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>A new syllabus is required for “New”, “Restored”, and/or substantially changed courses. </p> 
        <p>Substantially changed courses include courses that have <strong>increased the units </strong>and/or<strong> the activities have changed</strong>. The Committee on Courses needs to determine if the proposed activities have the necessary requirements to reflect the units.</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> How do I find a course that was renumbered?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>Use the <strong>Search/Modify Course</strong> tab in CRAMS (see Searching for Courses [RELATIVE LINK]) and select the course that was renumbered from. For example, if SOC 040 was renumbered to SOC 002M, enter SOC 040 in the <u>Subject Area</u> and <u>Course Number</u> fields and select <strong>approved </strong>in the course tracking (because SOC 040 <u>has been approved</u> previously). The rest of the fields should be set to <strong>&lt;list&gt;.</strong> Open the <strong>course identification</strong> tab for SOC 040 and look in the <u>Closing Notes</u> field. NOTE: The <u>Closing Notes</u> field will be populated when the new course has been approved. </p> 
        <p>If the renumbered course <strong>has not been approved</strong> yet, then using the <strong>Search/Modify</strong> <strong>Course</strong> tab in CRAMS, you need to leave the <u>Course Number</u> field and course tracking as <strong>&lt;list&gt;</strong>, only populating the <u>Subject Area</u> and the <u>Course Level </u>fields (SOC, undergraduate). You will come up with all courses in the subject area and course levels that are applicable to your search needs. All courses that are “in process” will be highlighted in RED. Selecting the courses in RED and reviewing the Course Identification tab by looking on the <u>Renumbered From</u> field, you will be able to find your course. Then using <a href="../LocationOfCourses/Default.aspx">Location of Courses</a> and <a href="../CourseManagement/SearchForACourse.aspx">Searching for a Course</a>, you will be able to find your courses that are “in process.”</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> How do I find an (E-Z) segment course?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>There are two kinds of (E-Z) segment courses. First, there are (E-Z) segment courses with (E-Z) segments in the course number identification. The course number identification includes: subject area, course number and course suffix. An example of this would be SOC 002J. SOC is the subject area, 002 is the number and J is the suffix.</p> 
        <p>To find this type of course, you would use the <strong><a href="../CourseManagement/SearchForACourse.aspx">Search/Modify Course</a></strong> process, filling in the <u>Subject Area,</u> <u>Course Number</u> and <u>Course Suffix</u>, with all the rest of the fields as <strong>&lt;list&gt;.</strong></p> 
        <p>All courses that are in process will be highlighted in RED.</p> 
        <p>All other courses including approved courses will appear in “white” </p> 
        <p>The second kind of (E-Z) segments (course suffix) are those listed within the Description. An example of this would be SOC 183 which lists the course suffix within the description: “Explores special topics in sociology. H. Aging in America; M. Geographic Information Systems and Mapping in Sociology and other Social Sciences: Principles, Techniques, and Research …”</p> 
        <p>To find this type of (E-Z) segment use the <strong><a href="../CourseManagement/SearchForACourse.aspx">Search/Modify Course</a></strong> process, filling in the <u>Subject Area,</u> <u>Course Number</u> and <u>Course Suffix</u>, with all the rest of the fields as <strong>&lt;list&gt;.</strong></p> 
        <p>All courses that are in process will be highlighted in RED.</p> 
        <p>All courses approved will appear in “white” </p> 
        <p>Review the Description/Grading/Etc. tab to help you in identifying the course you are searching for.</p> 
        <p>If you are unsure of what kind of Umbrella (E-Z) segment you are looking for, use the second kind of search above.</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> What is the process of an exception (late submission) request?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>An exception request is used when the course submission has passed <a href="http://senate.ucr.edu/committee/8/Submission%20Deadlines%20for%202014-15.pdf" target="_blank">the deadline date</a>. &nbsp;&nbsp; </p> 
        <p>Complete the <a href="http://senate.ucr.edu/committee/8/request_for_extension_of_course_proposal_submission_deadline_form.html" target="_blank">exception request</a>.</p> 
        <p>Email the request to the <a href="mailto:melinda.miller@ucr.edu">melinda.miller@ucr.edu</a> and <a href="mailto:beth.beatty@ucr.edu">beth.beatty@ucr.edu</a>.</p> 
        <p>If it is approved, the Committee on Courses chair will email the approved document to the requesting department and cc the course specialist, Committee on Courses analyst and any others listed on the original email.</p> 
        <p>After receiving the email with the approved extension, copy and paste the Word document into the <u>Faculty Correspondence</u> field within the CRAMS course proposal.</p> 
        <p>Lastly, submit the course through the CRAMS process, after you have completed the entire course proposal as required in CRAMS.</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> Is there a shortcut to receiving an approval on a course/or having a course approved?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>No, there is no short cut or EASY button. All course(s) must be submitted through the UCR Course Request and Maintenance System (CRAMS). </p> 
       </div>

     <h3>Cutting Edge Research that Reaches the World</h3> 
     <p> Through <a href="http://or.ucr.edu/">Research and Economic Development</a> and the dedicated work of faculty and student researchers in the pursuit of new knowledge, UC Riverside is fulfilling its role as the research university of Inland Southern California. </p> 
     <p> The university's research heritage, our resources and our location make it an ideal place for exploring air, water, energy, biodiversity, sustainability, land use, the habitat/agriculture interface and much more. </p> 
     <p> What we do and study here makes a difference to people every day: Every orange, lemon, lime and tangerine found on your grocer’s shelf has a genetic connection to the trees at our Citrus Research Collection. Our scientists have earned worldwide recognition for their expertise and breakthrough findings in the areas of plant sciences, environmental and natural resources and pest management. </p> 
     <p> Our recent research innovations include: </p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Engine technology that allows cars to run on a mix of hydrogen and natural gas.</li> 
      <li>New varieties of citrus and asparagus.</li> 
      <li>An efficient and clean method of turning waste materials into a sulfur-free diesel fuel.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <div class="nota-bene"> 
      <h3><em>We’re Making the World a Better Place, One Discovery at a Time.</em></h3> 
      <ul> 
       <li>In 1980, we released our first patented citrus variety, the Oroblanco grapefruit. Other citrus breeding program releases include the Melogold grapefruit and the Gold Nugget mandarin.</li> 
       <li>Plant physiologist Charles Coggins registered gibberellic acid for use in California citrus groves to delay fruit maturation. The ultimate result of his work was the extension of the citrus-growing season in California from four months to nine. 
        <div class="figure"> 
         <img src="/research/images/main_images/research_citrus.jpg" alt="Picture of Citrus Fruit" width="413" height="134" /> 
         <p>For over 100 years, UCR has been instrumental in the improvement of citrus production.</p> 
        </div> </li> 
       <li>In 1967, biochemist and molecular biologist Anthony Norman was one of two U.S. scientists working independently who isolated and described the chemical nature of a substance the public knew as vitamin D, but was, in fact, a steroid hormone. Several life-saving drug treatments were developed as a result of research by the Norman lab. </li> 
       <li>William Frankenberger, professor of soil microbiology and biochemistry, developed patented technologies using fungi and other microbial agents that naturally detoxify water contaminated with selenium, perchlorate, and nitrate.</li> 
       <li>Our high-energy physicists were part of an international effort that provided the world with evidence of the top quark — a fundamental particle which was first theorized about in the 1960s but never before observed.</li> 
       <li>Chemist Guy Bertrand and his research team were the first to create a stable singlet diradical, a development announced worldwide. The unique compound could lead to a new generation of non-metallic magnetic devices for medical imaging or electronics.</li> 
       <li>Plant pathologist Noel Keen and biochemist Fran Jurnak identified a previously unknown protein structure, the parallel beta-helix as part of Keen’s pioneering work in plant pathology. </li> 
       <li>Plant geneticist Michael Clegg led a research group that was the first to recover and decode ancient plant DNA genetic material from a magnolia leaf 20 million years old. </li> 
       <li>Geophysicist Harry Green II and graduate student Pamela Burnley provided the first explanation for “deep-focus earthquakes,” which occur 300 kilometers or more beneath the earth’s surface.</li> 
      </ul>

      <p>Multimedia Technologies Group provides and maintains presentation technology in support of instruction and special events. We drafted and implemented the Classroom Technology Plan, a commitment to classroom technology renewal and replacement. We have equipped 100% of UCR's general assignment classrooms with LCD data/video projectors, VHS VCRs, DVD players, computer interface and network connections. Lecture halls were also equipped with wireless microphones, PC computers and document cameras. Our commitment to instructional technology, has led us to design and implement &quot;smarter&quot; classrooms, such as the <a href="http://flex.ucr.edu">Flex Rooms</a> and the <a href="http://hyperstruction.ucr.edu">Hyperstruction Studio</a>.</p> 
      <p>Multimedia Services also delivers and operates presentation equipment in non-equipped spaces. With our videoconferencing technology, you can hold seminars, classes, or meetings with any UC campus or other sites worldwide. Our staff is available for consultation on classroom design issues or the planning of special events.</p> 
      <h3>Mission Statement</h3> 
      <p>As UCR's main presentation technology provider, we at Multimedia Technologies Group support the university's teaching mission by enhancing the quality and effectiveness of instructional delivery methods. With state-of-the-art media systems, we provide a means to achieve greater impact in the classroom, from idea inception to final solution.</p>

Equipment is available to departments and registered campus organizations.</a></li> 
       <li><a href="http://iclassroom.ucr.edu"><img src="/images/classrooms.jpg" alt="Class Tech" width="131" height="68" /><strong>Classroom Technology </strong>UCR has fifty general assignment classrooms that are state-of-the-art teaching facilities. Classroom Technology provides faculty with the multimedia tools for all their instructional needs -- Smart Classrooms are equipped with a standard array of presentation equipment.</a></li> 
       <li><a href="http://classrooms.ucr.edu/"><img src="/images/classrooms-2.jpg" alt="Classrooms" width="131" height="68" /><strong>Smart Classrooms</strong>You can locate any general assignment &quot;smart classroom&quot;, view floorplans and photographs of actual rooms, review the technology available in each room, and obtain instructions for equipment operation.</a></li>
