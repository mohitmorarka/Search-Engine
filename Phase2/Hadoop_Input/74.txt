        <p>The Office of Resource Planning and Budget endeavors to provide objective, accurate, and timely information and analysis to every level of campus leadership, thereby supporting the effective management of all campus resources. The responsibilities of this office include:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li>developing and implementing policies and procedures related to the management of campus financial resources&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </li> 
         <li>administering the annual campus-wide budget process</li> 
         <li>managing the campus budget, the staffing system, and unallocated resources </li> 
         <li>allocating campus-level funding&nbsp;</li> 
         <li>administering the annual Auxiliary and Self-Supporting Enterprise budget and rate review process</li> 
         <li>monitoring campus debt ratios and reviewing capital budget appropriation requests</li> 
         <li>acting as a liaison with the Office of the President and others </li> 
        </ul>

        <p> It is essential for each operating unit on campus to engage in meaningful resource planning and financial management in order to perform its role in the University’s mission of teaching, research, and public service.</p> 
        <p> It is the responsibility of each unit head to ensure that resource planning and financial management occurs in an effective manner at all organizational levels.</p> 
        <h3> I.&nbsp;Resource Planning and Budget Development</h3> 
        <p> Resource planning is the process through which the unit’s strategic plan is articulated into resource terms. This process ultimately leads to the development of the budget. All resource planning and budget development should include the following components:</p> 
        <ol> 
         <li> <h5> A plan with clearly defined goals and objectives</h5> The plan should be easily understood, with attainable goals and measurable objectives. The goals and objectives should be specific enough to be integrated into the overall planning and budget process.</li> 
         <li> <h5> A process for identifying and evaluating key factors required to accomplish the plan</h5> <p> The evaluation, selection, and ultimate usage of key factors should be based on a clear understanding of the nature of the key factor and the impact it has in the achievement of the goals and objectives. Key factors may vary depending upon each organization’s unique mission. Examples of key factors are:</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li> Student Enrollment</li> 
           <li> Teaching workload (including faculty FTE, instructional assistants)</li> 
           <li> Salaries and benefits (including FTE)</li> 
           <li> Equipment and Supplies</li> 
           <li> Technology</li> 
           <li> Support and auxiliary services</li> 
           <li> Space needs and related costs</li> 
           <li> Anticipated revenue</li> 
           <li> External regulations</li> 
           <li> The Market</li> 
          </ul> </li> 
         <li> <h5> A budget development process that includes a thorough analysis of all relevant data and supports management decision-making</h5> This process should ensure consistent use of proven methods for gathering and analyzing data, as well as compliance with relevant budget and financial policies. The analysis should include the following, as appropriate:<br /> 
          <ul> 
           <li> A thorough re-evaluation of all assumptions, analyses, plans and budgets used in the previous year's planning process</li> 
           <li> An examination of budget and financial performance from prior years</li> 
           <li> An evaluation of current year's performance against both the budget and the goals and objectives</li> 
           <li> An identification of the modifications required in the new plan to reflect changes in the goals and objectives</li> 
           <li> An evaluation of cost and risk factors</li> 
          </ul> </li> 
         <li> <h5> A budget that articulates the goals and objectives in resource terms</h5> The budget should be realistic, reasonable, and attainable and should be accompanied by a descriptive narrative. Components of the budget and narrative should include:<br /> 
          <ul> 
           <li> All funding sources</li> 
           <li> Revenue estimates</li> 
           <li> Major expenditures by category, including identification of indirect expenditures</li> 
           <li> A description clearly supporting the resource requirements</li> 
           <li> Explanation of major assumptions and forecasting methods used</li> 
           <li> Identification of significant changes in current operation</li> 
           <li> Contingency plans</li> 
          </ul> </li> 
        </ol> 
        <h3> II. Financial Management</h3> 
        <p> Financial management is the process of ensuring that the unit's financial performance is consistent with the utilization of resources as delineated in the budget developed to accomplish the objectives of the plan. It is assumed that data integrity has been assured throughout. Financial management should be supported through the following activities:</p> 
        <h5> Review and Analysis of Financial Performance</h5> 
        <p> At a minimum, financial reports should be prepared by the end of the subsequent period ensuring adequate time to make adjustments or corrections. These reports should:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li> Compare budget to actual financial performance</li> 
         <li> Include all sources of revenue and expense</li> 
         <li> Provide projections of year-end balances</li> 
         <li> Identify any other problems or exceptions</li> 
        </ul> 
        <p> Analysis should be conducted to achieve the following objectives:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li> Ensure that expenditures are reasonable and necessary to accomplish the unit's goals</li> 
         <li> Ensure that resources are being utilized efficiently</li> 
         <li> Measure and understand financial performance</li> 
         <li> Determine whether sufficient resources exist for continued operation</li> 
         <li> Clearly identify trends and special areas of concern</li> 
         <li> Detect changes in circumstances or the business environment</li> 
        </ul> 
        <h5> Corrective Actions</h5> 
        <p> Corrective action should be taken in a timely manner, as appropriate, in response to the review and analysis of financial performance. Examples of specific corrective actions are:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li> Revising plans or budgets to reflect changed circumstances</li> 
         <li> Changing or eliminating activities</li> 
         <li> Obtaining additional funding</li> 
         <li> Modifying goals or objectives</li> 
         <li> Altering future budget</li> 
        </ul>

    <p class="p1">With the release of his new movie, &quot;Muppets Most Wanted,&quot; RICKY GERVAIS is running scared, and right into Ellen's hot seat! The lively comedian has previously gotten together with the Sesame Street gang, he's spent a tumultuous &quot;Night at the Museum,&quot; and made copy machines a water-cooler topic on the British edition of &quot;The Office.&quot; It seems about right that Ricky would use his playful wit to lead Kermit, Miss Piggy &amp; Co. in an international crime spoof. Last time he was in the building, he flashed some major skin and his gilded Ellen boxers! This time, keep your eyes peeled... Ricky is most unpredictable.</p> 
    <p class="p1">Then, JOE MANGANIELLO is here! The handsome &quot;True Blood&quot; star will take on the role of action hero in his new movie &quot;Sabotage&quot; -- and more action from Joe is a good thing! The dynamic actor can play nearly every part in the book: werewolf, stripper, police officer, and hot guy, just to name a few. He can also play a mean game of Pictionary! See how Ellen tests Joe's skills when he joins her again to chat!</p> 
    <p class="p1">Plus, two R&amp;B legends take the stage as a duo! TONI BRAXTON &amp; BABYFACE unite to perform a song from their new CD, &quot;Love, Marriage &amp; Divorce,&quot; and it's going to be twice as much greatness as usual. As solo artists, they have a combined total of 18 albums and 16 Grammys, and too many hits to list. Together, the volume gets louder and more beautiful. They will open their hearts and voices to Ellen!</p> 
    <p class="p1">On Tuesday, Ellen's playing with your emotions!<br /><br /></p>

        <h3>Welcome to Enrollment Management Systems</h3> 
        <p>A department of Student Affairs, EMS provides quality programming and system support to help Enrollment Management recruit students, administer financial aid, and admit, enroll and retain an academically excellent and diverse student population.</p> 
        <p>A liaison to campus and divisional technical groups, EMS helps make informed decisions and meet strategic goals for:</p> 
        <ul class="list-style_double-arrow"> 
         <li><a title="Financial Aid" href="http://finaid.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx" target="_blank"><u>Financial Aid</u></a></li> 
         <li><a title="" href="http://registrar.ucr.edu/registrar/default.aspx" target="_blank">Registrar's Office</a></li> 
         <li><a title="Undergraduate Admissions" href="http://admissions.ucr.edu" target="_blank"><u>Undergraduate Admissions</u></a></li> 
        </ul> 
        <p>We also grant access to the <a title="" href="SIS.aspx">Student Information System (SIS)</a> and generate data and reports from SIS. </p> 
        <h5>Use this website to:</h5> 
        <ul class="list-style_double-arrow"> 
         <li>Request access to <a href="SIS.aspx">SIS</a>. This system allows you to manage the student record and perform administrative tasks, such as enrolling a student in a class, accepting payment from a student, and scheduling classrooms.</li> 
         <li>Request <a href="../DataRequests/Pages/default.aspx">student data</a>, such as educational reports, student mailing lists, etc.</li> 
         <li>Take <a href="http://registrar.ucr.edu/registrar/privacy-ferpa/default.aspx">Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) training</a>, which is required to access SIS.</li> 
        </ul>

     The University of California, Riverside (
    <a href="http://www.ucr.edu/">www.ucr.edu</a>) is a doctoral research university, a living laboratory for groundbreaking exploration of issues critical to Inland Southern California, the state and communities around the world. Reflecting California's diverse culture, UCR's enrollment has exceeded 21,000 students. The campus opened a medical school in 2013 and has reached the heart of the Coachella Valley by way of the UCR Palm Desert Center. The campus has an annual statewide economic impact of more than $1 billion. A broadcast studio with fiber cable to the AT&amp;T Hollywood hub is available for live or taped interviews.
