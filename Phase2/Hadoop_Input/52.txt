     <h3>Policy</h3> 
     <p> The University of California, Riverside is committed to ensuring the privacy and accuracy of your confidential information to the extent possible, subject to provisions of state and federal law. Other than as required by laws that guarantee public access to certain types of information, or in response to subpoenas or other legal instruments that authorize access, personally-identifiable information is not actively shared. In particular, we do not re-distribute or sell personal information collected on our web servers. </p> 
     <h3>Information Collected</h3> 
     <p> UCR websites may collect personal information such as name, address, e-mail address, telephone number(s), and/or educational interests. Such personal information may be requested by us for research, public service or teaching programs, or for administrative purposes. Additional personal information, such as credit card account information, may be requested for purchases or enrollment purposes. </p> 
     <p> Web servers typically collect, at least temporarily, the following information: Internet Protocol (IP) address of computer being used; web pages requested; referring web page; browser used; date and time. UCR may collect statistics identifying particular IP addresses from which our websites are accessed. </p> 
     <h3>Use of Collected Information</h3> 
     <p> UCR may use personal information collected from websites for the purpose of future communication back to online enrollees, in order to keep you informed of such activities as campus programs, symposia and/or special events, but only if you are provided the opportunity to opt out of that type of use. </p> 
     <p> UCR may use browser-IP-address information and anonymous-browser history to report information about site accesses and for statistical purposes. This information is generally used to improve Web presentation and utilization. The campus also may use IP address information for troubleshooting purposes. </p> 
     <p> Some UCR online activity sites may use &quot;cookies&quot; in order to deliver web content specific to individual users' interests or to keep track of online purchasing transactions. Sensitive personal information is not stored within cookies. </p> 
     <h3>Distribution of Collected Information</h3> 
     <p> UCR will not disclose, without your consent, personal information collected about you, except for certain explicit circumstances in which disclosure is required by law. </p> 
     <p> UCR will not distribute or sell personal information to third-party organizations. </p> 
     <h3>Individual Choice</h3> 
     <p> Individuals who wish to use methods other than online enrollment may submit requests by e-mail or U.S. mail addressed to the UC Riverside organization responsible for the website. </p> 
     <h3>Access to Your Own Information</h3> 
     <p> Questions regarding users' rights to review, modify or delete their previously provided personal information should be directed to the campus organization to which they provided the information. Any disputes will be resolved under existing records regulations applicable to UCR. </p> 
     <h3>Main Campus Site</h3> 
     <p> The main campus site (i.e. www.ucr.edu) in addition to general logs, also collects usage information the links on its pages. This information is only used in an aggregate form to determine how resources are used. </p>

     <h2 class="intro"> Geniuses Wanted</h2> 
     <h3 class="verd intro"> From research to law to pre-med, see how UC Riverside<br /> springboards the ambitious.</h3> 
     <div class="genericBody version2 bleed"> 
      <div class="grid_4 alpha"> 
       <img alt="Double helix" class="centerImg" width="173" height="221" src="/Images/DNA_honors.jpg" /> 
       <h3> Take a Pre-Med Path</h3> 
       <p><a href="http://medschool.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">The School of Medicine at UCR </a>will enroll its first four-year class of 50 medical students in August 2013.&nbsp;Within the school, the <a href="http://medschool.ucr.edu/admissions/haider_program.html">Thomas Haider Program at the UCR School of Medicine</a> will provide a unique pathway to medicine exclusively for UCR students. Through the program, up to 24 of the seats will be filled by students who attend UCR for at least six consecutive quarters and complete their bachelor’s degree at UCR. <br /> <br /> UCR also has several pre-med pipeline programs designed with your success in mind. </p> 
       <ul type="disc"> 
        <li>If you come from a disadvantaged background and are interested in a career in science and/or medicine, <a href="http://faststart.ucr.edu/">FastStart</a> can help you start college strong.</li> 
        <li>Enjoy academic support, research and career development with <a href="http://msp.ucr.edu/">The Medical Scholars Program</a>, a community of students working toward careers in the health sciences.</li> 
        <li>Find support with <a href="http://hpac.ucr.edu/">The Health Professions Advising Center</a>, a resource for those pursuing careers in the health industry. </li> 
        <li>Join <a href="http://fpl.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Future Physician Leaders</a>, a mentorship program for pre-med students interested in serving the community as physician leaders in the Coachella Valley and Riverside-San Bernardino. Highlights include lectures, workshops and a six-week summer shadowing program.</li> 
       </ul> 
       <p><img alt="Bunsen burner" class="centerImg" width="130" height="194" src="/Images/flask_honors.jpg" /> </p> 
      </div> 
      <div class="grid_4"> 
       <p> UCR nurtures brilliance. Fast track to medical school, conduct innovative research and live in residence halls with students as dedicated as you are. If your goals are sky-high, UCR is your school. </p> 
       <h3> Prepare for Law School</h3> 
       <p> UC Riverside offers pre-law advising, support services and a broad range of majors perfect for those on a pre-law track.</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li>Combine your major with UCR’s unique <a href="http://lawandsociety.ucr.edu/">Law and Society cooperative major</a> for an even more comprehensive pre-law foundation. </li> 
        <li>Apply for <a href="https://advancementservices.ucr.edu/Scholarship/ScholarshipFundInfo.aspx?fund=600096"> The Ernest Lopez Award</a> and earn a cash award for law school.</li> 
        <li>Intern at <a href="http://uccs.ucdavis.edu/">The UC Center at Sacramento</a> and get hands-on experience working with California’s top policy makers. </li> 
        <li>Intern at the <a href="http://www.ucdc.edu/">UC Washington Center</a> and see first-hand how the law comes to life. </li> 
       </ul> 
       <img alt="Law books and gavel" class="centerImg" width="182" height="123" src="/Images/books_honors.jpg" /> 
       <h3> Conduct Research as an Undergraduate</h3> 
       <p> As a cutting-edge research university, UCR offers you an opportunity some other major universities won’t: the chance to conduct research as an undergraduate. Study mathematical billiards like <a href="http://www.ucr.edu/research/undergrads.html"> Robert Neimeyer</a>, or stage an original play with a faculty mentor. (Not all of our research happens in a lab.)</p> 
       <p> Programs across all disciplines connect you with faculty mentors, host several symposia to showcase your accomplishments and publish the year’s best work in an undergraduate research journal. </p> 
       <p> <a href="http://www.ucr.edu/research/undergrads.html">Learn more about undergraduate research at UCR</a>. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div class="grid_4 omega"> 
       <img alt="Scientists" width="215" height="307" src="/Images/scientists_honors.jpg" /> 
       <h3 style="margin-top: 20px" ;=""> Join a Stimulating Learning Community</h3> 
       <p> If you are naturally curious and value intellectual challenge, you should apply for <a href="http://honors.ucr.edu/">University Honors</a>. If accepted, you will: </p> 
       <ul> 
        <li>Work closely with talented professors in small classroom settings </li> 
        <li>Thrive through exciting seminars, experiential learning and a culminating thesis. </li> 
        <li>Prepare for prestigious scholarships and awards. </li> 
        <li>Live in the Honors Hall surrounded by students as driven as you. </li> 
        <li>Graduate with a distinction desired by graduate programs and prospective employers. <br /> </li> 
       </ul> 
       <h4><br /> University Honors Admissions</h4> 
       <p> Most enter as freshmen, but there are later entry points. Admitted students typically have superior grade point averages, a strong application essay, exceptional leadership qualities, unusual life experiences and/or significant community involvement. For application deadlines and more information, visit <a href="http://honors.ucr.edu/">University Honors.</a> <br /> <br /> </p> 
       <p><img alt="Two students studying." width="210" height="187" src="/Images/study__honors.jpg" /> </p> 
      </div>

      <h2>UCR Publications<br /> <strong>for the iPad</strong></h2> 
      <h3>Now you can read UCR Publications anywhere, anytime, from your iPad.</h3> 
      <p>UC Riverside has begun adding its popular publications, such as Living the Promise and UCR Magazine, to the Apple iBookstore. This guide will show how you can download and view these publications on your device.&nbsp;</p> 
      <p><a class="l_ibookstore_button" href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/university-california-riverside/id532213447?mt=11"><span class="accessibility">Available on the iBookstore</span></a></p> 
      <div class="clear"></div>
     </div> 
     <h3 class="l_ipad_header"><span class="text_blue">How</span> <span class="text_red">to navigate</span><br /> <span class="text_grey">the magazine</span></h3> 
     <div class="glider" id="my-glider"> 
      <div class="scroller"> 
       <div class="content"> 
        <div class="slide" id="slide1"> 
         <img class="img_R" src="/images/design6/slider_step_01.png" alt="Section Menu Thumbnail View" width="506" height="415" /> 
         <h4>How to Navigate Your Magazine</h4> 
         <p>When you open the magazine for the first time, it will bring you to the <strong>Section Menu Thumbnail View</strong>. From here you can swipe to see other sections, or tap on a page to start.</p> 
         <h4>Swipe Between Sections</h4> 
         <p>At the bottom of the screen there is a bar with white dots that represent all the available sections for the magazine. The dot with an outside box is the current section.</p> 
         <p>Swipe sideways left/right or right/left to navigate through all sections.</p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="slide" id="slide2"> 
         <img class="img_R" src="/images/design6/slider_step_02.png" alt="Section Menu Thumbnail View" width="506" height="415" /> 
         <h4>Scroll Thumbnails</h4> 
         <p>While in the Sections Navigation, you'll see thumbnail previews of all the pages at the bottom of the screen. Just tap one to open that page.</p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="slide" id="slide3"> 
         <img class="img_R" src="/images/design6/slider_step_03.png" alt="Section Menu Thumbnail View" width="506" height="415" /> 
         <h4>Swipe to Read a Story</h4> 
         <p>Read stories by swiping right/left.</p> 
         <h4>Going Back to Thumbnail View</h4> 
         <p>To go back to the thumbnail navigation, pinch with two fingers anywhere on your screen. The page will minimize and go back to the bottom of the screen. Now you'll have access to all the pages from that section.</p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="slide" id="slide4"> 
         <img class="img_R" src="/images/design6/slider_step_04.png" alt="Section Menu Thumbnail View" width="506" height="415" /> 
         <h4>Tap to Show iPad Top Navigation</h4> 
         <p>Tap anywhere on the screen to quickly access the iPad top navigation. From here you can go back to your library, see the table of contents, and add your notes.</p> 
         <h4>Add a Bookmark</h4> 
         <p>To the right of the navigation bar, you can find the bookmark icon. Click the icon to bookmark the page. To see all your bookmarks, just click again on the icon and a pop down list will appear.</p> 
         <p>To make the navigations tool dissapear, tap anywhere on the screen.</p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="slide" id="slide5"> 
         <img class="img_R" src="/images/design6/slider_step_05.png" alt="Section Menu Thumbnail View" width="506" height="415" /> 
         <h4>Read the Magazine in Portrait Mode</h4> 
         <p>You can read the magazine in portrait mode just by turning your iPad.</p> 
         <p>Your book's appearance changes based on the orientation. In portrait orientation, some graphics do not appear, or they might appear on the side rather than within the text flow.</p> 
         <p>To read the articles swipe up and down.</p> 
         <p>Pinch anywhere on the screen to open theTable of Contents.</p> 
        </div>

      <p class="deck">Five UC Riverside researchers are part of $40 million project to develop materials and structures to enable more energy efficient computers and cell phones</p> 
      <p class="byline">By <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/author/snealon" title="View all posts by Sean Nealon" rel="author">Sean Nealon</a> on <em>February 4, 2013</em></p>

      <p>RIVERSIDE, Calif. (<a href="http://www.ucr.edu">www.ucr.edu</a>) — Five University of California, Riverside professors will receive a total of $5 million as part of a $35 million research center aimed at developing materials and structures that could enable more energy efficient computers, mobile phones, and other electronic devices.</p> 
      <p>The research center, which will be called the <a href="http://www.src.org/program/starnet/fame/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.src.org/program/starnet/fame/']);">Center for Function Accelerated nanoMaterial Engineering (FAME)</a>, will be located at UCLA and led by Jane P. Chang, a professor of chemical and biomolecular engineering at UCLA.</p> 
      <p>Four professors from UC Riverside’s <a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.engr.ucr.edu/']);">Bourns College of Engineering </a>are part of the center:<a href="http://ndl.ee.ucr.edu/balandinw.htm" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://ndl.ee.ucr.edu/balandinw.htm']);"> Alexander A. Balandin</a>, Alexander Khitun, <a href="http://www.ee.ucr.edu/~jianlin/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.ee.ucr.edu/~jianlin/']);">Jianlin Liu </a>and <a href="http://www.ee.ucr.edu/~rlake/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.ee.ucr.edu/~rlake/']);">Roger Lake</a>, all of whom are part of the electrical engineering department and materials science and engineering program. <a href="http://promise.ucr.edu/profile-technology-lau.html">Jeanie Lau</a>, a professor of physics and astronomy who is also part of the materials science and engineering program, is the fifth professor. Each professor will receive about $1 million.</p> 
      <p>FAME is <a href="http://www.src.org/newsroom/2013/press-release/starnet-launch/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.src.org/newsroom/2013/press-release/starnet-launch/']);">one of six new university microelectronics research centers recently established</a> with $194 million over the next five years from the <a href="http://www.src.org/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.src.org/']);">Semiconductor Research Corporation (SRC)</a> and the <a href="http://www.darpa.mil/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.darpa.mil/']);">Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA)</a>. The funding supports the continued growth and leadership of the U.S. semiconductor industry.</p> 
      <p>The other five centers will be located at UC Berkeley, University of Michigan, University of Notre Dame, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and University of Minnesota.</p> 
      <p>The University of Minnesota center is called the <a href="http://www.src.org/program/starnet/c-spin/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.src.org/program/starnet/c-spin/']);">Center for Spintronic Materials, Interfaces and Novel Architectures (C-SPIN)</a>. Three UC Riverside researchers – <a href="http://physics.ucr.edu/People/Home/roland_kawakami_.html">Roland Kawakami</a>, <a href="http://research.chem.ucr.edu/groups/bartels/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://research.chem.ucr.edu/groups/bartels/']);">Ludwig Bartels</a> and <a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/~cengizlab/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.engr.ucr.edu/~cengizlab/']);">Cengiz Ozkan</a> – received a total of $3 million as part of that center.</p> 
      <p>The goal of the FAME center is to create and investigate new nonconventional atomic scale engineered materials and structures of multi-function oxides, metals and semiconductors to accelerate innovations in analog, logic and memory devices for the semiconductor and defense industries.</p> 
      <p>The center includes 35 faculty researchers from 16 universities: UCLA, Columbia, Cornell, UC Berkeley, MIT, UC Santa Barbara, Stanford, UC Irvine, Purdue, Rice, UC Riverside, North Carolina State, Caltech, Penn, West Virginia and Yale.</p> 
      <p>Balandin, Lau and Liu will focus on van der Waals materials – a broad range of crystalline solids with layer structures. The van der Waals materials include graphene, topological insulators and charge-density wave materials. It is expected that this class of materials can be used in future information processing.</p> 
      <p>Balandin’s project is “Phonon Engineering and Energy Conversion in van der Waals Materials.” Liu will lead research on “Van der Waals Molecular Beam Epitaxy Growth of Two-Dimensional Layered Material Systems.” Lau’s project is “Quantum Engineered Collective States in van der Waals Materials.”</p> 
      <p>Lake will lead one of the four thrusts of the center: physics, mechanisms, and device prototyping. His project is “Switching by Voltage Control of the Quantum Phase in Misoriented Layers of van der Waals Materials.” Khitun’s project is “Magnonic Holographic Memory for Information Storage and Data Processing.”</p>
