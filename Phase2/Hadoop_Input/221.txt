     <h1 align="center">2012 FORREST S. MOSTEN LECTURE </h1> 
     <p align="center"><strong>You are cordially invited to attend the 2012 Forrest S. Mosten Lecture and Reception<br /> on<br /> Thursday, April 26, 2012<br /> 6:00 p.m.<br /> Barbara and Art Culver Center of the Arts</strong></p> 
     <h2 align="center">Reconciliation in Private and Political Life: A Conversation</h2> 
     <p align="center">How do we repair damaged relationships on either personal or political levels? How can we let go of anger and resentment to reconcile with those who have hurt us? Must we let go of anger and resentment to reconcile? Should we reconcile? Do the answers to these questions differ on the personal and the political level? What, for instance is the legacy of the South African Truth and Reconciliation Commission? How can individuals, groups and societies best make up for the injustices of their pasts?</p> 
     <p align="center">Please join us as Professor Emerita of Religious Studies, June O'Connor and Associate Professor of Political Science, Bronwyn Leebaw explore these and related questions.<br /> Moderated by Professor Georgia Warnke, Director, Center for Ideas and Society. </p> 
     <p align="center">This event is sponsored by the Center for Ideas and Society through a generous gift by Forrest and Jodi Mosten.<br /> <br /> <strong>We hope you will be able to join us! Please click below to RSVP.</strong></p> 
     <p align="center"><strong><a href="http://events.r20.constantcontact.com/register/event?llr=gttk67iab&amp;oeidk=a07e5pgwweuec6566a5&amp;oseq=" target="_blank">Get more information</a></strong></p> 
     <p align="center"><strong><a href="https://events.r20.constantcontact.com/register/eventReg?llr=gttk67iab&amp;oeidk=a07e5pgwweuec6566a5&amp;oseq=" target="_blank">Register Now!</a></strong></p>

      <h3>UCR's Resource for Employees to Gain Access to Enterprise Systems</h3> 
      <p>UCR has many computer systems available to Faculty and Staff to assist them in conducting campus business. The computer systems range from those that can be accessed via the web or those that require software to be installed on your computer.<br /> Access for Enterprise systems are granted one of two methods:</p> 
      <ol> 
       <li>EACS (Enterprise Access Control System), via SAA’s (System Access Administrators), or</li> 
       <li>SAA approval via paper form.</li> 
      </ol> 
      <h5>UCR Computing and Communications Authentication Methodology</h5> 
      <p>C&amp;C has embarked on an aggressive program to provide a single-sign on portal environment for UCR Enterprise Systems.&nbsp; The major of enterprise systems now utilize the single-sign on portal environment, which enables access to be granted to employees, using their UCR NetID, by their units SAA.&nbsp; However, some key enterprise systems still require paper approval.&nbsp; These legacy systems are:&nbsp; UCOP PPS, SIS (Student Information System), Storehouse, Equipment Management/Inventory, and Cashiering.</p> 
      <p>For<a href="/passwords/changepass.html" target="_blank"> information how to change your password</a>.</p> 
      <p>To determine which systems you may need access to, select one of the options to the left:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>All Systems</li> 
       <li>Staff Systems (Further broken down by classification: Analyst/AA, CFAO, MSO, or SAO.)</li> 
       <li>Faculty Systems</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p>The categories of systems represent suggested applications for each type of employee. Please refer to the listing of all systems for a complete listing. Depending on your selection, all systems that are in general used by Staff or Faculty, or All systems, are identified with a brief summary of the system uses. If you are interested in obtaining access to a system, click on the system name and follow the step-by-step instructions. All required forms are available for printing directly from this site to assist you.</p> 
      <p>To further assist you in obtaining access to the appropriate systems, we have provided an Application Responsibility Matrix. This matrix summarizes the application, application description, potential users, responsible unit, expected turnaround, and the escalation procedures.</p> 
      <p>As a final note, please keep in mind that several systems require a two step process to obtain access. For example, access to SIS+ (Student Information System) required Computing and Communications to create an IBM account and the privileges are assigned by the Registrar's Office.</p>

     <p>Allison entered the PhD program in Entomology in 2013, but her ties to the Hoddle lab stretch back much farther. While an undergraduate, she worked in Dr. Paul de Ley's lab (UCR Department of Nematology) researching marine and freshwater nematode ecology. After receiving her bachelor's degree in 2008, she left the De Ley lab and was hired by the Hoddle lab to assist on glassy-winged sharpshooter (GWSS) research being conducted in organic vineyards. After the GWSS project came to an end, Allison was transferred to a new project focusing on the Asian citrus psyllid (ACP), <em>Diaphorena citri</em>. She has been working with ACP and its associated natural enemies from Pakistan (<em>Tamarixia radiata</em> (Hymenoptera: Eulophidae) and <em>Diaphorencyrtus aligarhensis</em> (Hymneoptera: Encyrtidae)) for the past 3 years, and is continuing this research as she pursues her PhD.</p> 
     <p>Currently, the majority of her time is spent in isolation in UCR's Insectary and Quarantine facility completing safety testing of <em>D. aligarhensis</em>, as well as conducting life table studies to describe the biology of several species of hyperparasitoids of <em>T. radiata</em> and <em>D. aligarhensis</em> from Pakistan. The ultimate goal of her research is to get <em>D. aligarhensis</em> approved for release as a biological control agent of ACP, in addition to the previously introduced <em>T. radiata</em>. She also plans to track genetic markers within each <em>D. aligarhensis</em> population to determine if one genotype is better suited as a biocontrol agent in southern California than others, and determine if there is any drift toward speciation of populations across various habitats.</p>

     <p>Aside from its many other distinctions, the UCR School of Business Administration (SoBA) is proud to be an AACSB-accredited institution. This distinction places the business school in a league above most others. Below are some facts about AACSB accreditation:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Only five percent of business schools in the world are AACSB-accredited.</li> 
      <li>Twenty percent of companies recruit only from AACSB-accredited business degree programs.</li> 
      <li>Graduates from AACSB-accredited programs receive higher and more competitive salaries.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>In keeping with the AACSB imprint of excellence, SoBA centers its efforts around two central themes:</p> 
     <ol> 
      <li><strong>Innovation, Entrepreneurship and Economic Growth</strong></li> 
      <li><strong>Leadership, Strategic Thinking and Personal Growth</strong></li> 
     </ol> 
     <p>The first theme is outward-looking (fostering growth that benefits the economy) while the second theme is inward-looking (fostering growth on an individual level). For more information, read the SoBA <a href="/pdf/AGSMStrategicPlan2008-2013Final.pdf" target="_blank">strategic plan</a>.</p>
