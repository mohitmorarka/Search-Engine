     <div class="span-11"> 
      <h3>What is &quot;Residency for Tuition and Fee Purposes&quot;?</h3> 
      <p>Your residency status (whether you are a California resident or not) determines whether your tuition and fees will be charged at a California-resident or nonresident rate. Our campus residence affairs officer determines that status based on information you provide in your Statement of Legal Residence. Access to the Statement of Legal Residence is provided through <a href="http://my.ucr.edu">MyUCR</a> to all students who have completed the Statement of Intent to Register (SIR).</p> 
      <span class="warning">Important</span>: 
      <ul class="orange-bullets"> 
       <li><a href="http://www.ucop.edu/general-counsel/guidance/educational-affairs-and-campus-services/residence-tuition-fees-student-info.html" target="_blank">The UC Office of the President website</a> provides a useful summary of residency procedures, requirements, policies and appeal information.</li> 
       <li>If the outcome of any of these processes is a nonresident status, supplemental tution must be paid. This includes failure on your part to complete the process by the deadline.</li> 
       <li>Education Code sections and UC Regents' regulations are available at <a href="/registrar/help-desk/about-us.aspx" class="noUnderline">our office</a>.</li> 
       <li style="padding-bottom: 10px">Residency requirements are subject to change.</li> 
      </ul> 
      <div class="accordion" style="margin-top: 32px;"> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> Am I a resident?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p> There are four residence requirements you must meet in order to qualify for California resident status. <a href="http://www.ucop.edu/general-counsel/guidance/educational-affairs-and-campus-services/residence-tuition-fees-student-info.html#residence-requirements" target="_blank">See them here.</a> </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> What is the Statement of Legal Residence (SLR)?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p> The SLR is how you declare your residency status when you first come to UCR (as a freshman, transfer or graduate student) or when you return after a significant break (longer than three quarters). You submit it after your Statement of Intent to Register (SIR). Additional documentation may be required to prove your status. </p> 
        <h4>How to complete the SLR:</h4> 
        <ul> 
         <li>Freshmen and transfer students who have submitted their Statement of Intent to Register can complete the SLR in <a href="http://my.ucr.edu">MyUCR</a>. Graduate students will receive a packet from <a href="http://graduate.ucr.edu/grad_admissions.html" target="_blank">Graduate Admissions</a>.</li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> I want to establish residency, how do I do that?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>You should aim for meeting the four residence requirements <a href="http://www.ucop.edu/general-counsel/guidance/educational-affairs-and-campus-services/residence-tuition-fees-student-info.html#residence-requirements" target="_blank">outlined here</a>. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> My residency status changed, what do I do?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p> If your residency status changed, <strong>you must immediately notify </strong>the campus residence affairs officer (<a href="mailto:Kendra.McCann@ucr.edu" target="_blank">Kendra.McCann@ucr.edu</a>). </p> 
        <p> <strong>Petition for Resident Classification</strong> <br /> If you want to be classified as a resident, you must petition in person at our office. All status changes must be initiated before the first day of classes for the applicable term. <a href="http://www.ucop.edu/general-counsel/guidance/educational-affairs-and-campus-services/residence-tuition-fees-student-info.html#residence-requirements" target="_blank">See the requirements for resident status</a>. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> I was classified incorrectly, what do I do?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p> If your residency status was classified incorrectly, <strong>you must immediately notify</strong> the campus residence affairs officer (<a href="mailto:Kendra.McCann@ucr.edu" target="_blank">Kendra.McCann@ucr.edu</a>). If you were incorrectly classified as a resident, you are subject to nonresident tuition. If you concealed or gave false information, you may be subject to university discipline. </p> 
        <p> <strong>Petition for Resident Classification</strong> <br /> If you want to be classified as a resident, you must petition in person at our office. All status changes must be initiated before the first day of classes for the applicable term. <a href="http://www.ucop.edu/general-counsel/guidance/educational-affairs-and-campus-services/residence-tuition-fees-student-info.html#residence-requirements" target="_blank">See the requirements for resident status</a>. </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> My resident status was denied, can I appeal?</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p>Students denied resident status might have the right to appeal. Appeals must be filed within 30 days of notification of the campus residence affairs officer’s decision.</p> 
        <p> <a href="http://www.ucop.edu/general-counsel/_files/ed-affairs/uc-residency-instructions-appeal-form.pdf" target="_blank">Instructions and Application to Appeal a Nonresident Classification</a> </p> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" alt="" /> I still have questions.</h4> 
       <div> 
        <p> <strong>All inquiries should be directed to: </strong> <br /> <br /> Kendra McCann <br /> Residence Affairs Officer<br /> University of California, Riverside <br /> Office of the Registrar <br /> 2249 Student Services Building </p> 
        <p><br /> <a href="mailto:Kendra.McCann@ucr.edu" target="_blank">Kendra.McCann@ucr.edu<br /> </a>951-827-5970 <br /> <br /> No other university personnel are authorized to supply information connected to residency for tuition and fee purposes. </p> 
       </div>

     The University of California, Riverside (
    <a href="http://www.ucr.edu/">www.ucr.edu</a>) is a doctoral research university, a living laboratory for groundbreaking exploration of issues critical to Inland Southern California, the state and communities around the world. Reflecting California's diverse culture, UCR's enrollment has exceeded 21,000 students. The campus opened a medical school in 2013 and has reached the heart of the Coachella Valley by way of the UCR Palm Desert Center. The campus has an annual statewide economic impact of more than $1 billion. A broadcast studio with fiber cable to the AT&amp;T Hollywood hub is available for live or taped interviews.
