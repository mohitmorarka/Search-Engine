        <p>The Office of Resource Planning and Budget endeavors to provide objective, accurate, and timely information and analysis to every level of campus leadership, thereby supporting the effective management of all campus resources. The responsibilities of this office include:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li>developing and implementing policies and procedures related to the management of campus financial resources&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </li> 
         <li>administering the annual campus-wide budget process</li> 
         <li>managing the campus budget, the staffing system, and unallocated resources </li> 
         <li>allocating campus-level funding&nbsp;</li> 
         <li>administering the annual Auxiliary and Self-Supporting Enterprise budget and rate review process</li> 
         <li>monitoring campus debt ratios and reviewing capital budget appropriation requests</li> 
         <li>acting as a liaison with the Office of the President and others </li> 
        </ul>

        <p> It is essential for each operating unit on campus to engage in meaningful resource planning and financial management in order to perform its role in the University’s mission of teaching, research, and public service.</p> 
        <p> It is the responsibility of each unit head to ensure that resource planning and financial management occurs in an effective manner at all organizational levels.</p> 
        <h3> I.&nbsp;Resource Planning and Budget Development</h3> 
        <p> Resource planning is the process through which the unit’s strategic plan is articulated into resource terms. This process ultimately leads to the development of the budget. All resource planning and budget development should include the following components:</p> 
        <ol> 
         <li> <h5> A plan with clearly defined goals and objectives</h5> The plan should be easily understood, with attainable goals and measurable objectives. The goals and objectives should be specific enough to be integrated into the overall planning and budget process.</li> 
         <li> <h5> A process for identifying and evaluating key factors required to accomplish the plan</h5> <p> The evaluation, selection, and ultimate usage of key factors should be based on a clear understanding of the nature of the key factor and the impact it has in the achievement of the goals and objectives. Key factors may vary depending upon each organization’s unique mission. Examples of key factors are:</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li> Student Enrollment</li> 
           <li> Teaching workload (including faculty FTE, instructional assistants)</li> 
           <li> Salaries and benefits (including FTE)</li> 
           <li> Equipment and Supplies</li> 
           <li> Technology</li> 
           <li> Support and auxiliary services</li> 
           <li> Space needs and related costs</li> 
           <li> Anticipated revenue</li> 
           <li> External regulations</li> 
           <li> The Market</li> 
          </ul> </li> 
         <li> <h5> A budget development process that includes a thorough analysis of all relevant data and supports management decision-making</h5> This process should ensure consistent use of proven methods for gathering and analyzing data, as well as compliance with relevant budget and financial policies. The analysis should include the following, as appropriate:<br /> 
          <ul> 
           <li> A thorough re-evaluation of all assumptions, analyses, plans and budgets used in the previous year's planning process</li> 
           <li> An examination of budget and financial performance from prior years</li> 
           <li> An evaluation of current year's performance against both the budget and the goals and objectives</li> 
           <li> An identification of the modifications required in the new plan to reflect changes in the goals and objectives</li> 
           <li> An evaluation of cost and risk factors</li> 
          </ul> </li> 
         <li> <h5> A budget that articulates the goals and objectives in resource terms</h5> The budget should be realistic, reasonable, and attainable and should be accompanied by a descriptive narrative. Components of the budget and narrative should include:<br /> 
          <ul> 
           <li> All funding sources</li> 
           <li> Revenue estimates</li> 
           <li> Major expenditures by category, including identification of indirect expenditures</li> 
           <li> A description clearly supporting the resource requirements</li> 
           <li> Explanation of major assumptions and forecasting methods used</li> 
           <li> Identification of significant changes in current operation</li> 
           <li> Contingency plans</li> 
          </ul> </li> 
        </ol> 
        <h3> II. Financial Management</h3> 
        <p> Financial management is the process of ensuring that the unit's financial performance is consistent with the utilization of resources as delineated in the budget developed to accomplish the objectives of the plan. It is assumed that data integrity has been assured throughout. Financial management should be supported through the following activities:</p> 
        <h5> Review and Analysis of Financial Performance</h5> 
        <p> At a minimum, financial reports should be prepared by the end of the subsequent period ensuring adequate time to make adjustments or corrections. These reports should:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li> Compare budget to actual financial performance</li> 
         <li> Include all sources of revenue and expense</li> 
         <li> Provide projections of year-end balances</li> 
         <li> Identify any other problems or exceptions</li> 
        </ul> 
        <p> Analysis should be conducted to achieve the following objectives:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li> Ensure that expenditures are reasonable and necessary to accomplish the unit's goals</li> 
         <li> Ensure that resources are being utilized efficiently</li> 
         <li> Measure and understand financial performance</li> 
         <li> Determine whether sufficient resources exist for continued operation</li> 
         <li> Clearly identify trends and special areas of concern</li> 
         <li> Detect changes in circumstances or the business environment</li> 
        </ul> 
        <h5> Corrective Actions</h5> 
        <p> Corrective action should be taken in a timely manner, as appropriate, in response to the review and analysis of financial performance. Examples of specific corrective actions are:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li> Revising plans or budgets to reflect changed circumstances</li> 
         <li> Changing or eliminating activities</li> 
         <li> Obtaining additional funding</li> 
         <li> Modifying goals or objectives</li> 
         <li> Altering future budget</li> 
        </ul>

     <h2 class="intro" style="font-size: 3em;"> Apply to UCR in Three Easy Steps </h2> 
     <div class="grid_9 prefix_3 alpha omega"> 
      <ul class="bigIcon"> 
       <li class="first"><span><a href="#gather">Gather</a></span> Your Information</li> 
       <li class="second"><span><a href="#write">Write</a></span> Your Personal Statement</li> 
       <li class="third"><span><a href="#complete">Complete</a></span> the UC Application</li> 
      </ul> 
     </div> 
     <div class="genericBody version2"> 
      <div class="clearfix divider"> 
       <div class="grid_6 alpha"> 
        <br /> 
        <h3> <a name="gather" id="gather"></a>1. Gather Your Information</h3> 
        <p class="subhead v1 orange"> You will need:</p> 
        <ul style="font-size: 13.2px;"> 
         <li>Transcripts</li> 
         <li>Test scores </li> 
         <li>Annual income (optional) </li> 
         <li>Social Security number (if you have one) </li> 
         <li>Citizenship status </li> 
         <li>Statewide Student ID (optional). </li> 
         <li>Credit card (or check)</li> 
         <li>Up-to-date browser (Firefox, Internet Explorer 8, Chrome 6, Safari 5)</li> 
         <li>Enable JavaScript </li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <div class="grid_6 omega"> 
        <img alt="" width="341" height="324" src="/Images/apply/guyComputer_apply.png" /> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
      <div class="clearfix orange divider" style="margin-top: 26px;"> 
       <div class="grid_12 alpha omega">
        <h3> <a name="write" id="write"></a>2. Write Your Personal Statement </h3>
       </div> 
       <div class="clearfix">
        &nbsp;
       </div> 
       <div class="grid_6 alpha"> 
        <p> <strong>Respond to two prompts</strong>— one general and&nbsp;<em>either</em>&nbsp;the freshman or transfer. Use a maximum of 1,000 words <em>total</em>. Allocate the word count as you wish, but each answer should be at least 250 words. </p> 
        <img src="/Images/apply/girlComputer_apply.png" alt="" width="337" height="395" /> 
       </div> 
       <div class="grid_6 omega"> 
        <p> <strong>General Prompt </strong>(All): Tell us about a personal quality, talent, accomplishment, contribution or experience that is important to you. What about this quality or accomplishment makes you proud and how does it relate to the person you are? </p> 
        <p> <strong>Freshman: </strong>Describe the world you come from — for example, your family, community or school — and tell us how your world has shaped your dreams and aspirations. </p> 
        <p> <strong>Transfer:</strong> What is your intended major? Discuss how your interest in the subject developed and describe any experience you have had in the field — such as volunteer work, internships and employment, participation in student organizations and activities — and what you have gained from your involvement. </p> 
        <p> See additional instructions for military in the <a href="http://www.universityofcalifornia.edu/admissions/how-to-apply/apply-online/index.html" target="_blank"> UC quick-start guides</a>. All applicants will have a chance to add “additional comments.”</p> 
        <p> <strong>Tips:</strong> Start early. Consider your audience. Be persuasive. Proofread. Get feedback. Be yourself. Relax, the personal statement is just one of many components considered. </p> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
      <div class="clearfix orange" style="margin-top: 26px;"> 
       <div class="grid_6 alpha"> 
        <h3> <a name="complete" id="complete"></a>3. Complete the UC Application</h3> 
        <ul class="version1"> 
         <li>Use worksheets in <a href="http://www.universityofcalifornia.edu/admissions/how-to-apply/apply-online/index.html" target="_blank"> UC quick-start guides</a> to organize your activities and achievements.&nbsp;</li> 
         <li><a href="https://admissions.universityofcalifornia.edu/applicant/login.htm" target="_blank">Access the online UC application</a> beginning Oct. 1. Submit your application Nov. 1-30. </li> 
         <li>All applications are <strong>due Nov. 30. </strong></li> 
         <li>The fee is $70 for each UC ($80 for international/non-immigrant applicants). </li> 
         <li>You can apply for a fee waiver when you fill out the<a href="https://admissions.universityofcalifornia.edu/applicant/login.htm" target="_blank"> online UC application</a>. You’ll need your family's income and the number of people supported by it.</li> 
         <li>If you have difficulties, email <a href="mailto:ucinfo@applyucsupport.net">ucinfo@applyucsupport.net</a> or call (800) 207-1710 (US) or (661) 336-5723 (outside US).</li> 
        </ul> 
        <h3> After You Apply</h3> 
        <ul class="version1"> 
         <li><strong>Print</strong> your receipt.</li> 
         <li><strong>Watch </strong>for a confirmation email. As soon as you apply, you should recieve one. If you do not, contact <a href="mailto:ucinfo@applyucsupport.net">ucinfo@applyucsupport.net, </a>(800) 207-1710 (US) or (661) 336-5723 (outside US).</li> 
         <li><a href="http://vcsaweb.ucr.edu/MyUCR/CreateAccount.aspx" target="_blank">Create a My UCR Account</a> and link it to your application to get the most up-to-date information on your admissions status. Freshmen admission notification begins <strong>February 1</strong>. Transfer admission notification begins <strong>March 1</strong>. </li> 
         <li><strong>Order</strong> SAT/ACT test score reports.</li> 
         <li><a href="http://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/how-to-apply/after-you-apply/update-transfer-application/index.html" target="_blank">Update your application</a> (transfer students). Log in and update your grades and course records by <strong>January 31</strong>.</li> 
         <li>You can<strong> make changes</strong> to your application after you apply! See the <a href="http://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/how-to-apply/apply-online/index.html" target="_blank">UC quick-start guides</a> for instructions. </li> 
        </ul> 
       </div>

      <div class="span4 hero-unit"> 
       <p>We provide contract management and oversight of LBNL and ensure UC obligations for the LANS and LLNS, LLCs.</p> 
      </div> 
      <div class="span4">
       <p>The Laboratory Management Office (LMO) is responsible for managing contract administration and governance at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL) and for UC's obligations as an owner of Los Alamos National Security (LANS) and Lawrence Livermore National Security (LLNS), LLCs. The&nbsp;LMO contains two main organizations: Operations and Administration and Laboratory Programs. <a href="about-lab-management.html">Read more</a></p>

     <div class="span-11"> 
      <h3>What is iGrade?</h3> 
      <br /> 
      <p> <a href="http://igrade.ucr.edu/igrade/login" target="_blank">iGrade</a> is a Web-based grade submission system enabling UCR faculty to submit grades electronically (via the Web or from an Excel spreadsheet) directly into the campus Student Information System. </p> 
      <h4>How do I change a grade?</h4> 
      <ul>
       <li>If you are a faculty member and made an error in grade submission, please fill out a Grade Change and Incomplete Removal Form and submit it to the Registrar’s Office.</li>
      </ul> 
      <ul>
       <li>Submission of the Grade Change and Incomplete Removal Form can be submitted by intercampus mail or in person with a photo ID. Please note that students and student assistants may not submit this form in person.</li>
      </ul> 
     </div>

     <div class="span-11"> 
      <div class="accordion" style="margin-right: 5px;"> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> iGrade Functionality </h4> 
       <div class="addBullet"> 
        <strong>iGrade provides campus faculty with the following important functionality:</strong> 
        <ul> 
         <li>Ability to view class roster on the Web.</li> 
         <li>Input grades via a Web interface OR upload grades from an Excel spreadsheet.</li> 
         <li>Perform edit checks online and obtain information on whether or not grades are ready to post.</li> 
        </ul> 
        <strong>And iGrade provides the following benefits:</strong> 
        <ul> 
         <li>Eliminates the need for paper submission of grades.</li> 
         <li>Increases the speed of grade distribution to students via Growl (the campus website providing students Web access to grades, registration, etc.).</li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> iGrade Availability and Deadlines </h4> 
       <div class="addBullet"> 
        <ul> 
         <li>iGrade is available to view class rosters on the day the quarter begins.</li> 
         <li>iGrade opens to submit grades the Monday of 10th week.</li> 
         <li>Deadline to submit grades is the Tuesday after finals week for Fall. Winter and Spring quarters. See <a href="/content/pdfs/SUMMER2010SUBMISSIONCALENDAR.pdf" target="_blank">schedule <img src="/images/pdf.gif" /></a> for Summer Sessions. </li> 
         <li>While grades may be entered into iGrade at any time, the availability to submit grades using the iGrade system is dependent on the availability of the Student Information System. SIS and iGrade may be (and is usually) available before 8:00am. It becomes available whenever the nightly process completes. </li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> Who to Contact </h4> 
       <div class="addBullet"> 
        <ul> 
         <li>NetID and Password Help - Faculty: Contact the Help Desk (x2-3555) for password assistance, or lookup your NetID <a href="http://www.cnc.ucr.edu/esai/index.php?content=ucrnetid.html">here</a>.</li> 
         <li>If there are any questions regarding faculty course assignments, please contact the scheduling contact for the department.</li> 
         <li>If you have any questions about iGrade or you need further assistance, please contact <a href="mailto:josie.hernandez@ucr.edu">Josie Hernandez</a> or call x2-3409.</li> 
         <li>For technical support with the iGrade application please contact <a href="mailto:mark.nicolay@ucr.edu">Computing &amp; Communications</a>.</li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <h4> <img src="/Images/square-bullet.png" /> MSO Checklist </h4> 
       <div class="addBullet"> 
        <ul> 
         <li>Please ensure that all new faculty appointments or promotions are submitted via Excel to Academic Scheduling in the Registrar's Office. These should be sent as soon as possible but no later than 3rd week of the quarter. <strong>This is especially critical for all new faculty and any faculty transitioning from a TA appointment</strong>. </li> 
         <li>Please assist all new faculty in ensuring that they have a working UCR NetID; the NetID is required to log into iGrade. Email reminders to faculty will be sent from the Registrar's Office via webmail. </li> 
         <li>Please ensure that any temporary ID numbers in SIS are appropriately updated to permanent faculty ID numbers. Permanent faculty ID numbers traditionally start with 85. </li> 
         <li>Please ensure all courses have a faculty designation. Courses assigned to &quot;Staff&quot; will not be visible in iGrade and therefore will not be available for grading.</li> 
         <li>If the instructor of a course changes in the middle of the quarter, please ensure that the new information is entered into SIS so the new instructor can grade the course. </li> 
         <li>If you have faculty who are classified as an affiliate or visiting faculty who cannot be added to our payroll system because they are currently on the payroll of another UC institution--please direct the faculty to the appropriate department staff member to have the SIS screen printed for them. For these faculty only we will allow grades to be submitted by paper to the Registrar's Office to data enter.</li> 
        </ul>

     The University of California, Riverside (
    <a href="http://www.ucr.edu/">www.ucr.edu</a>) is a doctoral research university, a living laboratory for groundbreaking exploration of issues critical to Inland Southern California, the state and communities around the world. Reflecting California's diverse culture, UCR's enrollment has exceeded 21,000 students. The campus opened a medical school in 2013 and has reached the heart of the Coachella Valley by way of the UCR Palm Desert Center. The campus has an annual statewide economic impact of more than $1 billion. A broadcast studio with fiber cable to the AT&amp;T Hollywood hub is available for live or taped interviews.
