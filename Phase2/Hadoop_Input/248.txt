      <h3>The Eaton Collection of Science Fiction &amp; Fantasy</h3> 
      <p>The Eaton Collection of Science Fiction &amp; Fantasy is the largest publicly-accessible collection of science fiction, fantasy, horror and utopian literature in the world. It is housed in the UC Riverside Libraries' Special Collections &amp; Archives in the Tom&aacute;s Rivera Library.</p> 
     </div> 
     <ul class="column3R  l_navmenu"> 
      <li><a href="using.html">About the collection and how to use it</a></li> 
      <li><a href="about.html">History of the Eaton Collection</a></li> 
      <li><a href="giving.html">Contributing to the Eaton Collection</a></li> 
     </ul> 
     <div class="clear"></div> 
    </div> 
   </div> 
   <div class="l_background_pannel"> 
    <div class="l_pannel_center"> 
     <div class="column23"> 
      <p><strong>The collection is a major resource for research</strong> and is visited by scholars from around the world for both its American and international holdings. It features over 300,000 items including:</p> 
      <ul class="tight l_collection_list"> 
       <li>Over 100,000 hardback and paperback books.</li> 
       <li>Full runs of many pulp magazines.</li> 
       <li>Nearly 100,000 fanzines.</li> 
       <li>Film and visual material, including 500 shooting scripts from science fiction films.</li> 
       <li>Comic books, anime and manga.</li> 
       <li>Collectible ephemera and realia, including cards, posters, pins and action figures.</li> 
      </ul>

      <h3>eClassroom Support Site</h3> 
      <p>A partnership between the College of Natural and Agricultural Sciences and Computing and Communications.</p>
      <p> </p>
      <p>The eClassroom@UCR, a collaboration between the College of Natural and Agricultural Sciences, the Department of Statistics, and Computing and Communications is now being used for undergraduate Statistics lab sections. The eClassroom was developed to provide an interactive, participative learning environment for classes utilizing computer-aided instruction, especially undergraduate Statistics. Using videoconferencing equipment and IP-based computer lab management software, the eClassroom incorporates two multimedia labs into a single virtual classroom.</p>
      <p> </p>
      <p align="center"><a class="highslide" onclick="return hs.expand(this, {captionId: 'caption1'})" href="/eclassroom/images/eClassroom Diagram - Conceptual Drawing.jpg"><img src="/eclassroom/images/eclassroom_concept_thumb.jpg" alt="" title="" width="150" height="112" /></a></p> 
      <div align="center"> 
       <table width="38%" border="0" height="185"> 
        <tbody>
         <tr> 
          <td> 
           <div align="center">
            <a class="highslide" onclick="return hs.expand(this, {captionId: 'caption1'})" href="/eclassroom/eclassroom_linked_rooms.gif"><img src="/eclassroom/images/linked_rooms_frontpage.gif" alt="" title="" width="350" height="120" /></a>
           </div> <p> click to enlarge</p> </td> 
         </tr> 
        </tbody>
       </table> 
      </div> 
      <p>In the CNAS Statistics building, two multimedia computing facilities(rooms 2680 and 2686) were interactively connected with videoconferencing equipment, a document camera, a 60&quot; projection screen, NetOp School classroom control software, and a Windows NT server addressing 42 Pentium-class personal computers (including an instructor control station).</p> 
      <p>The eclassroom was created to meet the following goals:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>A virtual classroom large enough to make a meaningful reduction in the number of undergraduate statistics sections.</li> 
       <li>Faculty control and management over each student's work from a central location.</li> 
       <li> Faculty able to highlight work from a particular student or group of students, thus engaging the class in a &quot;participative&quot; learning environment.</li> 
       <li>Faculty able to introduce multimedia material (from a VCR, laptop, document camera, etc.) to offer students a richer, more stimulating classroom experience.</li> 
       <li>An infrastructure that would allow the facility to &quot;grow&quot;; specifically, by linking classrooms across campus via UCR's high speed network and technology already in place.</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p>The eClassroom came on-line in the Fall 2000 academic quarter and has been used successfully by a number of faculty teaching various courses in Statistics. The combination of classroom management technology, multimedia functionality and interior design have provided a rich and robust learning environment.</p>

      <h1>Mobius Trio</h1> 
      <br /> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">Culver Center of the Arts</h2> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">Student Reading Session - Wed, Mar 12, 2014, 10am - 12pm<br />Concert - Wed, Mar 12, 2014, 8pm - 9:30pm<br /></h2> 
      <br />
      <h2 class="spaced-out"><strong><em>Free Admission, limited seating for Reading</em></strong></h2> 
      <br /> 
      <br /> 
      <p>Described by the eminent Sergio Assad as &quot;the most inventive and exciting young guitar ensemble today&quot;, Mobius Trio has made it its mission to fully integrate the classical guitar into the 21st century's nascent musical lexicon. The Trio exclusively performs music that they have commissioned; they seek to expand the guitar ensemble's repertoire to encompass all of the myriad currents passing through contemporary art music. Mobius Trio has commissioned over a dozen composers in their first two years of existence, and that pace isn't slowing. <br /><br /> Mobius Trio was formed in 2010 by guitarists Robert Nance, Mason Fish, and Matthew Holmes-Linder while studying at the San Francisco Conservatory of Music. They started things off with a bang, commissioning and learning five pieces within the span of a couple of months, and performing many of those pieces at Washington, DC's Kennedy Center the following April. Matt, Rob, and Mason are all accomplished, competition-winning guitarists in their own right, and they all live and work in San Francisco. When not rehearsing, the group enjoys BBQ, scotch, and hacky sack. <br /><br /> Mobius Trio's residency at the Culver includes an exciting public masterclass where UCR student composer works will be read and workshopped. Not only are these sessions an invaluable resource for student composers, but also offer a fascinating glimpse into the creative process for general audiences. Here new playing techniques are tested, phrasing and dynamics are evaluated, and interactive electronics, a hallmark of the UCR digital composition program, are fine-tuned. In this environment, the group will have a direct dialogue with composers that transcends the printed notes on the page. Don't miss this rare behind-the-scenes opportunity! </p>
