      <p><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">RIVERSIDE, Calif.</span><i style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;"> (</i><a style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;" href="http://www.ucr.edu/"><i>www.ucr.edu</i></a><i style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">) – </i><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">Susan Straight, a native of Riverside and a longtime professor of creative writing at UC Riverside, will receive the Kirsch Award for lifetime achievement at the awards ceremony for the Los Angeles Times Book Prizes. The event is Friday, April 11 at the University of Southern California’s Bovard Auditorium. &nbsp;</span></p> 
      <p><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">“Susan Straight is a Southern California original and a tireless supporter, and creator, of our literary culture,” said Times book critic David L. Ulin. “Her novels opened up not just California literature but American literature to the Inland Empire and to the often-neglected voices of the people there. Through her work as a teacher, she has inspired a new generation of California writers.”</span></p> 
      <p><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">Straight is the author of eight novels and two books for children, essayist, short story writer and reviewer. Her honors include a Guggenheim Fellowship and Lannan Literary Prize. Her novel “Highwire Moon” was a National Book Award finalist in 2001 and “A Million Nightingales” was shortlisted for the 2006 Los Angeles Times Book Prize in Fiction. Many of her books are set in a fictional version of Riverside, called “Rio Seco.” She has three grown daughters and she blogs regularly for KCET at “<a href="http://www.kcet.org/updaily/socal_focus/commentary/notes-of-a-native-daughter/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.kcet.org/updaily/socal_focus/commentary/notes-of-a-native-daughter/']);">Notes of a Native Daughter</a>.”&nbsp;</span></p> 
      <p>Andrew Winer&nbsp;the chair of the creative writing department, called Straight “a brilliant and compassionate writer.”</p> 
      <p><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">“A tireless chronicler, in her work, of the underprivileged and the ignored, Susan Straight has also championed multitudes of young writers who might otherwise not have had a shot at publishing,” he said. “Her influence and reach is so pervasive that her name is virtually synonymous with the Inland Empire, and certainly with UCR’s Department of Creative Writing, where she has been instrumental in helping to shape and grow one of the most vital creative writing programs in the country.”<br /> </span><br /> The Kirsch award is named in honor of&nbsp; Robert Kirsch, the&nbsp;<i>Los Angeles Times</i>&nbsp;book critic from 1952 until his death in 1980. It was his idea to establish the book prizes.</p> 
      <p><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">The Book Prizes recognize 50 works in 10 different categories: biography, current interest, fiction, first fiction (the Art Seidenbaum Award), graphic novel/comics, history, mystery/thriller, poetry, science and technology, and young adult literature. Finalists and winners are selected by judging panels composed of writers who specialize in each genre. The complete list of this year’s finalists and further information, including past winners, is available at </span><a style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;" href="http://events.latimes.com/bookprizes/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://events.latimes.com/bookprizes/']);">latimes.com/bookprizes</a><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">. Tickets will be available online at 10 a.m. Monday, March 17</span><sup>th</sup><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">.</span></p> 
      <p><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">The event is the prelude to the 19</span><sup>th</sup><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;"> annual </span><a style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;" href="http://events.latimes.com/festivalofbooks/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://events.latimes.com/festivalofbooks/']);">Los Angeles Times Festival of Books</a><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">, the largest public literary festival in the nation, to be held on USC’s campus April 12</span><sup>th</sup><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;"> and 13</span><sup>th</sup><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">.</span></p> 
      <p>On Saturday, April 26, Straight will be at UC Berkeley to receive the 2014 Carey McWilliams Award from the California Studies Association. The award is given each year to a writer, scholar, or artist who lives up to the best tradition of McWilliams’s work, giving voice to the struggles and triumphs of the people of California.</p>

           <tr> 
            <td> <h4 class="BulletIntro">Launch of UCnet</h4> <p class="BulletIntro">On February 6, 2014, <a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a>, a new systemwide website for faculty, staff, and retirees was launched as part of an initiative to improve the online tools and resources offered to UC audiences. <a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a> is a place where UC faculty, staff and retirees can:</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li>Read timely news and interesting stories about the university and the people who work here</li> 
              <li>Find information about benefits and policies as well as a wealth of university tools and resources</li> 
              <li>Share views with colleagues from around the system</li> 
              <li>Learn about opportunities to get involved in UC community activities</li> 
             </ul> <p class="Body"><a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a> has replaced the At Your Service website. Due to this transition, certain links from the UCR Human Resources website that formerly pointed to the At Your Service website are now being redirected to the <a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a> home page, and not necessarily to the content expected. The Human Resources office is working to update these links to point directly to the specific content on <a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a> as they did previously to the At Your Service website. <a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a> does feature a robust search tool that should prove helpful during this transitional phase and in the future to assist in navigating to needed information.</p> <p>Please note that <a class="external" href="https://atyourserviceonline.ucop.edu/ayso/" target="_blank">At Your Service Online</a>, the password-protected portal used to access personal pay and benefits information, still exists.</p> </td> 
           </tr> 
           <tr> 
            <td> <h4>Human Resources Re-Opens Lobby</h4> <p>In an effort to better serve the campus community, we are pleased to announce that the Human Resources lobby is now open and &nbsp;staffed during normal business hours. In addition, we will continue to provide a computer kiosk for job applicants and a phone for visitors to use to contact HR/LR employees directly.</p> <p><strong>Our full service office hours are now</strong>:<br />Monday – Friday: 8:00 a.m. – 5:00 p.m.</p> </td> 
           </tr> 
           <tr> 
            <td> <h4>Yes, You Can Change Your Medical Plan Selection</h4> <p>The University has made significant changes to the health care plans for 2014. While the campus made every effort to provide information regarding the new options, it is possible that there may be faculty and staff who wish to change their current election. If you conclude you are in the wrong plan and decide you want to make a change, please contact the Benefits Office at <a href="mailto:benefits@ucr.edu?subject=2014%20Medical%20Plan">benefits@ucr.edu</a> or by phone at (951) 827 -4766 as soon as possible, but no later than <em><strong>March 31, 2014</strong></em>.</p> </td> 
           </tr> 
           <tr> 
            <td> <h4>Bargaining Bulletin:&nbsp; UC Reaches Agreements with UPTE</h4> <p>On December 13, 2013, after many months of negotiations, representatives of UC and UPTE came to tentative agreements on successor contracts covering Healthcare Professionals (HX), Research Support&nbsp; Professionals (RX) and Technical (TX) staff.&nbsp; The bargaining unit members ratified the agreements on December 20, 2013.&nbsp; This follows the recent ratifications of agreements covering represented Librarians (LX), Registered Nurses (NX) and Police Officers (PA).</p> <p>The multi-year agreements for each of these bargaining units will be posted to the HR website as soon as they are finalized by the parties.&nbsp; Training dates for supervisors, managers, and HR professionals will be announced soon.</p> </td> 
           </tr>
