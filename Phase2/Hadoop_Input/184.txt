      <p><img src="/images/staffpro.jpg" alt="Administrative and Staff Employees" /></p> 
      <p>Administrative and staff employees who want to work in a multicultural setting with promising practices supporting career advancement and professional development opportunities should look to UCR. Our campus values a family friendly atmosphere and a supportive work climate emphasizing a collaborative effort between central administration and campus employees. Currently, UCR is implementing a series of staff-recommended incentive programs.</p> 
      <p>In 2006, UCR conducted a survey to assess staff’s perception of the current state of the campus climate.&nbsp; From the&nbsp;results of the <a href="http://www.climatesurvey.ucr.edu/">survey</a>, the Staff Assembly, union represented employees and the Vice Chancellor of Administration created three committees to formulate and implement campus initiatives to&nbsp;enhance campus Communications, Employee Recognition and Career Advancement Opportunities.</p> 
      <p>In concert with these efforts, Human Resources, Labor Relations, Academic Personnel, the AVC Diversity, Excellence &amp; Equity and Affirmative Action have been working together to raise awareness of available education and training&nbsp;- particularly in the area of diversity - as well as developing new and innovative opportunities.&nbsp; Providing a positive and productive work environment calls for a campus culture that not only is diverse in its appearance, but prides itself on its cross-cultural communications and multicultural competencies.&nbsp;For programs and resources currently available to UCR staff/employees, see:</p>

      <h3>Solar Energy in Inland Southern California: The Future is Now</h3> 
      <p></p> 
      <div class="two-thirds"> 
       <hr /> 
       <h3 class="center"><a href="/events/solar2014/linked_agenda/SOLAR-2014_LinkedAgenda.pdf" target="_blank">Agenda with Presentation Links</a></h3> 
       <p>On behalf of the Bourns College of Engineering – Center for Environmental Research and Technology; the Center for Sustainable Suburban Development; and the Southern California Research Initiative for Solar Energy, welcome to the first solar conference at the University of California, Riverside -- &nbsp;“Solar Energy in Inland Southern California: The Future is Now.”</p> 
       <p>This conference is designed to inform attendees about the importance of solar energy by discussing the latest technology, public policy and regulations, economics, and opportunities for incorporating solar energy in Inland Southern California communities.</p> 
       <p>We believe solar energy can play a significant role in the region’s future, not only because of the abundance of sunlight afforded to the region and its potential as another source of efficient and cost effective energy, but as an integral contributor to Inland Southern California’s employment base.</p> 
       <p>We are fortunate in having many of the solar energy industry’s leading experts with us today, and an impressive group of individuals who will share outstanding solar energy programs that are in place in their organizations.</p> 
       <p>Finally, we would like to thank all of our sponsors for helping to make this conference possible. We hope this will be the first of an annual conference at UCR on the contributions of solar energy to the quality of life in Inland Southern California.&nbsp;</p> 
       <p>Sincerely,&nbsp;</p> 
       <p>Matthew Barth<br />Director, CE-CERT</p> 
       <p>Ronald O. Loveridge<br />Director, CSSD</p> 
       <p>Alfredo Martinez-Morales<br />Managing Director, SC-RISE</p>
