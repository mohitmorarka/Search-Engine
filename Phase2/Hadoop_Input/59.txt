     <h2 class="intro"> Play With Heart</h2> 
     <h3 class="verd intro"> From fired-up fans, to heroic athletes, to die-hard intramural teams – we play with heart at UCR. </h3> 
     <div class="clearfix"> 
      <div> 
       <div class="grid_4 alpha"> 
        <img alt="Male UCR basketball player dribbling" width="242" height="280" src="/Images/Athlet/Basketball.png" /> 
        <br />
        <br /> 
        <h3> Come to a Game! </h3> Face paint is temporary, but glory is forever! 
        <a href="http://gohighlanders.com/">Cheer on your team</a> with nearly 3,000 other fans. 
        <br /> 
        <img alt="Brenda Martinez running" width="231" height="260" src="/Images/Athlet/running.png" style="margin-top:30" /> 
       </div> 
       <div class="grid_8 omega"> 
        <h3>Our Highlanders </h3> 
        <p> After five national championships, countless conference titles and more than 100 All-American accolades at the Division II level, we moved to NCAA Division I, the nation's highest level of intercollegiate athletics competition.</p> 
        <p> Since then, <a href="http://gohighlanders.com/">our Highlanders</a> have celebrated Big West Conference regular season and/or tournament championships in baseball, women's basketball, women's cross country and men's golf while making NCAA tournament appearances as teams or individuals in baseball, women's basketball, men's and women's cross country, men's golf, women's soccer and men's and women's track &amp; field.</p> 
        <p> Five former Highlanders compete professionally in basketball, two in soccer, and two in track and field. Dozens of former Highlanders play in the majors and minors. One of our athletes even competed in the <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/7182">Inline Hockey World Championships</a>! </p> 
        <p>Visit <a href="http://www.gohighlanders.com">Go Highlanders</a> to learn more about our teams, athletes and the 17 sports we compete in. </p> 
        <div class="grid_4 alpha"> 
         <h3 class="lightBlue fingHuge centerTxt" style="margin-top: 10px;"> 4</h3> 
         <p> The number of former <strong><span class="orange">Highlanders who own World Series Championship rings.</span></strong> Marc Rzepczynski (St. Louis Cardinals, 2011), Dan Runzler (San Francisco Giants, 2010), Anthony Claggett (New York Yankees, 2009) and Troy Percival (Anaheim Angels, 2002). </p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="grid_4 omega "> 
         <img alt="Kayaker navigating rapids" width="246" height="308" src="/Images/Athlet/rapids.png" class="right" /> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
      <div> 
       <div class="grid_4 alpha"> 
        <h3 class="darkblue giant"> 2016 </h3> 
        <p>The year three-time All-American Brenda Martinez has her sites set on to run in the Olympics in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.</p> 
        <h3> Sports Clubs </h3> 
        <p>Our <a href="https://highlanderlink.ucr.edu/organizations?SearchValue=Recreational&amp;SearchType=Category&amp;CurrentPage=1&amp;SelectedCategoryId=4173">sports clubs</a> give you the opportunity to dance, box, fence, rock climb, swim and more.</p> 
        <h3> Intramural Sports </h3> 
        <p> Choose from <a href="http://imsports.ucr.edu/">recreational and competitive divisions</a> and sports ranging from volleyball and water polo to table tennis and Xbox. </p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="grid_8 omega"> 
        <div class="grid_4 alpha"> 
         <img alt="Female UCR volleyball player bumping" width="218" height="240" src="/Images/Athlet/Volletball.png" style="margin-top:50" /> 
         <br />
         <br /> 
         <h3> Your Gym </h3> 
         <p>The <a href="http://www.recreation.ucr.edu/Pages/default.aspx">Student Recreation Center </a>provides you with state-of-the art equipment, personal training and fun classes. Plus, we’re adding a huge swimming pool, indoor track and triple the cardio and weights<a href="http://www.recreation.ucr.edu/SRC_expansion/Pages/default.aspx"> by 2014! </a></p> 
         <p>Membership is automatically included in your student fees. </p> 
        </div>

         <br /> 
         <h3>Outdoor Excursions</h3> 
         <p> Camp, kayak, surf, hike and climb with <a href="http://www.recreation.ucr.edu/outdoor_excursions/Pages/OutdoorExcursionsHome.aspx">UCR Outdoor Excursions</a> or head to the exclusive <a href="http://www.recreation.ucr.edu/Big_Bear/Pages/default.aspx">UCR Lake House at Big Bear</a> for some fun in the snow.</p>

       <h3>The Department of Environmental Sciences</h3> 
      </div> 
      <div class="column23"> 
       <!-- com.omniupdate.div label="main" group="Everyone" button="707" -->
       <!-- ouc:editor csspath="/z-omniupdate/css/style.css" cssmenu="/z-omniupdate/css/style.txt" width="460"/ -->
       <p>We are an interdisciplinary department within the College of Natural and Agricultural Sciences at UC Riverside with both undergraduate and graduate programs in environmental science. The Department seeks to expand knowledge of the physical, chemical, biological and human components of the Earth System, through cutting edge research, rigorous student training and service to the community. The Department consists of 16 faculty and 50 graduate students, post docs and research staff working within and across the fields of soil sciences, aquatic sciences, microbial ecology, atmospheric sciences and environmental economics and policy.</p> 
       <p>Our undergraduate program exposes students to a wide array of environmental science disciplines and prepares them for careers in State/Federal resource agencies, private consulting and for graduate education. There are four areas within our graduate program: Soil and Water Sciences, Environmental Microbiology, Environmental Chemistry and Ecotoxicology, and Environmental and Natural Resource Economics and Policy. All of our graduate areas of study emphasize quantitative, interdisciplinary study of the Earth System. Alumni from our graduate programs have gone on to successful careers in government and university science, resource management, education and economics.</p> 
       <p>We invite you to explore our new and expanded website to learn more about our exciting research and education programs and to meet our faculty and students!</p>

       <p><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">RIVERSIDE, Calif. (</span><a style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;" href="http://www.ucr.edu/">www.ucr.edu</a><span style="font-size: 1.083em; line-height: 1.231em;">) – In order to emphasize the importance of undergraduate research to the student learning experience, Chancellor Kim A. Wilcox and his wife Diane Del Buono have announced they will endow a new Chancellor’s Research Fellowship at UC Riverside with a personal gift of $100,000.</span></p> 
       <p>This will be the 13<sup>th</sup> research fellowship providing financial support to one more of an elite set of undergraduate scholars at UCR.&nbsp; Chancellor’s Research Fellows work with individual faculty mentors on original research generally leading to national presentations and publication of their work.</p> 
       <p>The Fellows are an important part of the undergraduate program at UCR, where more than half of all undergraduate students participate in basic and applied research or creative and artistic projects with faculty members.</p> 
       <p>Chancellor Wilcox and Ms. Del Buono have asked that a priority be given to first-generation college students when selecting this new Fellow.</p> 
       <p>“Diane and I have felt such a warm welcome from the UCR community, including from our fantastic students, and we noted that about 60 percent of undergraduates are first in their families to seek a degree,” said Wilcox. “Diane and I want to make sure that we leave a legacy here, and that all of our undergraduate students have access to research opportunities that can transform their personal and professional aspirations.”</p> 
       <p>Chancellor Wilcox himself was among the first in his family to earn a college degree. He grew up in Northern Michigan, and, after undergraduate years at Michigan State University, earned master’s and doctoral degrees from Purdue University in speech and hearing science. Wilcox has held academic leadership positions at Michigan State University, the University of Kansas, the Kansas Board of Regents and the University of Missouri. He became UCR’s ninth chancellor in August, 2013.</p> 
       <p>His wife, Diane Del Buono, earned a master’s degree from Purdue University and a law degree from the University of Kansas, where she also served as director of financial aid.</p> 
       <p>“Kim Wilcox and Diane Del Buono have a deep and personal understanding of the transformative effect on students of collaborative contact with faculty members,” said UCR Foundation Chair S. Sue Johnson. “On behalf of the UCR Foundation, I am grateful to them not only for their leadership, but for this gift that will create an enduring impact on campus for generations to come.”</p>
