    <h2 align="center">Center Mission </h2> 
    <p align="left" class="style1">Whether it's a strip mall 15 miles from downtown Paris or ranch-style houses creeping down the freeway south of Manila, the suburb has become the dominant form of urban growth around the world.</p> 
    <p align="left" class="style1">The Center for Sustainable Suburban Development at the University of California Riverside was established in 2003 to provide research and analysis with a policy focus on the wide range of issues the suburbs confront.</p> 
    <p align="left" class="style1"> The Center is rooted in its locale, for Riverside and San Bernardino counties offer an unrivaled laboratory for studying the issues related to suburban growth. Inland Southern California is the great release valve for the expanding economies of Los Angeles and Orange counties. It is projected to absorb an additional 3.7 million people by 2030, more than a 110% increase. If it is not planned well, it could easily be a mess. </p> 
    <p align="left" class="style1"> The challenge is not merely population growth, but how the region provides quality jobs, develops the infrastructure to get these people to work in a timely manner, give them the opportunity to live with clean air and water, quality schools, adequate parks and all the other factors housed under the label, &quot;quality of life.&quot; </p> 
    <p align="left" class="style1">Equally challenging is that all this growth will take place in one of the most biologically diverse regions in the United States, in an area already lapping at the limits of its water resources, with remarkable ethnic and racial diversity and significant differences in economic status and education levels.</p> 
    <p align="left" class="style1"> These concerns are not merely those of two counties in Southern California. The Center will study its backyard not merely for its own sake, but because the lessons learned here can be exported. And, because the Center will draw on suburban experts elsewhere, it will recognize that there are lessons to be learned elsewhere and imported.</p> 
    <p align="left" class="style1"> Of necessity, the Center's work will be multi-disciplinary. The suburbs, as human artifacts, touch on all aspects of human experience - from interactions with species, through the planning of communities that meet all human needs, to the questions of how to govern and finance those communities.</p> 
    <p align="left" class="style1"> The Center's name was carefully chosen; the burgeoning suburbs must be sustainable. There is no point in creating communities which will die on the vine. They must be sustainable not only in making sensible use of resources, but because they must continue to meet human needs for housing, jobs, shopping and recreational activities.</p> 
    <p align="left" class="style1"> But sustainability is not a code word for stopping growth. Whether in the Center's neighboring communities, or on the fringes of cities around the world, it is clear that suburban development will take place. The challenge is to plan and direct that development to make the communities it creates livable now and sustainable for the future.</p>

       <p>Six UC Riverside students will be honored at the Celebration for a Day of Appreciation and Recognition of Women Students on Thursday, March 6, 2014.       <p>RIVERSIDE, Calif. — Six University of California, Riverside students will receive awards for efforts in leadership and civic engagement, social justice and overcoming adversity at the fourth annual&nbsp;Celebration for a Day of Appreciation and Recognition of Women Students on Thursday, March 6, 2014.</p> 
      <p>The event, which is free and open to the public, is organized by the UCR Women’s Resource Center and sponsored by campus and community partners and is part of the campus’ annual celebration of Women’s History Month. It will take place from 6 to 8 p.m. in HUB 302S.<a href="https://highlanderlink.ucr.edu/form/step/1?Guid=15ae91af-67d8-467d-bdd5-5be08b3df562"> An RSVP is requested.</a></p> 
      <p>“This annual celebration forwards the university’s strategic plan for diversity and former Chancellor Tim White’s Proclamation for A Day of Appreciation and Recognition of Women Students,” said Women’s Resource Center Director Adrienne Sims.</p> 
      <div id="attachment_20125" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 277px">
       <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/20102/eloise" rel="attachment wp-att-20125"><img class="size-full wp-image-20125" alt="Eloise Reyes Gomez" src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/eloise.jpg" width="277" height="339" /></a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">Eloise Gomez Reyes</p>
      </div> 
      <p>The keynote speaker is Eloise Gomez Reyes, a long-time Inland Empire resident who is <a href="http://www.EloiseForCongress.com" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.EloiseForCongress.com']);">running for the congressional seat in the 31<sup>st</sup> district.</a></p> 
      <p>A native of Colton, she worked in the onion fields at the age of 12 with mother and five siblings to earn money for school clothes. She attended San Bernardino Valley College, then graduated from USC. She later earned her J.D. from Loyola Law School and began her legal career representing injured workers and unions in workers comp cases. She later moved back to Colton and started her own law office, which she has run for more than two decades. She is a founding Board member of the Inland Empire Community Health Center in Bloomington, is on the Executive Board for the Children’s Spine Foundation, and served on the Dean’s Medical School Mission Committee here at UC Riverside.</p> 
      <p>Sims said she is looking forward to hearing Reyes speak.</p> 
      <p>“Eloise Gomez Reyes, is not only a local – she was born and raised in Colton, but she’s a frontrunner for the IE – the first Latina to start to her own law practice here.&nbsp; Her experiences give a strong voice to women’s issues,” Sims said.</p> 
      <h2>Information on the awards and the recipients</h2> 
      <h3>Leadership and Civic Engagement Award</h3> 
      <p>The award is presented to an undergraduate and graduate student who devotes extra-ordinary time and effort towards leadership and civic engagement activities.<br /> </p>
      <div id="attachment_20089" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 130px">
       <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/20102/katherine-head-shot" rel="attachment wp-att-20089"><img class="size-thumbnail wp-image-20089" alt="Katherine Tsai" src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Katherine-Head-shot-e1390864012769-130x78.jpg" width="130" height="78" /></a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">Katherine Tsai</p>
      </div>
      <p></p> 
      <h4>Undergraduate Student Honoree:&nbsp; Katherine Tsai, Biology</h4> 
      <p>Katherine is an exemplary academic who is majoring in biology.&nbsp; In addition to forwarding academic excellence, she devotes countless hours to helping others.&nbsp; Her drive to improve humankind pushes her to optimize leadership roles in which she can make positive impacts on fellow students and other communities.<br /><br /><br /><br /><br /></p> 
      <div id="attachment_20091" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 130px">
       <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/20102/vickie-head-shot" rel="attachment wp-att-20091"><img class="size-thumbnail wp-image-20091" alt="Vickie Vertiz" src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Vickie-Head-shot-e1390864542888-130x78.jpg" width="130" height="78" /></a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">Vickie Vertiz</p>
      </div> 
      <h4>Graduate Student Honoree:&nbsp; Vickie Vertiz, Creative Writing</h4> 
      <p>A self-identified Xicana with a background in community activism and advocacy, Vickie, through academic work, volunteerism and community service, demonstrates a commitment to bettering all our communities.&nbsp; As a leader, her goal is to rectify power imbalances.<br /><br /><br /><br /><br /></p> 
      <h3 class="section3">Social Justice Award</h3> 
      <p>The award is presented to an undergraduate and graduate student who works to create a society based on principles of equality and solidarity.<br /> </p>
      <div id="attachment_20093" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 130px">
       <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/20102/gabriela-head-shot" rel="attachment wp-att-20093"><img class="size-thumbnail wp-image-20093" alt="Gabriela Bobadilla" src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Gabriela-Head-shot-e1390864677664-130x78.jpg" width="130" height="78" /></a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">Gabriela Bobadilla</p>
      </div>
      <p></p> 
      <h4>Undergraduate Student Honoree:&nbsp; Gabriela Bobadilla, Spanish</h4> 
      <p>Gabriela’s work with the anti-human trafficking task force and her commitment to social justice issues in Riverside’s local community are unparalleled.&nbsp;Her work with Operation SafeHouse and the Child Leaders Project in Riverside show her hard work and dedication to key causes.<br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /></p> 
      <div id="attachment_20090" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 130px">
       <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/20102/remi-head-shot" rel="attachment wp-att-20090"><img class="size-thumbnail wp-image-20090" alt="Remie Rahman" src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Remi-Head-Shot-e1390863892171-130x78.jpg" width="130" height="78" /></a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">Remie Rahman</p>
      </div> 
      <h4>Graduate Student Honoree:&nbsp; Remie Rahman, Education</h4> 
      <p>Remie is among the first to volunteer for and respond to social justice platforms.&nbsp; What stands out is her tireless contribution to social issues organizations and programs, like the R.E.A.C.H. Peer Educators, Project LEAD, and the recent Student of Color Conference.<br /><br /><br /><br /><br /></p> 
      <h3 class="section3">Overcoming Adversity Award</h3> 
      <p>The award is presented to an undergraduate and graduate student who withstands a misfortune, setback, or debilitating circumstance.</p> 
      <div id="attachment_20094" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 130px">
       <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/20102/jackie-head-shot" rel="attachment wp-att-20094"><img class="size-thumbnail wp-image-20094" alt="Jacklyn Kozich" src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Jackie-Head-shot-e1390864761782-130x78.jpg" width="130" height="78" /></a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">Jacklyn Kozich</p>
      </div> 
      <h4>Undergraduate Student Honoree:&nbsp; Jacklyn Kozich, Psychology</h4> 
      <p>Jacklyn has overcome what seemed to be insurmountable bouts of adversity, including family tragedies and her own blunt trauma and disability. A Navy veteran, she served the USS Boxer, an amphibous assault carrier that participated in the rescue Captain Richard Phillips of the MV Maersk Alabama from Somali pirates. That rescue is the subject of the recent blockbuster film “Captain Phillips.”<br /><br /><br /><br /></p> 
      <div id="attachment_20092" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 130px">
       <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/20102/divya-head-shot" rel="attachment wp-att-20092"><img class="size-thumbnail wp-image-20092" alt="Divya Sain" src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Divya-Head-shot-e1390864635303-130x78.jpg" width="130" height="78" /></a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">Divya Sain</p>
      </div> 
      <h4>Graduate Student Honoree:&nbsp; Divya Sain, Genetics, Genomics, and Bioinformatics</h4> 
      <p>Despite medical challenges, including limited mobility, Divya is an outstanding student who recently receivied the Guru Gobind Singh Graduate Fellowship, which is only awarded to one UC student per year. She will complete her Ph.D. in December.<br /><br /><br /><br /><br /></p> 
      <div id="attachment_20649" class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 366px">
       <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/wrc.png"><img class="size-large wp-image-20649" alt="A flyer for the WRC event." src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/wrc-366x585.png" width="366" height="585" /></a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">A flyer for the WRC event.</p>
      </div>

     The University of California, Riverside (
    <a href="http://www.ucr.edu/">www.ucr.edu</a>) is a doctoral research university, a living laboratory for groundbreaking exploration of issues critical to Inland Southern California, the state and communities around the world.      <p><span style="color: #22456f; font-size: 18px; line-height: 24px;">A Great History, A Bright Future</span></p> 
     <p>The business programs at the University of California, Riverside have had many names since their humble beginnings in the early 1970’s: The Graduate School of Administration, the Graduate School of Management in 1982, the A. Gary Anderson Graduate School of Management in 1994, and finally, the formation of the School of Business Administration in 2009.</p> 
     <p>Regardless of the name, the cause has been the same — taking advantage of the great research-based culture of the University of California and using it to develop executives, entrepreneurs and scholars who are as diverse as the challenges they face, the workforces they lead and the enterprises they grow. As one of the three business schools within the University of California, the UCR School of Business Administration is devoted to taking our diverse student body and molding them to address the need for leadership within both the local and global economies. </p>

      <h3><strong>Alumni —</strong> Share Your Pride</h3> 
      <p>Ray Shirazi is a principal partner at Cadwalader, Wickersham and Taft LLP, the oldest law firm on Wall Street. He would occasionally work with Rob Fraley, who is managing director of one of the multi-billion dollar hedge funds at Fortress, another Wall Street firm.</p> 
      <p>But in all their years of working together, they never realized the greatest thing they had in common: They were both alumni of UC Riverside. Shirazi got his degree in economics in 1984 before going on to UCLA Law School, while Fraley earned a BA in business administration from UCR in 1992 before going on to New York University for a JD/MBA. It was former dean David Stewart who made the connection between the two businessmen during a trip to the city.</p> 
      <p>Our alumni are critical to the future success of the School of Business Administration and the university as a whole, and we encourage you to show your &quot;Highlander Pride&quot; by joining the UCR Alumni Association and by staying involved with the activities at your alma mater.</p>
