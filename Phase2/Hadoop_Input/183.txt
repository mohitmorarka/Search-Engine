      <h3>AGSM's Business Ph.D.</h3> 
      <p>Providing business students with another avenue for pursuing their business education, the Ph.D. program in business administration&nbsp;combines UCR's tradition as a major research university with AGSM's strengths of leadership, innovation, and entrepreneurship in Inland Southern California, a living laboratory that explores the meaning of growth in today's complex marketplace.</p> 
      <h4 class="section">About the Program</h4> 
      <p>The Ph.D. program at UCR fills a growing demand for doctoral students in management in the UC system.&nbsp;It is an academic program that provides candidates with the chance to do intensive research in the field of business.&nbsp;It is built upon an interdisciplinary theme with collaborations from other departments at UCR, including economics, sociology, psychology and statistics.</p> 
      <p class="p1">The Ph.D. program trains graduate students in the design and execution&nbsp;<span style="font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em;">of original research in management, specifically in the major fields of&nbsp;</span><span style="font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em;">(1) Marketing and (2) Strategic Management and Organizations.</span></p> 
      <p class="p2"><em><strong><span style="font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em;">For fall 2014 we are recruiting only applicants who are interested&nbsp;</span><span style="font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em;">in Strategic Management and Organizations (not Marketing).</span></strong></em></p> 
      <h4 class="section">Financial Aid</h4> 
      <p class="p1">UCR offers a variety of <a href="http://graduate.ucr.edu/fin_aid.html" target="_blank"><span class="s1">multi-year fellowship packages</span></a> for incoming students that may include stipends, full or partial payment of tuition and fees, and appointment as a Teaching Assistant (TA) or Graduate Student Researcher (GSR). Ph.D. applicants must complete the Supplemental Fellowship Form included as an optional form in the UCR Graduate Application for Admission. This form will be reviewed as part of your submitted graduate application.</p>

      <h3>Shaping the Future of the Emissions in Our Global Environment</h3> 
      <p></p> 
      <div class="two-thirds"> 
       <hr /> 
       <p>The world is changing quickly in an age of rapid information and networking. In-use testing provides new information not available from the laboratory and is revolutionizing how emissions data is utilized. Portable Emissions Measurement Systems (PEMS) are being used to answer critical questions in the areas of modeling, policy, and fuel economy that come from manufacturers, fleets, regulators and the public. Additionally, Portable Activity Measurement Systems (PAMS) are gaining interest to help increase our understanding of these in-use emissions and fuel efficiency. As PEMS/PAMS continue to shape our future and guide us down different paths, users need to better understand these tools and their strengths and weaknesses. As those applications change, so must the tools, and their implementation. For example, current 1065 compliant PEMS are accurate, but large. Other PEMS/PAMS are smaller or lighter, but can be less accurate.</p> 
       <p>In this workshop you will learn from experts in the industry about understanding the newest available PEMS/PAMS tools, how to use those tools, and what future tools are&nbsp; needed, based on various emerging markets.</p> 
       <p>The European Union recently has adopted the European Real Driving Emissions requirements for light duty vehicles. This requirement depends solely on the collection of high quality PEMS data from the in-use fleet. US heavy duty engine manufacturers must also verify their engines are within legal not-to-exceed limits utilizing PEMS. Agencies, large fleets, and researchers must account for the emissions impact of aftertreatment systems, hybrids, alternative fuels, and many other emissions control approaches that are being implemented. However, these elements are hard to characterize without large datasets of information, thus, necessitating smaller, low cost PEMS or PAMS. Both types of PEMS are needed, but how we get there? In-use particle number is also an area of growth and needs to be investigated further.</p> 
       <p>This year’s conference is expanding to a two-day event at the recommendation of last year’s participants. The agenda will focus on lessons learned both domestically and internationally, advanced vehicle in-use testing (hybrids, all electrics), education, applications, emerging tools, micro PEMS/sensors, PAMS benefits, new measurements (particle number), future needs, new tools and procedures, and many other topics.</p> 
       <p><em>Those who should attend are:</em></p> 
       <ul> 
        <li>Regulators\Agencies\Port Authorities</li> 
        <li>Test equipment manufacturers&nbsp;</li> 
        <li>Modeling researchers</li> 
        <li>Engine and vehicle manufacturers</li> 
        <li>In-use testing engineers</li> 
        <li>Scientists</li> 
       </ul> 
       <p>The PEMS conference is in its 4<sup>th</sup> year and has consistently been attended by more than 150 people from over seven nations representing more than 55 different institutions, agencies, and industries. We look forward to seeing you at this year’s PEMS 2014 workshop.</p> 
       <p>Sincerely,</p> 
       <p>Kent Johnson</p> 
       <p><em>PEMS/PAMS user, developer, and conference coordinator</em></p>
