      <h2><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/post/79178681035/spotlight-campus-apartment-resident-association">Spotlight: Campus Apartment Resident Association</a></h2> 
      <div class="metadata"> 
       <span class="metalink date">March 10, 2014</span> 
       <span class="metalink note"><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/post/79178681035/spotlight-campus-apartment-resident-association#notes" title="Notes">3 notes</a></span> 
       <a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/post/79178681035/spotlight-campus-apartment-resident-association" class="metalink permalink"> Full Story</a> 
       <span class="metalink tags"><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/cara" class="taglink"> cara</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/campus-apartment-resident-association" class="taglink"> campus apartment resident association</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/get-involved" class="taglink"> get involved</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/rubi" class="taglink"> rubi</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/spotlight" class="taglink"> spotlight</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/ucr" class="taglink"> ucr</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/uc-riverside" class="taglink"> uc riverside</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/campus-apartments" class="taglink"> campus apartments</a></span> 
       <div class="clear"></div> 
      </div> 
      <p><span><img src="https://31.media.tumblr.com/51b6f192e02bb1aae7d456241c109e86/tumblr_inline_n28eqeer8H1r08h92.jpg" /></span></p> 
      <p><a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/UCR-Campus-Apartment-Resident-Association/210886378984840" target="_blank"><em>Source.</em></a></p> 
      <p id="docs-internal-guid-627115c8-ad12-73cd-2a17-e0f9750ba903"><span>I started my time at UC Riverside four years ago, knowing absolutely no one and nothing about the area. Before I even moved into my freshman dorm room I decided I needed to join an organization on campus, seeing as it was how I made some of my good friends in high school. With so many options it was difficult to choose, but I decided to literally stay close to home and become part of the <a href="http://rhaucr.wordpress.com/" target="_blank">Residence Hall Association </a>for Aberdeen-Inverness. </span></p> 
      <p><span>The Residence Hall Association was sort of like the student government of the dorm residences. They filled orders for repairs, planned events and generally made living in the dorm halls more enjoyable. RHA is limited to those who live in campus dorms, though, so after my freshmen year I had no choice but to leave the organization. My second year passed by in a blur and it wasn’t until my third year that I heard about the <a href="http://housing.ucr.edu/residential-life/resident-associations.aspx" target="_blank">Campus Apartment Resident Association</a>, the sister org to RHA. Doing what RHA does but to a for a larger amount of people, CARA offered me another opportunity to improve the community I was living in, plan events and meet people with the same interests as mine. </span></p>

     <div class="textpost entry"> 
      <div class="metadata"> 
       <span class="metalink date">March 10, 2014</span> 
       <a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/post/79173548759/so-you-said-its-only-for-medical-necessity-so-then" class="metalink permalink"> Full Story</a> 
       <span class="metalink tags"><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/answers" class="taglink"> answers</a></span> 
       <div class="clear"></div> 
      </div> 
      <p><b>Anonymous asked: So you said it's only for medical necessity so then that means it's less likely for someone who doesn't have a medical necessity to get their single room. I'm just scared of getting a roommate because I'm not good at talking &amp; I fear sleeping with strangers in a room</b></p> 
      <p>I totally understand your concern because living with someone new, on top of moving to an unfamiliar place, is all sorts of weird and unfamiliar and just a little terrifying. What you’re feeling is totally normal, and a lot of other people you’ll meet in the residence halls feel the exact same way. It’s okay to feel scared — I really feel like 90% of my college experience has involved feeling afraid of one thing or another, and the other 10% is free food — but I really encourage you to look at this as an opportunity to get to know your very first person in a college setting. If your roommate releases his or her information before move-in day, you’ll have an opportunity to speak ahead of time via Facebook or email.</p> 
      <p>Some easy topics to get the conversation started and establish your relationship as people sharing a living space: Where are you from? Do you like it there? Have you shared a room before? Are you going to be okay if I have to wake up early? Do you mind if I keep a light on and study late? Can you give me a heads-up if you bring friends over? Do you mind if I bring friends over? (Ask this one before you actually bring them over, not when they’re right outside the door.) If you ever want to borrow something, can you please ask and let me know? Here’s my phone number in case you need to reach me — can I please have yours, too? I know it sounds kind of silly to point out, but these things are not okay: (x, y, z. Wearing my clothes without asking, eating my food without asking, going through my things, not respecting my space). I want to respect your space, too — is there anything you want me to know?</p> 
      <p>I know I’ve mentioned this before, but your RA (Resident Assistant) is going to be an invaluable resource to help you work through your concerns and talk to someone in a safe and welcoming environment. Your RA is literally there to help you feel better transitioning into dorm life, and if you ever feel like you don’t know how to talk to your roommate or feel uncomfortable about living with a stranger, your RA can help you get to know your roommate — and the other people in your hall — so maybe you won’t feel like you’re in a hall with strangers, but are instead supported by friends.</p> 
      <p>If you don’t feel like you can do this, I do recommend contacting Housing and speaking with someone about the requirements for a single room. Share your concerns and indicate your interest and reasoning for being placed in a single. You can always email them at housinginfo@ucr.edu.</p> 
      <p>But honestly, if you do end up sharing a space with someone, just consider their needs and how those needs relate to yours. Of course you might need to study late — so just keep your desk light on, and let your roommate know you need to work on things. Of course you might have to wake up early to catch a class while your roommate gets to sleep in late — let him or her know so they can expect to hear your alarm. You might not even totally click with your roommate. That’s okay. Not everyone becomes uber-best-friends. That’s okay. You’re a team in this room, not enemies, and not necessarily strangers. You’re working toward the common goal of sharing your room and making it feel like a miniature home. If you communicate your needs, and bring your concerns to your RA as they come up, you’ll have all the resources you need to make this a truly stellar year.</p> 
      <p>Good luck!</p>

       <span class="metalink date">March 04, 2014</span> 
       <span class="metalink note"><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/post/78595037986/why-i-chose-ucr#notes" title="Notes">16 notes</a></span>

       <span class="metalink tags"><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/jessica" class="taglink"> jessica</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/why-i-chose-ucr" class="taglink"> why i chose ucr</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/why-ucr" class="taglink"> why ucr</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/ucr" class="taglink"> ucr</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/uc-riverside" class="taglink"> uc riverside</a><a href="http://lifeatucr.tumblr.com/tagged/spotlight" class="taglink"> spotlight</a></span>

      <p><img alt="image" src="https://31.media.tumblr.com/de4beb3462ab8eadeedde808895171c3/tumblr_inline_n1xsrgLhNS1r08h92.png" /></p> 
      <p><a href="http://instagram.com/lifeatucr" target="_blank"><em>Source.</em></a></p> 
      <p><span>“Why did you choose UC Riverside?” isn’t a question I get asked often enough, and when I am asked, I don’t say nearly enough. I was a high school senior in northern California three years ago, and at my high school, it’s a huge trend to move far away from home. A lot of my friends were getting into schools like Cal Poly SLO, UC Irvine, UC Berkeley, UCLA, UCSD, MIT, Johns Hopkins … I think you get the picture.</span></p> 
      <p>But before we get to the part where I actually decided where to go to college, let’s talk about my college selection process. I didn’t apply out of state. I didn’t want to put my parents under the financial burden of paying out-of-state tuition, especially since I was a pretty average student and not exactly the most active. All of the schools I applied to were in California, and the majority of them were UCs. I knew my chances weren’t bleak, but I also knew they weren’t amazing.</p>

      <p>Our kiosks sell parking&nbsp;permits for the current day only. If you need to purchase multiple day parking permits or parking permits in advance, please call or visit&nbsp;Parking Services.&nbsp; Parking Services is located on campus at 683 Linden St.&nbsp; Phone:&nbsp; 951-827-1294</p> 
      <p><strong>Our kiosks accept cash, check, Visa and Mastercard</strong>.</p> 
      <p>&nbsp;<strong>Campus Kiosk (located&nbsp;on University Ave. and West Campus Dr.)&nbsp;Hours&nbsp;are</strong>:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <ul> 
        <li>Monday - Saturday: 7:00&nbsp;a.m. to 8:00 p.m.</li> 
        <li>Sunday:&nbsp;CLOSED</li> 
       </ul> 
      </ul> 
      <p><strong>Canyon Crest Kiosk (located on Martin Luther King Blvd. and Canyon Crest Dr.) Hours are:</strong></p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>Monday - Friday: 7:00 a.m. to 2:30 p.m.</li> 
       <li>Saturday - Sunday: CLOSED</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p><strong>AFTER&nbsp;HOURS PERMITS</strong></p> 
      <p>For your convienence a Permit Dispenser is available and located at the Campus Kiosk.</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>&nbsp;After hour permits cover evening and weekend parking and cost $5.00.</li> 
       <li>This Permit Dispenser accepts Mastercard, Visa and Discover&nbsp;payment transactions.</li> 
       <li>Permits are&nbsp;valid in parking spaces and lots throughout the campus that are not otherwise posted as reserved, pay-by-space, disabled,&nbsp;or in&nbsp;any Housing parking lot.</li> 
      </ul>

     <div class="clear section"></div> RIVERSIDE, Calif. (www.ucr.edu) – Stopping the tangled Web of deceit is the end result of a new company launched by UCR student Anirban Banerjee, who received his Ph.D. last year, and Michalis Faloutsos, professor of computer science and engineering at UCR’s Bourns College of Engineering. The collaboration resulted in a Web site protection service called StopTheHacker.com 
     <br /> 
     <br /> “Everyone who owns or manages a Web site should be aware of the dangers and have an active protection system,” said Faloutsos. “Unfortunately, very few people are aware of this. Most sites harbor at least one major vulnerability and more than 80 percent have had a critical security flaw. ”
     <br /> 
     <br /> What started as an idea from a doctoral student grew into a successful collaboration. However, Faloutsos first tried to persuade his student to shift his focus to other areas because this project was not connected to Banerjee’s Ph.D. research. His Ph.D. research focused on understanding how emerging technologies such as peer to peer (P2P) file sharing and Podcasts are affecting the Internet.
     <br /> 
     <br /> “I am glad he did not listen to me,” Faloutsos said. 
     <br /> 
     <br /> With Faloutsos onboard, Banerjee was able to secure a $100,000 National Science Foundation Small Business Innovative Research (SBIR) grant titled: &quot;Stopping URL-fraud One Web Site at a Time.” 
     <br /> 
     <br /> Although hard at work on his Ph.D., Banerjee admitted that he was fascinated by Web security and had been involved with it for the past few years.
     <br /> 
     <br /> “It is a constantly morphing arena,” he said. “There is something new to learn every day.”
     <br /> 
     <br /> The work is rewarding, too.
     <br /> 
     <br /> “I feel that my work with Jaal LLC (the holding company for StopTheHacker.com.) helps people in a direct way,” Banarjee said. “We help customers get their Web sites back on their feet. It’s a great feeling to get thank you e-mails.”
     <br /> 
     <br /> The service complements the other security functions of anti-virus, anti-spam, and firewall protection, thus making it more comprehensive. 
     <br /> 
     <br /> “The hacker doesn’t want to hurt the site when they use code injection, they want it to continue to function so everyone will still go there,” Faloutsos said. “In the meantime, a search engine like Google comes along and checks the Web site and they blacklist it if they see a problem.” 
     <br /> 
     <br /> Code injection is an emerging cyber-crime proliferation mechanism that introduces malicious code or hyperlinks in compromised, but legitimate Web sites. The hackers hurt the visitors of the Web site, who are infected by malware or viruses, or fall victim to identity theft. 
     <br /> 
     <br /> Regardless of Web site size or the size of the company, the number of attacks is increasing at an alarming rate. Faloutsos said the Web site infection rate in 2008 is three times that of 2007. 
     <br /> 
     <br /> “It’s not size that attracts would-be hackers, but traffic,” Faloutsos said. “If your Web site has nice interactive functions, like asking the user’s name or other questions, hackers can enter some characters that force the site to crash. Then the hacker can take control of the system.”
     <br /> 
     <br /> Faloutsos said that StopTheHacker.com’s advantage is that it provides services that are non-intrusive because they are offered on Software as a Service (SaaS) basis, and they are run outside the firewall. Users don’t have to install software or change anything from what is already being done by the Web site host. It fortifies the site before, during, and after an intrusion. It assesses a site’s vulnerability, detects security breaches and ensures the recovery of a compromised site. Each site is checked twice daily for 45 minutes. 
     <br /> 
     <br /> So far, the response has been favorable, Faloutsos said.
     <br /> 
     <br /> “We got tremendous help, advice and encouragement from local companies and entrepreneurs,” Faloutsos said, “We already have paying customers and expect to have 10 to 20 Web sites under our protection this summer.”
