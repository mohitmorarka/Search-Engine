     <h2 class="intro">Walk Backward. Lead Forward.</h2> 
     <h3 class="verd intro">Discover what it takes to get the<br /> &quot;happiest job on campus.&quot;</h3> 
     <p style="text-align: center;"> <img alt="" width="692" height="305" src="/Images/header_beCampTourGuide.jpg" /> </p> 
     <div id="featured"> 
      <div class="content" style=""> 
       <h4>&quot;I love being a campus tour guide! I enjoy engaging with prospective students and the rewarding feeling I get knowing that I positively influenced their decision to attend UCR.&quot; <span>- Michael Corpuz</span> </h4> 
      </div> 
      <div class="content" style=""> 
       <h4>&quot;Being a tour guide is a great job to have. I love meeting and interacting with new people. To me, it definitely beats sitting behind a desk. I can converse with prospective students from all over and grow overall as a presenter, leader and speaker.&quot; <span>- Noelle Espero</span> </h4> 
      </div> 
      <div class="content" style=""> 
       <h4>&quot;At the Campus Tours Office, we have a welcoming and loving staff. We provide an individual with a better understanding about the world through interaction with others.&quot; <span>- Gabriel Hernandez </span> </h4> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p><strong>Being a campus tour guide is more than</strong> just giving tours. Youre the face of UCR to prospective students, their families and important community members. Tours have a huge impact on college decision-making, which means you have a huge impact on everyone that comes through our doors. If youre excited about sharing your college experience and have a passion for changing lives, we encourage you to apply! </p> 
     <h3>We Look for Students Who:</h3> 
     <div class="genericBody version2 verd"> 
      <ul> 
       <li>Are articulate, professional and responsible.</li> 
       <li>Share a strong enthusiasm for UCR.</li> 
       <li>Are positive and outgoing.</li> 
       <li>Work well with individuals and groups in a public environment.</li> 
       <li>Know our campus, its events and activities well. </li> 
       <li>Know UC admissions requirements well.</li> 
       <li>Can address all questions optimistically.</li> 
       <li>Reflect the diversity of our student population.</li> 
       <li>Are active in the Highlander community.</li> 
       <li>Speak fluent English. </li> 
      </ul> 
     </div> 
     <p>For the benefit of our visitors, when we hire first-year students, we only do so in their second quarter. We hire very few seniors because training is so time intensive and their focus tends to be degree completion. Work-study applicants are welcome.</p> 
     <h3>As a Campus Tour Guide, You:</h3> 
     <div class="genericBody version2 verd"> 
      <ul> 
       <li>Guide memorable walking tours for individuals, families and large groups. </li> 
       <li>Provide a positive visit experience to all guests.</li> 
       <li>Staff the front desk.</li> 
       <li>Assist in Undergraduate Admissions projects.</li> 
       <li>Perform data entry and office support as needed.</li> 
       <li>Participate in training.</li> 
       <li>Work a minimum of 12 hours and a maximum of 19 hours per week.</li> 
       <li>Change lives!</li> 
      </ul> 
     </div> 
     <h3>Apply Now </h3> 
     <p>We hire about once a quarter. Heres how to apply:</p> 
     <div class="genericBody version2 verd"> 
      <ol> 
       <li><a href="https://auth.ucr.edu/cas/login?service=https%3A%2F%2Fucr-csm.symplicity.com%2Fsso%2Fstudents%2Flogin">Complete an online application in SCOTlink.</a></li> 
       <li>If selected, you will participate in a group audition.</li> 
       <li>If selected from the audition, you will participate in an individual interview. <br /> <br /> <strong>Before the interview, in SCOTlink, please:</strong> 
        <ul style="margin-top: 9px;"> 
         <li>Print a Federal Work-Study Placement Form.</li> 
         <li>Print the job description and attach it to the Placement Form.</li> 
         <li>Take both to the interview.</li> 
        </ul> </li> 
      </ol> 
     </div> 
     <p>Newly hired guides are trained by seasoned mentors on all aspects of guiding tours, including tour presentation, customization, campus facts and customer service. New guides will not lead a tour until deemed ready.</p>

He is applying to several law schools and we pray he gets in for the fall, but again it may be put on hold due to the court systems slowness. Anyways, the only job he has pays very little. I pay for his health insurance, but cannot afford his car payment and car insurance also. So, he has to get rid of his car. There is so much more to his story. The community loved him and just knew he would go far. He is so disappointed.I hope he can make his dreams begin again within this next year. A great young man should not be held down like this. I retire in another year and a half from teaching. His father and I are divorced and I am our son's only means of financial support. It has made me struggle somewhat, but I will do everything I have to to help him get back on his feet. I know you do much for so many, any help would be appreciated. Your personality is so kind and I love to watch you dance on your show and hear your jokes. <br /> Sincerely,<br /> Marsha Henry <br /> 115 S.       <h3>Course Proposals: A Team Effort </h3> 
      <p>Course proposals are how academic departments suggest creating a new course, changing an existing course, or bringing an old course back to life. </p> 
      <p>When faculty initiate a proposal, a designated preparer enters it into a program called the Course Request and Maintenance System, or CRAMS. </p> 
      <p>The proposal is then routed through an approval process in which the shared system allows for multiple approvers to provide feedback. Ultimate approval is granted by the <a href="http://senate.ucr.edu/committee/?do=info&amp;id=8">Committee on Courses.</a></p> 
      <p>See a detailed overview of <a href="/CRAMS/course-proposal/default.aspx">how course proposals work.</a></p> 
      <p style="margin-bottom: 14px; margin-top: 27px;"> <a href="https://cramsportal.ucr.edu/crams/courses.search" target="_blank" class="ctaButton" style="width: 150px; display: block; margin: 0 auto;">Search for a Course</a> </p>
