     <h2 class="intro"> Ways to Pay </h2> 
     <h3 class="verd intro"> You can pay for school, and do it wisely. Learn how to find funds, <br /> get grants, earn scholarships and more.</h3> 
     <h4 style="text-align: center; width: 70%; margin-bottom: 48px; font-size: 1.2em;
    width: 100%" class="grey verd"> FAFSA - <a href="http://www.fafsa.ed.gov/">Apply for Most Aid With One Application</a><a href="#"></a></h4> 
     <div class="iconSections version3 clearfix"> 
      <div class="grid_6 alpha"> 
       <div> 
        <img src="/Images/icons/handShakeIcon.png" alt="" /> 
        <h3> Blue and Gold <br /> Opportunity Plan</h3> 
        <h4> Attend UCR for Free </h4> 
        <p> <a href="http://www.universityofcalifornia.edu/blueandgold/">The Blue and Gold Opportunity Plan </a>covers (all!) tuition and student services fees for qualified California residents whose families earn less than $80,000 a year. If you already have partial aid, Blue and Gold can help make up the difference. Some students can even get help with books, housing and transportation.</p> 
        <ul class="flushleft noUnderline"> 
         <li><a href="http://www.universityofcalifornia.edu/admissions/paying-for-uc/financial-aid/grants/blue-gold/index.html" class="rightArrowCTA">Learn more about the Blue and Gold plan and see if you qualify.</a> </li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <div> 
        <img src="/Images/icons/morterBoardIcon.png" alt="" /> 
        <h3> <span>Scholarships</span></h3> 
        <h4> Earn a Scholarship </h4> 
        <p> Scholarships abound! They can be awarded for everything from special talents and leadership ability to showing promise in your future career. UCR offers many. There’s a high chance you will find one right for you. </p> 
        <ul class="flushleft noUnderline"> 
         <li><a href="http://scholarships.ucr.edu/" class="rightArrowCTA">Search UCR scholarships and fellowships.</a></li> 
         <li><a href="http://scholarshiplist.ucr.edu/" class="rightArrowCTA">Discover scholarships from outside agencies.</a></li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <div> 
        <img src="/Images/icons/refreshIcon.png" alt="" /> 
        <h3> <span>Pay in Installments</span> </h3> 
        <h4> Deferred Payment Plan</h4> 
        <p> Pay your tuition and fees in monthly installments with our Deferred Payment Plan (DPP). Another great way to relieve financial stress and focus on what matters – like your finals! </p> 
        <ul class="flushleft noUnderline"> 
         <li><a href="http://www.sbs.ucr.edu/" class="rightArrowCTA">Contact Student Business Services for details.</a></li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
      <div class="grid_6 omega"> 
       <div> 
        <img src="/Images/icons/wadOCashIcon.png" alt="" /> 
        <h3> <span>Grants</span></h3> 
        <h4> Get a Grant</h4> 
        <p> Grants are funds you don’t have to pay back! By submitting a <a href="http://www.fafsa.ed.gov/"> Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)</a>, you are automatically applying for many grants including UCR grants, Federal Pell Grants, Cal Grants (with a <a href="http://www.csac.ca.gov/doc.asp?id=1177">verified GPA form</a>) and more. Research grants offered by departments or outside agencies for additional free funding. </p> 
        <ul class="flushleft noUnderline"> 
         <li><a href="http://www.fafsa.ed.gov/" class="rightArrowCTA">Learn more about FAFSA.</a> </li> 
         <li><a href="http://www.calgrants.org/index.cfm?navId=22" class="rightArrowCTA">Learn how to apply for a Cal Grant.</a></li> 
         <li><a href="http://www.ed.gov/fund/grants-apply.html" class="rightArrowCTA">Learn more about federal grants.</a></li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <div> 
        <img src="/Images/icons/employeeIcon.png" alt="" /> 
        <h3> <span>Work at UCR</span> </h3> 
        <h4> Work Study </h4> 
        <p> Select from on-campus jobs, earn a paycheck, gain experience and establish a schedule that works well with your studies. In 2011-12, hourly rates at UCR ranged between $8-$15. If you don't qualify, you can still apply for great on- and off-campus jobs through our&nbsp;<a href="http://careers.ucr.edu/jobs/Pages/Jobs.aspx" title="Career Services Center">Career Center</a>.</p> 
        <p> <a href="http://finaid.ucr.edu/typesAid/Pages/WorkStudy.aspx">Learn more about Work Study.</a><a href="http://finaid.ucr.edu/typesAid/Pages/WorkStudy.aspx" class="rightArrowCTA"></a></p> 
       </div> 
       <div> 
        <img src="/Images/icons/cogsIcon.png" alt="" /> 
        <h3> <span>Student Loans</span></h3> 
        <h4> Low Interest Rates</h4> 
        <p> When selected carefully, student loans can offer low interest rates and ample time to pay them back. Loans can come from the federal government, UCR, private lenders and more. </p> 
        <ul class="flushleft noUnderline"> 
         <li><a href="http://finaid.ucr.edu/typesAid/Pages/Loans.aspx" class="rightArrowCTA"> Learn more about student loans.</a></li> 
        </ul> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <div class="bottomSectionSchol clearfix"> 
      <img src="/Images/femaleStudent_financial_aid_scholarships.png" class="positionAbs" alt="Female student with backpack" /> 
      <div> 
       <h4>Applying for financial aid is easier than you think!</h4> 
       <p>You don’t have to be overwhelmed. By completing the Free Application for Federal student Aid (FAFSA), you are automatically applying to most sources of available aid, including the&nbsp;<a href="http://www.universityofcalifornia.edu/admissions/paying-for-uc/financial-aid/grants/blue-gold/index.html">Blue and Gold Opportunity Plan</a> and Cal Grants (with a <a href="http://www.csac.ca.gov/doc.asp?id=1177">verified GPA form</a>).&nbsp; </p> 
       <p>Fill out a FAFSA even if you don’t think you will qualify. UCR has aid available for students at all income levels. You have nothing to lose, and everything to gain – including a free (or close to free) education!</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li><a href="http://finaid.ucr.edu/apply/Pages/OverviewforApplying.aspx" class="rightArrowCTA">Learn how to file your FAFSA.</a> </li> 
        <li><a href="http://www.fafsa.ed.gov/deadlines.htm" class="rightArrowCTA">FAFSA Deadlines.</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://finaid.ucr.edu/contact/Pages/default.aspx" class="rightArrowCTA">Contact a UCR Financial Aid Counselor.</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://finaid.ucr.edu/typesAid/Pages/default.aspx" class="rightArrowCTA">Learn More About Types of Financial Aid.</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div>

    <h2>Emergency Warning Siren</h2>

    <div id="main"> 
     <p><img class="imageRight" height="150" alt="" width="141" src="/emergency/images/siren2.gif" />Intended to provide&nbsp;notification to individuals in the outdoor spaces of UCR,&nbsp;the <strong>Emergency Warning Siren </strong>is an audible&nbsp;safety communication link that will be used to warn the University of California Riverside community of emergency conditions on campus. It is&nbsp;a signal that response actions should be taken so that you may secure yourself and seek additional campus provided information relating to the incident.</p> 
     <ol> 
      <li> <h4><span style="color: #0066cc"><strong><a href="#When_I_Hear"><span style="font-size: small"><span><span style="color: #0066cc">What should I&nbsp;do when I&nbsp;hear the siren?</span></span></span></a></strong></span></h4> </li> 
      <li> <h4><span style="color: #0066cc"><span><strong><span style="font-size: larger"><font size="3"><a href="#Should_I_call"><span style="font-size: small"><span style="color: #0066cc">Should I call 911 when I&nbsp;hear the siren?</span></span></a></font></span></strong></span></span></h4> </li> 
      <li> <h4><span style="color: #0066cc"><span><strong><span style="font-size: larger"><a href="#Should_I_Evacuate"><span style="font-size: small"><span style="color: #0066cc">Should I evacuate my building&nbsp;if I hear the siren?</span></span></a></span></strong></span></span></h4> </li> 
      <li> <h4><span style="color: #0066cc"><span><strong><span style="font-size: larger"><span style="color: #0066cc"><span><strong><span style="font-size: larger"><font size="+0"><span style="color: #0066cc"><a href="#Where_get_information"><span style="font-size: small"><span style="color: #0066cc">Where can I get more information during an emergency?</span></span></a></span></font></span></strong></span></span></span></strong></span></span></h4> </li> 
      <li> <h4><span style="color: #0066cc"><span><strong><span style="font-size: larger"><a href="#When_will_the_siren"><span style="font-size: small"><span style="color: #0066cc">When will the siren be used?</span></span></a></span></strong></span></span></h4> </li> 
      <li> <h4><span style="color: #0066cc"><span><strong><span style="font-size: larger"><a href="#Testing"><span style="font-size: small"><span style="color: #0066cc">Will the siren be tested?</span></span></a></span></strong></span></span></h4> </li> 
      <li> <h4><span style="color: #0066cc"><span><span><span><span><span><strong><span style="font-size: larger"><a href="#Located"><span style="font-size: small"><span style="color: #0066cc">Where is the siren located?</span></span></a></span></strong></span></span></span></span></span></span></h4> </li> 
     </ol> 
     <p>&nbsp;</p> 
     <div>
      <span style="color: #0066cc"><span style="font-size: small"><span><b><span style="font-size: medium"><a class=" FCK__AnchorC" name="When_I_Hear"><span style="color: #0066cc">What should I&nbsp;do&nbsp;when&nbsp;I hear the siren?</span></a></span></b></span></span></span>
     </div> 
     <div>
      &nbsp;
     </div> 
     <div style="margin-left: 40px">
      <span style="color: #0066cc"><span><span style="font-size: larger"><strong>TAKE&nbsp;ACTION</strong></span></span></span>
      <strong> </strong>to protect yourself
      <br /> Go inside your office or residence, a nearby building, or your car and shelter inside to avoid exposure. If driving a car, safely pull&nbsp;over to the side of the road, turn off the engine and stay tuned.
     </div> 
     <div style="margin-left: 40px">
      &nbsp;
     </div> 
     <div style="margin-left: 40px">
      <span style="color: #0066cc"><span style="font-size: larger"><strong>TAKE&nbsp;COVER</strong></span><span><strong>&nbsp;</strong></span></span>to prevent exposure to the incident
      <br /> Shut all doors and windows. Building managers should turn off ventilation systems, if feasible.
     </div> 
     <div style="margin-left: 40px">
      &nbsp;
     </div> 
     <div style="margin-left: 40px">
      <span style="color: #0066cc"><span><span style="font-size: larger"><strong>TAKE&nbsp;NOTICE</strong></span></span></span>
      <strong> </strong>and&nbsp;pay attention
      <br /> Obtain campus emergency information
     </div> 
     <h4><span style="color: #0066cc"><span style="font-size: medium"><a name="Should_I_call"><strong>Should I&nbsp;call 911 when I hear the siren?</strong></a></span></span><strong><span style="font-size: medium"><a name="Should_I_call"></a></span></strong></h4> 
     <p style="text-align: center"><span style="color: #ff0000"><span style="font-size: medium"><strong><strong>DO NOT CALL 911<br /> if you hear the warning siren</strong></strong></span></span></p> 
     <p style="margin-left: 40px; text-align: left"><span style="color: #ff0000"><span style="font-size: medium"><font color="#444444" size="2">Dial 911 only if you have a life threatening emergency.</font></span></span></p> 
     <div> 
      <h4><span style="color: #0066cc"><span style="font-size: medium"><a class=" FCK__AnchorC" name="Should_I_Evacuate"><strong>Should I evacuate my building if&nbsp;I hear the siren?</strong></a></span></span><span style="font-size: medium"><a name="Should_I_Evacuate"></a></span></h4> 
      <p style="margin-left: 40px">No, just the opposite.&nbsp; You should stay where you are, remain sheltered and protected,&nbsp;and seek additional information regarding the siren notification from the Campus Status website, emergency information line, or campus radio station.</p> 
      <h4><strong><span style="font-size: medium"><a name="Where_get_information"><span style="color: #0066cc">Where can I get more information during an emergency?</span></a></span></strong><span style="color: #0066cc"><span style="font-size: medium"><a name="Where_get_information"></a></span></span></h4> 
      <div>
       &nbsp;
      </div> 
      <div>
       Access one the following information venues&nbsp;to obtain campus emergency information such as disaster type, evacuation routes, shelter and aid locations, special instructions, etc.:&nbsp;
      </div> 
      <p style="margin-left: 40px"><span style="font-size: larger"><span style="color: #800080"><strong>Campus Status Website</strong><br /> </span></span><span style="color: #0066cc"><a href="http://campusstatus.ucr.edu/">http://campusstatus.ucr.edu</a>&nbsp;<br /> </span>This Web presence is reachable anytime, from anywhere. Like the 800 service, information concerning the emergency will be posted here and will be updated as the situation and conditions evolve.</p> 
      <p style="margin-left: 40px"><span style="font-size: larger"><span style="color: #800080"><strong>Emergency Information Line</strong></span><strong><br /> </strong></span>1-888-UCR-WARN (827-9276)<br /> This number<b> </b>allows access to a recorded message&nbsp;by any standard, cell or pay phone, free of toll charges. Information concerning the emergency is recorded as an outgoing message, and is updated as the situation evolves.&nbsp;</p> 
      <p style="margin-left: 40px"><span style="font-size: larger"><span style="color: #800080"><strong>Campus Radio Station</strong></span></span><strong><br /> </strong><a href="http://www.kucr.org"><span style="color: #0066cc">KUCR 88.3 FM </span></a><br /> The campus radio station will be utilized to disseminate emergency information during critical incidents and disasters.&nbsp;KUCR normally broadcasts 24 hours every day with live programming. Local stations KXFG (92.9 FM), KTDD (1350 AM), and KLYY (97.5 – Spanish Language) also carry Inland Empire area emergency information.</p> 
      <h4><span style="color: #0066cc"><span style="font-size: medium"><a name="When_will_the_siren">&nbsp;<strong>When will the siren be used?</strong></a></span></span><span style="font-size: medium"><a name="When_will_the_siren"></a></span></h4> 
      <p style="margin-left: 40px">While the siren will not be used in during every emergency incident, it will be&nbsp;used to notify campus personnel of emergency conditions&nbsp;resulting from&nbsp;man made, natural, or technical hazards such as:</p> 
      <p style="margin-left: 40px; text-align: left">&nbsp;</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>Chemical spills</li> 
       <li>Flooding</li> 
       <li>Fires</li> 
       <li>Violence on campus</li> 
       <li>Storms</li> 
       <li>Power outages</li> 
       <li>Transportation incidents</li> 
       <li>Other public safety incidents.</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p>&nbsp;</p> 
      <div> 
       <h4><span style="font-size: medium"><span style="color: #0066cc"><a class=" FCK__AnchorC" name="Testing"><span style="color: #0066cc"><b>Will UCR test the Emergency Warning Siren?</b>&nbsp;</span></a></span></span></h4> 
       <p style="margin-left: 40px"><strong>Yes</strong>.&nbsp;The Emergency Warning Siren will be tested on a regularly scheduled basis.&nbsp;</p> 
       <h4><span style="font-size: medium"><span style="color: #0066cc"><a class=" FCK__AnchorC" name="Located"><span style="color: #0066cc"><strong>Where is the siren located?</strong></span></a></span></span><span style="color: #0066cc"><a name="Located"></a></span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div>
       &nbsp;
      </div> 
      <div style="margin-left: 40px">
       UCR has 
       <a href="/emergency/images/sirencoverageareamap.jpg"><span style="color: #0066cc">strategically located </span></a>the Emergency Warning Siren in the central area of campus in order to provide the greatest amount of coverage to the main campus and adjacent campus facilities.&nbsp;Depending on the nature of the incident, the siren will be deployed in conjunction with other information systems such as the Emergency Notification System (text message), campusstatus.ucr.edu, voicemail, KUCR, and others.
      </div> 
      <div style="margin-left: 40px">
       <br /> By their nature, disasters are volatile&nbsp;and erratic&nbsp;so&nbsp;we all have to&nbsp;be prepared for emergencies in a variety of locations;&nbsp;at home, at work, at school, or in our cars. Take the time to take stock of&nbsp;the places where you spend your time and what it will take to be prepared&nbsp;for an emergency in those places or at any given location and time. The ability to aquire emergency information during a disaster is vital to taking appropriate action before, during, and after an emergency so consider keeping a battery operated multiband radio and extra batteries on hand at home, in your car, and in your office.
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <div>
      &nbsp;
     </div> 
     <div>
      For more information, visit the 
      <a href="http://www.ehs.ucr.edu/emergency/index.html"><span style="color: #0066cc">Emergency Management</span></a> program website.&nbsp;
     </div>
