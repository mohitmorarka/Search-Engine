      <p>Clickers (known also as Personal Response Systems or PRS) are an emerging technology that allows real-time interactions between the instructor and their students. The interactions are usually in the nature of queries by the instructor with students responding electronically to those queries. The student responses are digitally collected both as a group and individually and the information (data) reflects the individuals and/or group's consensus to the queries. These data can be immediately displayed as visual feedback, not only to the instructor but also to the queried audience. Information obtained in this way can be manipulated pedagogically and administratively via statistical analysis of the queried data to support programmatic goals of a course, a department or a campus wide initiative.</p> 
      <p>In actual use on this campus this technology has been shown to:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>Increase attendance (sometimes dramatically)</li> 
       <li>Coax participation from normally non-participative students</li> 
       <li>Create a more engaging lecture environment</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p>Computing and Communications has partnered with several academic departments to pilot the use of this technology. After three successful pilot programs, C&amp;C has now equipped all UCR General Assignment classrooms with the hardware and software necessary to utilize this technology.</p>

      <h1>Barbara and Art Culver Center Mission</h1> 
      <br />
      <br /> 
      <p>Exhibitions, installations, dance, music and theatrical performances, spoken word, and a foreign and independent film program: the Barbara and Art Culver Center of the Arts explores the art of our time in a dynamic setting. <br /><br /> The Culver Center's ground floor houses the new permanent home of the Sweeney Art Gallery, a caf&eacute;, a 75-seat film screening room, and an expansive atrium gallery beneath a 40-foot high skylight. Upstairs in Culver's Media Lab, university students, faculty, visiting artists and filmmakers explore state-of-the-art digital technology while dance, music, and theater faculty and students rehearse in studios with sprung wood floors and work out performances in a black box video/sound laboratory. In the lower level the California Museum of Photography's world-treasure Keystone-Mast glass-plate stereo collection is preserved in state-of-the-art seismically isolated cabinets. <br /><br /> Recognizing that artists play a crucial role in society, Culver has an innovative program of lectures, symposia, community forums and dialogues with artists, filmmakers, playwrights, dancers, and musicians. The Culver Center is a presentation site that is simultaneously a major cultural presence and a deeply inclusive institution. Here you will encounter arts and artists from around the world and from Southern California. <br /><br /> The Culver Center was initiated with a lead gift from the Culver family. The transformation of the 1895 Rouse Department Store, one of Riverside's architectural jewels, into the Culver Center has been supported by private donors, UCR, and the City of Riverside. This adaptive reuse enlivens the city's downtown core, bridges the community and the university, and marries history and 21st century digital technology. </p>

One outcome of the UCPath Initiative will be the creation of a systemwide Shared Service Center, called the UCPath Center. The UCPath Center will process selected payroll, benefits, academic personnel, leave management and workforce administration transactions for UC's ten campuses and five medical centers. This implementation is planned to occur over a three-year time frame, in multiple phases. Upon its completion, the UCPath Center will provide services to over 190,000 UC employees and operate with a staff of approximately 500 UC employees. 

The Senior HRIS Support Analyst will work within the UCPath Center, reporting directly to the Technology Manager. The Senior HRIS Support Analyst uses advanced business analysis concepts and campus objectives to resolve highly complex issues where analysis of situations or data requires an in-depth evaluation of variable factors. The Senior HRIS Support Analyst exercises judgment in selecting methods, techniques and evaluation criteria for obtaining results. The Senior HRIS Support Analyst will be responsible for ensuring the functionality of the Human Resources and Payroll system meets the information needs of the University. This position is responsible for maintaining functionality of PeopleSoft HCM/Payroll solution through maintenance of procedural documentation and user training programs. Ultimately, the Senior HRIS Support Analyst serves as the UCPath Center subject matter expert for PeopleSoft functionality. 

Successful completion of a background check is required for this critical position. 

To apply, please be prepared to attach your resume and cover letter as part of the application process. 

Job Duties 

Technology Subject Matter Expert: 60%Analyzes and identifies system discrepancies to determine the problem sources, identifies solutions, and works with Shared Technology Services (STS) to implement in an efficiently and timely manner. Assists business analysts in identifying and developing recommendations for system enhancements. Supports system testing ensuring that processes and information are providing the required results. Analyzes and determines information (and related format) which must be retained on the system. Assists with supporting and maintaining the security administration of PeopleSoft. Assists with developing and maintaining all procedures and documentation related to the operation of the PeopleSoft HR/Payroll system. Uses in-depth knowledge of HR and Payroll business and rules to communicate system needs and requirements. Understands the processes and procedures applicable to each HR/Payroll area and how they relate to PeopleSoft. Ensures HR technology priorities are moving forward and issues are being resolved. Assists in developing and updating training manuals. 

User Support: 40%Participates in establishing the development of system administrative procedures and policies. Develops and maintains relationships with users to identify needs and ensure the system is providing information upon which management and business decisions can be based. Provides support for escalated system issues and collaborates with STS on resolution if required; addresses escalated security/PIN reset issues. Provides on-going support to the PeopleSoft Users including the provision of system demonstrations, advice, training and technical support. Develops and delivers educational and information sessions respecting the scope, functionality, capability and applicability of UCPath to employees in order to enhance their productivity." />
