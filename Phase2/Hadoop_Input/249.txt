     <h3>The 2013 Eaton Science Fiction Conference</h3> 
     <p class="l_kicker">The 2013 Eaton Science Fiction Conference will be held April 11 to April 14, 2013 at the Riverside Marriott Hotel. The 2013 Eaton Science Fiction Conference—co-sponsored with the Science Fiction Research Association (SFRA)—will examine science fiction in multiple media.</p> 
     <div class="l_standout_box" id="l_red_arrow"> 
      <p>The Conference is sponsored by the University of California, Riverside <a title="UCR Libraries" href="http://library.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Libraries</a> and the <a title="CHASS" href="http://chass.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">College of Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences</a>. We are pleased this year&nbsp;to partner with the <a title="SFRA Website" href="http://www.sfra.org/">Science Fiction Research Association (SFRA)</a>.</p>
     </div>

      <p>The UCR Enterprise Directory functions as the on-line directory look up for faculty and staff and for the creation and management of faculty and staff electronic identity. The electronic identity of an employee is their UCR NetID. The UCR NetID enables authenticated access to UCR’s Enterprise Webmail, wireless network connectivity, and other UCR transaction processing systems.</p> 
      <p>Moreover, the Enterprise Directory is one of two core components of UCR’s administrative portal (known as iViews) and provides a single point of authentication for portal users (the second core component is EACS, the campus authorization system).</p> 
      <p>As more and more systems utilize the UCR Net ID for authentication, and as more and more systems become integrated into iViews, it is important that the campus community understand how UCR Net IDs are established and that these identifiers are established in quick, efficient, and secure fashion. With the goal of facilitating access to UCR’s growing world of cyber resources, this web pages will<span> </span>provide background and information on UCR’s Enterprise Directory and UCR Net IDs.</p>

         <div class="column"> 
          <p><font size="3" face="Arial">UCR Department of Art laboratory supervisor and video artist Joe Santarromana will be screening the first 5 episodes from his on-going web series <span style="font-style: oblique;">The Rememberers: Art &amp; Memory, </span>a collection of video portraits that examines creativity through a delicate weaving of biography, storytelling and art practice. This screening of <span style="font-style: oblique;">The Rememberers </span>features Southern California artists Thomas McGovern, who teaches at Cal State San Bernardino and frequently exhibits in Riverside; William Roper, tuba performer and visual artist; Lisa Adams, Los Angeles painter, who reflects on her transition from abstraction to more figurative work; John David O’Brien, Los Angeles artist, critic and curator; and Louis Jacinto, noted for his iconic images of the 1970’s LA Punk Rock music scene.</font></p>
