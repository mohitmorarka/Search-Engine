      <p>Our <a href="/people/faculty/index.html">faculty of forty</a> welcomes you to the rich curricula of <a href="/undergrad/majors.html">nine undergraduate majors</a>. We offer students enrichment through studying the literatures, languages, and cultures of an increasingly global society. We teach eleven world languages, film studies, theory, and linguistics. Comparative and transnational studies are emphasized and encouraged. Our <a href="/graduate/index.html">Graduate Program</a> is characterized by its balance of Asia alongside Europe and the Middle East, and offers tracks in <a href="/graduate/tracks.html">Interliterary</a>, <a href="/graduate/tracks.html#interdisc">Interdisciplinary</a>, and <a href="/graduate/courses.html">Science Fiction Studies</a>. Our passion for close reading and our investment in world literature is informed by the diversity of our texts and of our students themselves. We welcome you to join in the global adventure.</p> 
      <p><strong>Thomas Scanlon</strong><br /><em>Professor and Chair, Department of Comparative Literature and Foreign Languages</em></p> 
      <h4>Professor Ye Yang: Favourite CHASS Professor!</h4> 
      <p>(6/3/2014) Our very own Professor Ye Yang was chosen &quot;Favorite Professor&quot; of our entire CHASS college, according to a poll of students listing &quot;Our Favorite Things&quot; as published in last week's edition of the Highlander campus newspaper. The page of the paper listing the top 5 professors of CHASS is posted outside our conference room if you want to see the listing.</p> 
      <p>Students have said of Dr. Ye: he is &quot;A class act all the way around&quot;...&quot;he offered his students true words of wisdom not only to succeed in his class but also in life as well.&quot;</p> 
      <p>We are very proud of you, Yang! Congratulations!</p>

     <a name="Top of Page"> </a> 
     <h3><img class="img_L" title="Ricky Lara" src="/images/ricky_lara_ucr.jpg" alt="Ricky Lara" width="150" height="182" />Ricky Lara<br /><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;">Graduate Student Researcher<br /></span><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;">(951) 827-4360<br /></span><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;"><a href="mailto:jlara007@ucr.edu"><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;">jlara007@ucr.edu</span></a></span></h3> 
     <p>Ricky joined the Hoddle laboratory in 2008 as a graduate student pursuing a PhD in Entomology. His academic interests revolve around acarology, agroecology and integrated management of economically important pests in agriculture. This committed appreciation of acarology and service to agriculture was fostered by his undergraduate research apprenticeship while working with the beneficial phytoseiid <em>Galendromus occidentalis</em> and the phytophagous spider mites T<em>etranychus pacificus</em> and <em>Eotetranychus willamettei</em> in California vineyards.</p> 
     <h3>Degrees</h3> 
     <p>B.A. Environmental Science (2008), University of California, Berkeley</p> 
     <p>&nbsp;</p> 
     <h3 style="margin-top: 1em; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; margin-left: 0px; font-size: 1.5em; line-height: 1.333em; font-weight: lighter; letter-spacing: 0.02em; color: #2668b2; padding: 0px;">Awards</h3> 
     <ul style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; margin-left: 0px; font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em; padding: 0px;">

     <p><span style="color: #2668b2; font-size: 18px; line-height: 24px;">Current Projects&nbsp;</span></p> 
     <p><span style="color: #444444; font-size: 12px; font-weight: normal;"><img class="img_L" title="Persea Mite" src="/images/perseamite_75.jpg" alt="Persea Mite" width="75" height="50" /></span></p> 
     <p><a href="/hoddle/persea_mite.html" target="_blank"><span style="color: #2668b2; font-size: 18px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: normal;">Persea Mite</span></a><br />Development of Binomial Sampling Plan for Persea Mite On Avocado</p> 
     <p>&nbsp;</p> 
     <h3>Research Interests</h3> 
     <p>At its core, the focus my work is to promote the compatibility of biological and chemical methods of control in agricultural systems. Currently, I am working on updating and reinforcing the integrated pest control program against persea mite, <em>Oligonychus perseae</em> (Acari: Tetranychidae), that infests Southern California avocado orchards. My first objective is to develop a presence/absence sampling plan for growers so they can make quick and statistically reliable estimates of pest densities throughout the growing season to guide spray application decisions. This sampling method will reduce unwarranted pesticide applications because growers won’t overestimate mite numbers and spray unnecessarily, a situation that commonly occurs because of the difficulty of counting mites on avocado leaves.</p> 
     <p>Furthermore, I plan on assessing the risk that novel pesticides being developed for persea mite control pose to beneficial predatory mite populations that attack persea mite on avocado leaves. By reducing pesticide use and conserving the presence of predators, we expect to enhance the avocado orchard ecosystem’s capacity for self-regulation of persea mite by making better use of natural enemies for pest control. I will take the latter a step further by finding natural enemies that have co-evolved with persea mite in its native range and evaluate the biological control potential of other predators currently present in California.</p>

     <h3>Research and Facilities&nbsp;</h3> Our campus is a part of the University of California system, which fosters and promotes innovation, world-class education and research opportunities. Our faculty is committed to continuously producing relevant research and exposing AGSM students to cutting-edge ideas. Students are encouraged to interact with faculty, and together develop fresh perspectives and practical solutions. As an AGSM student, you benefit from studying in a &quot;living laboratory&quot; where you can perform innovative research and see how and why business works.

      <h3>About the Behavioral Research Lab</h3> 
      <p><img src="/images/elab.jpg" alt=" " width="139" height="74" />Realizing the growing importance of experimental research in all areas of business including marketing, management, finance, and accounting, the School of Business Administration established a Behavioral Decision Laboratory in 2009, directed by management professor Amnon Rapoport.</p> 
      <p>The lab supports 32 state-of-the-art terminals connected in a network and monitors for conducting experiments. The participants in these experiments are all UCR students who volunteer to take part in experiments for payoff contingent on performance.</p> 
      <p>Both SoBA faculty and Ph.D. students have been conducting experiments on dynamic pricing, congestion in networks, sequential search, payment mechanisms in marketing, and so on.</p> 
      <p>The lab has also been used as a classroom in which students interact with one another electronically. It has been a major factor in establishing SoBA as a center of research.</p>

     <h3 class="section2">UCR Palm Desert Center</h3> 
     <p>The <a href="http://palmdesert.ucr.edu">UCR Palm Desert Center</a> was established to expand the reach of the University of California, Riverside into one of the fastest-growing regions of California. <br /><br />Established as a teaching and research center, the UCR Palm Desert Center is a catalyst for diversification of the inland desert region by providing relevant regional research, offering innovative graduate programs that attract and retain world class talent to the region, convening and creating partnerships that advance the public good, and impacting the cultural life of the community.<img class="img_R" src="/images/palm_desert.jpg" alt="UCR Palm Desert Graduate Center" width="457" height="191" /></p> 
     <p>The facilities include:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>An auditorium that seats 300.</li> 
      <li>Three lecture halls.</li> 
      <li>11 classrooms.</li> 
      <li>Four boardrooms.</li> 
      <li>A 20-seat computer lab.</li> 
     </ul>
