      <h3><strong><a href="/ens/announce.html">Important Announcement from <br />Associate Vice Chancellor Rowley</a></strong></h3> 
      <p>The University of California Riverside is committed to providing a safe and secure environment to campus students, faculty, staff, and visitors. UCR engages in a broad array of activities supporting this goal, including comprehensive training for police and other safety related personnel, <a href="http://ehs.ucr.edu/emergency">disaster and emergency preparedness</a> planning, as well as a host of other activities aimed at preventing problems before they occur.</p> 
      <p>To enhance UCR's <a href="http://ehs.ucr.edu/emergency">emergency preparedness</a>, the campus has launched a new emergency communications system. This new system will build on existing methods of information distribution and communication, and will greatly expand UCR's ability to quickly and reliably communicate with students, staff, and faculty in an emergency.</p> 
      <p>The initial deployment of the new system will focus on emergency text messaging. In a crisis, this mode of communication will serve as a reliable tool enabling UCR police and safety personnel to contact the entire campus community. Future enhancements will include the ability to deliver messages to instant messaging accounts, campus digital displays, voice mail boxes, and a wide variety of other personal and business communications devices.</p> 
      <p>UCR encourages everyone on campus to sign up for this new emergency notification service, and also to become aware of the various programs, initiatives, and practices already in place aimed at making the campus the as safe and secure as possible.</p>

      <h1>Monster: MFA Thesis Exibition 2014</h1> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">Sweeney Art Gallery</h2> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">Apr 12 - May 3, 2014</h2> 
      <h3 class="spaced-out">Reception - Sat, Apr 12, 2014, 6pm - 9pm<br /></h3> 
      <br /> 
      <br /> 
      <p></p>
      <p class="p1">UCR ARTSblock's Sweeney Art Gallery &amp; Culver Center of the Arts present the annual &quot;MFA Thesis Exhibition 2014&quot; with works by three graduating Master of Fine Arts students in UCR's Department of Art:&nbsp;Chelsea Rector,&nbsp;Michael Shroads,&nbsp;and&nbsp;BANBAN.</p>
      <p class="p1"><br /></p>
      <p class="p1">These students join the ranks of Master of Fine Arts graduates from art departments and art schools across Southern California, a region that has become known for transforming its art students into major players on the international art scene. UCR's thesis exhibition is distinctive because it is the only graduate student exhibition in Southern California presented in an off-campus, public setting. The distinguished full-time faculty in UCR's Art Department includes Charles Long, John Divola, Jill Giegerich, Jim Isermann, Brandon Lattu, Yunhee Min, Erika Suderburg, and Amir Zaki.</p>
      <p class="p1"><br /></p>
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0.0001pt; font-size: 13px; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); line-height: normal;"> </p>
      <p class="p1">The exhibition is organized by UCR Department of Art and UCR ARTSblock. Support is provided by University of California, Riverside’s Department of Art and College of Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences (CHASS).</p>
      <p></p>
