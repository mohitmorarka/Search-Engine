     <h3>Welcome</h3> The Comparative Ancient Civilizations major combines the breadth of an interdisciplinary major with the focus of more traditional majors like History or Classical Civilization. By undertaking a comparison of several major cultures of the past, which have a continued importance in the construction of our present world, the program affords a truly liberal education. Students have a unique opportunity to employ the methods of humanities and social sciences in their major study.

      <p>UCR NetID is a unique ID available for all UCR Faculty and Staff. Once a UCR NetID is assigned to you, you can access UCR’s Electronic Communications such as UCR Webmail, and UCR Wireless Network, and your department SAA (System Access Administrator) can grant you access to UCR Enterprise Systems.</p> 
      <p>Your UCR NetID will remain active until you separate from the University. Upon separation from the University, your UCR NetID will be automatically “disabled”. Access will remain in effect for UCR Webmail access for 30 days for non-academic senate faculty and staff, 60 days for Academic Senate Faculty, and lifetime access is provided for Emeritus Faculty. (Please note that upon involuntary separation, access will be terminated immediately.)</p> 
      <h4><a name="new"> </a>Procedures to Obtain a UCR NetID for New Employees</h4> 
      <ol> 
       <li>If you are a new employee, once you have been hired by your department payroll administrator, you will be instructed to visit your department's Enterprise Directory Administrator the next day (the payroll system updates nightly).</li> 
       <li>Your Department Enterprise Directory Administrator will enter your information into the Enterprise Directory system, they will work with you to create a unique UCR NetID and provide you a temporary password. Click here for a listing of the Enterprise Directory Administrators.</li> 
       <li>Within 2 hours, your UCR NetID will be active. Users must register their UCR NetID and obtain a permanent password by visiting <a href="http://password.ucr.edu">http://password.ucr.edu</a> within 7 days. Your temporary password will expire after 7 days requiring you to re-register your temporary password with your Enterprise Directory Administrator.</li> 
       <li>If you do not set your permanent password within 7 days your temporary password will expire and you will have to have your Enterprise Directory Administrator re-register your temporary password</li> 
      </ol> 
      <h4><a name="reset"> </a>Procedures to re-register a UCR NetID Password</h4> 
      <p>In case you forgot your password or did not register within 7 days):</p> 
      <ol> 
       <li>To have your password re-registered (re-set), please complete the Application for UCR NetID and take it to your Department Enterprise Directory Administrator.</li> 
       <li>Your Department Enterprise Directory Administrator will create a new temporary password.</li> 
       <li>Within 2 hours, your UCR NetID will be active. Users must register their UCR NetID and obtain a permanent password by visiting <a href="http://password.ucr.edu">http://password.ucr.edu</a> within 7 days. Your temporary password will expire after 7 days requiring you to re-register your temporary password with your Enterprise Directory Administrator.</li> 
       <li>If you do not set your permanent password within 7 days your temporary password will expire and you will have to have your Enterprise Directory Administrator re-register your temporary password.</li> 
      </ol> 
      <h4><a name="affiliate"> </a>Procedures to Obtain a UCR NetID for University Affiliates</h4> 
      <p>An affiliate is a person who is engaging in official campus business but does not have an entry in PPS (the campus payroll system). This individual may be a consultant on contract, a visiting researcher, a temporary agency employee or any other identified individual who, for the benefit of the university, should have access to authenticated electronic communications. (See Campus policies http://www.cnc.ucr.edu/index.php?content=policies)</p> 
      <ol> 
       <li>Complete the UCR NetID application. It must be signed by the User (Affiliate) and the Enterprise Directory Administrator. The Enterprise Directory Administrator is responsible for confirming the new Users identity. Please leave the UCR NetID, Password &amp; UCR Enterprise directory “updated by…” blank.</li> 
       <li>Complete the UCR Affiliate Access Request Form. The request will include the Departmental Financial Managers / Entity Head signature authorization. It will also explain who this new User is (category of access), justification for request, and how long they will be affiliated with UCR. You will need to provide the date (e.g. 12/31/07) the affiliation will end. The Affiliates access to email and UCR systems will terminate the day following the affiliate end date. Include the following information on the request form, which is required to complete an entry in the Campus Enterprise Directory Database: 
        <ul> 
         <li>First Name, Middle Initial &amp; Last Name</li> 
         <li>Working Title</li> 
         <li>Phone &amp; Fax #</li> 
         <li>Room # and Bldg where located</li> 
         <li>Six letter department code (e.g. DO1129, same as Enterprise Directory Administrator)</li> 
         <li>Supervisors Name and UCR NetID</li> 
        </ul> </li> 
       <li>Scan both the signed UCR NetID application, and the signed UCR Affiliate Access Request Form; then send both forms as attachments to your Organizations CFAO for approval. The CFAO will submit their approval along with the scanned documents to AffiliateAcctRequests@ucr.edu. Please note Computing &amp; Communications will not accept paper forms for processing.</li> 
       <li>Upon receipt of the request, C&amp;C will process the request by creating an entry in the UCR Enterprise Directory, and will coordinate the delivery of the completed UCR NetID application with your Dept. Enterprise Directory Administrator. This information is confidential. The Department Administrator will issue a copy of the completed UCR NetID form including the temporary password to the new User and will retain the copies, as the official department of record.</li> 
       <li>The sponsoring department is responsible for notifying C&amp;C to make any changes to the Affiliate’s Enterprise Directory record. These changes include extension requests and early affiliation expiration. If an extension is needed, a new Request Form for UCR Affiliate Access must be submitted to C&amp;C. Extensions should always be requested prior to the expiration date to avoid the need for a new UCR NetID application. Once again, please note, C&amp;C will not accept paper forms for processing affiliate access requests or extensions.</li> 
      </ol> 
      <p>Please call Terri McDonald (x26468) with any questions regarding affiliate requests.</p> 
      <p><a href="/edir/guide.html">Enterprise Directory System Access Instructions</a></p> 
      <p>Now that you have a UCR NetID, you can now sign up for access to other UCR systems.</p>

      <h3>Academic Senate Awards</h3> 
      <p>The Faculty Research Lecturer, Distinguished Teaching Award, Distinguished Campus Service Award and the Chancellor’s Award for Excellence in Undergraduate Research are presented annually by the Academic Senate.</p> 
      <h3 class="section">The Edward A. Dickson Emeritus/a Professorship</h3> 
      <p>The Edward A. Dickson Emeritus/a Professorship Award at the Riverside campus will be awarded beginning with the 2008-2009 academic year and annually thereafter, to one or more emeritus professors for teaching, research, or public service activities that are in accordance with the intent of the donor. The awardee(s) shall be known as the Edward A. Dickson Emeritus/a Professor for the duration of the award.</p> 
      <br /> 
      <ul> 
       <li><a href="/awards/dickson/recipients_all.html">Dickson Award Recipients</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/awards/dickson/Dickson Professorship Call  2014 final.pdf">Call for Proposals</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/committee/?do=info&amp;id=12">Go to the Committee on Faculty Welfare</a></li> 
      </ul> 
      <h3 class="section">Faculty Research Lecturer</h3> 
      <p>From its inception well over half a century ago, the Faculty Research Lecturer Award has been the highest honor that the Academic Senate bestows. The recipient of the Faculty Research Lecturer award is an individual who has made a distinguished record in research to deliver a lecturer upon the topic of his selection.</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li><a href="/awards/frl/recipient_2013-14.html">2013–14 Faculty Research Lecturer Recipients</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/awards/frl/recipients_all.html">Previous Faculty Research Lecturers Recipients</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/awards/frl/Faculty Research Lecturer - Call for Nominations 2014.pdf">Call for Nominations</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/committee/?do=info&amp;id=11">Go to the Committee on Faculty Research Lecturer</a></li> 
      </ul> 
      <h3 class="section">Distinguished Teaching</h3> 
      <p>The Distinguished Teaching award recognizes exceptional effort and achievement in teaching by a professional faculty member. A maximum of two awards are selected annually by the Academic Senate Committee for Distinguished Teaching.</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li><a href="/awards/dta/recipients_2012-13.html">2012–13 Distinguished Teaching Awards Recipients</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/awards/dta/recipients_all.html">Previous Distinguished Teaching Award Recipients</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/awards/dta/2013-14/Distinguished%20Teaching%20Award%20-%20Call%20for%20Nominations%202014.pdf">Call for Nominations</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/committee/?do=info&amp;id=9">Go to the Committee on Distinguished Teaching</a></li> 
       <!--<li><a href="/awards/dta/2012-13/2012-13 Distinguished Teaching Nominations Guide.pdf">2012-13 Distinguished Teaching Nominations Guide</a></li>--> 
      </ul> 
      <h3 class="section">Doctoral Dissertation Mentoring Award</h3> 
      <p>Each year the Graduate Council and Graduate Division present the Doctoral Dissertation Advisor/Mentor Award to one or two UCR faculty members who have made outstanding contributions to the training of advanced graduate students.</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li><a href="/awards/ddma/recipients_2012-13.html">2012–13 Doctoral Dissertaion Menotoring Awards Recipients</a></li> 
      </ul> 
      <h3 class="section">Distinguished Campus Service</h3> 
      <p>The Distinguished Campus Service Award recognizes exceptional effort and achievement in campus service by a UCR faculty member. Honorees are recognized for demonstrating sustained dedication to the Academic Senate and/or temporary administrative posts.</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li><a href="/awards/dcs/recipients_2012-13.html">2012–13 Distinguished Campus Service Award Recipients</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/awards/dcs/recipients_all.html">Previous Distinguished Campus Service Recipients</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/awards/dcs/Call for Nominations 2014.pdf">Call for Nominations</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/committee/?do=info&amp;id=40">Go to the Committee on Distinguished Campus Service</a></li> 
      </ul> 
      <h3 class="section">Chancellor's Award for Excellence in Undergraduate Research and Creative Achievement</h3> 
      <p>The Chancellor’s Award for Excellence in Undergraduate Research and Creative Achievement is given annually to two graduating seniors who have conducted outstanding research or creative work and to two faculty members with a distinguished record of fostering undergraduate research or creative activity.</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li><a href="/awards/caeur/recipient_2012-13.html">2012–13 Chancellor's Award for Excellence in Undergraduate Research and Creative Achievement</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/awards/caeur/recipients_all.html">Previous Chancellor's Award for Excellence in Undergraduate Research&nbsp;and Creative Achievement Recipients</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/awards/caeur/Chancellors Awards for Excellence - Call for Nominations.pdf">Call for Nominations</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/committee/?do=info&amp;id=22">Go to the Committee on Scholarship and Honors</a></li> 
       <!--<li><a href="/awards/caeur/2012-13 Guidelines.pdf">Guidelines</a></li>--> 
      </ul>

     <h3><img class="img_L" title="Paul Rugman-Jones" src="/images/paul_rugman_jones_ucr.jpg" alt="Paul Rugman-Jones" />Paul F. Rugman-Jones, PhD<br /><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;">Associate Project Scientist<br /></span><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;">Phone: (951) 827-2627<br /></span><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: x-small;"><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;"><a href="mailto:paulrj@ucr.edu">paulrj@ucr.edu</a></span></span></h3> 
     <p>Dr. Rugman-Jones's research at UCR focuses on the problem posed by invasive insect pests to U.S. agriculture. Specifically, he is involved in the molecular identification and characterization of exotic insect pests and their natural enemies.</p> 
     <p>Based in the Stouthamer lab at UCR, Dr. Rugman-Jones exploits variations in the DNA sequences of individuals to: resolve taxonomic and diagnostic issues; investigate population structure; and, identify the area of origin, and routes of entry, of exotic pests. The accurate identification of pests and natural enemies is fundamental to encouraging fair trade, selecting appropriate mitigation measures, and developing successful biological control programs.</p> 
     <h3 style="margin-top: 1em; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; margin-left: 0px; font-size: 1.5em; line-height: 1.333em; font-weight: lighter; letter-spacing: 0.02em; color: #2668b2; padding: 0px;">Degrees</h3> 
     <p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; margin-left: 0px; font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em; padding: 0px;">PhD Zoology 2003, University of Sunderland, United Kingdom</p> 
     <p style="margin-top: 1.5em; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; margin-left: 0px; font-size: 1em; line-height: 1.5em; padding: 0px;">BSc (Hons) Environmental Biology 1999, University of Sunderland, United Kingdom</p> 
     <h3 style="margin-top: 1em; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; margin-left: 0px; font-size: 1.5em; line-height: 1.333em; font-weight: lighter; letter-spacing: 0.02em; color: #2668b2; padding: 0px;">Research Areas</h3> 
     <ul> 
      <li>DNA Sequencing</li> 
      <li>Molecular species identification</li> 
      <li>Population genetics</li> 
     </ul> 
     <h3>Research Interests</h3> 
     <p>The identification of many insects is difficult, particularly if diagnostic keys are restricted to particular life stages. Dr. Rugman-Jones is interested in developing rapid and practical molecular-based methods to aid identification of all life stages of insects, particularly methods that can be readily employed at ports of entry. There is also growing awareness that many insect pests, which on the surface (i.e. morphologically) appear to be a single species, in fact represent complexes of cryptic species (e.g., Rugman-Jones et al. 2010). This is only revealed if one &quot;scratches the surface&quot; and looks at DNA sequences. Dr. Rugman-Jones does exactly that. Taxa for which molecular identification methods are being developed include: armored scale insects and their parasitoids associated with avocado; parasitoids of soft scales on California citrus; egg parasitoids of LBAM and <em>Cactoblastis cactorum</em>e; and the thrips of California (around 200 native and exotic species).</p> 
     <p>Variation in DNA sequences can also be used to make inferences about the connectivity of populations of a species and to predict likely sources of invading populations. Such information may reveal common entry pathways for invasive pest insects and direct the search for natural enemies that may be considered for biological control. He is particularly interested in using molecular population genetics to reconstruct, and investigate the likelihood of, possible scenarios under which exotic species may have been introduced to California. Dr. Rugman-Jones has used such molecular methods to identify the area of origin of the California populations of avocado thrips, <em>Scirtothrips perseae</em>, and avocado lace bug, <em>Pseudacysta perseae</em>.</p> 
     <h3>Current Projects</h3> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Determining the specific status and areas of origin of three different invasive beetles: walnut twig beetle, <em>Pityophthorus juglandis</em>; polyphagous shot hole borer, <em>Euwallacea</em> sp.; and red palm weevil, <em>Rhynchophorus ferrugineus</em>.</li> 
      <li>Genetic characterization, and confirmation of the establishment, of releases of <em>Tamarixia radiata</em>, a parasitoid wasp which is being reared and released as a biological control agent of Asian citrus psyllid, <em>Diaphorina citri</em>, in California.</li> 
      <li>Genetic characterization of populations of greenhouse thrips, <em>Heliothrips haemorrhoidalis</em>, and citrus thrips, <em>Scirtothrips citri</em>, worldwide.</li> 
      <li>Investigating the effects of mass-rearing on genetic variation and fitness of biological control agents.</li> 
      <li>Characterization of microbial communities associated with the biting midge, <em>Culicoides sonorensis</em>.</li> 
     </ul>

     <h3>Master of Professional Accountancy (MPAc)</h3> 
     <p>The <a href="http://agsm.ucr.edu/mpac/" target="_blank">Master of Professional Accountancy (MPAc) program</a>&nbsp;is d<span>esigned to be completed in three quarters, with the goal of preparing students with the 20/20 vision needed to become the ideal accounting and auditing professionals in the year 2020 and beyond.</span></p> 
     <h3 class="section2">Flex MBA</h3> 
     <p>Earn an MBA according to your schedule. The <a href="http://agsm.ucr.edu/flexmba" target="_blank">Flex MBA program</a> is geared toward those seeking an MBA, but who need to do so according to their schedule. The program of choice for a broad swath of students, including but not limited to, full-time and part-time students, international students, working professionals and individuals who need to accommodate responsibilities at home and with family.</p> 
     <h3 class="section2">Master of Business Administration (MBA)</h3> 
     <p>The <a href="http://agsm.ucr.edu/mba_program/" target="_blank">MBA program</a>&nbsp;is designed for students who wish to earn the MBA during two years of full-time study or three-to-five years of part time study. It is designed for those with little or modest work experience who wish to advance in their careers through preparation in a functional specialty.</p> 
     <h3 class="section2">Master of Finance (MFin)</h3> 
     <p>The <a href="http://agsm.ucr.edu/mfin/" target="_blank">Master of Finance program</a> is designed to be completed in three quarters which offers students a graduate business degree that concentrates specifically on finance. The MFin program helps students learn the skills required to become a successful finance professional as well as provide preparation for all levels of the CFA, GRP, and other professional certifications.&nbsp;</p> 
     <h3 class="section2">Ph.D. in Business Administration (Ph.D.)</h3> 
     <p>The <a href="http://agsm.ucr.edu/phd_program/" target="_blank">Ph.D. in business administration program</a> offered by the A. Gary Anderson Graduate School of Administration combines the University of California’s tradition as a major research university with the Anderson Graduate School’s strengths of great leadership, innovation and entrepreneurship in Inland Southern California, a living laboratory that will define, explore and articulate the meaning of growth in today’s complex marketplace.</p> 
     <h3 class="section2">Bachelor in Business Administration</h3> 
     <p>The UC Riverside <a href="http://undergradbusiness.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">undergraduate program in business administration</a>&nbsp;is all about opportunity. UCR is a world-class research university dedicated to training the next generation of managers, leaders, and entrepreneurs. The Business Administration degree from UCR is an exclusive professional degree, and students have the ability to get a job in the area they studied the moment they graduate from the university.</p> 
     <p>The <a href="http://undergradbusiness.ucr.edu/minor.html" target="_blank">minor in business administration</a> exposes students to the core areas of business such as marketing, management, accounting, finance, and more. By providing a solid base in basic business practices, the business administration minor allows students of other majors to receive the necessary education to pursue their career with financial responsibility.</p>
