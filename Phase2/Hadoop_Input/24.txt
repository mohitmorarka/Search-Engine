       <p>Anirban Banerjee, co-founder of StopTheHacker</p>

      <p>RIVERSIDE, Calif. (www.ucr.edu) — <a href="https://www.stopthehacker.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.stopthehacker.com/']);">StopTheHacker</a>, an anti-malware company created by a graduate of the University of California, Riverside <a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.engr.ucr.edu/']);">Bourns College of Engineering</a>, <a href="http://blog.cloudflare.com/cloudflare-acquires-stopthehacker" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://blog.cloudflare.com/cloudflare-acquires-stopthehacker']);">has been bought</a> by <a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.cloudflare.com/']);">CloudFlare</a>, the web performance and security company who protects more than 1.5 million web sites and sees five percent of Internet traffic worldwide.</p> 
      <p>StopTheHacker was co-founded by <a href="http://www.stopthehacker.com/about-us/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.stopthehacker.com/about-us/']);">Anirban Banerjee</a> shortly after he received his Ph.D. from UC Riverside in 2008. CloudFlare, which is based in San Francisco, is valued at $1 billion by venture capital firms, <a href="http://graphics.wsj.com/billion-dollar-club/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://graphics.wsj.com/billion-dollar-club/']);">according to an analysis by the Wall Street Journal</a>.</p> 
      <p>Banerjee, now a systems engineer at CloudFlare in charge of integrating the technology developed by StopTheHacker into CloudFlare’s system, said the acquisition is the exact path he wanted StopTheHacker to take.</p> 
      <p>“This deal validates that we went into the right market,” he said. “It shows that, yes, people do value what we are doing.”</p> 
      <p>Financial details of the acquisition are not being disclosed.</p> 
      <p>The acquisition addresses a problem CloudFlare faced. While the company is good at stopping new malware infections, it needed a way to ensure that sites that just signed up for its service weren’t already infected.</p> 
      <p>Over a period of several months CloudFlare talked to about six malware scanning companies, including StopTheHacker, whose malware scanning service has been available for two years in the CloudFlare Apps Marketplace, said <a href="http://www.cloudflare.com/people" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.cloudflare.com/people']);">Matthew Prince</a>, CEO and co-founder of CloudFlare.</p> 
      <p>CloudFlare opted for StopTheHacker because of positive reviews from its customers that used the StopTheHacker app and because of the company’s focus on engineering and technology, Prince said.</p> 
      <p>“We respected the work that StopTheHacker had done and saw them as a leader in the field of anti-malware remediation and identification,” Prince said.</p> 
      <p>Other large technology companies have made similar investments recently to ensure they are not being used to spread malware. For example, Google recently acquired VirusTotal and Twitter acquired Dasient.</p> 
      <p>StopTheHacker was based in Riverside until moving to San Francisco in 2011. Banerjee, who came to the United States from India in 2004 to pursue his Ph.D. at UC Riverside, co-founded the company with Michalis Faloutsos, a former computer science and engineering professor at UC Riverside who advised Banerjee. Faloutsos is now a professor and chair of the computer science department at The University of New Mexico.</p> 
      <p>StopTheHacker got started with <a href="http://newsroom.ucr.edu/2141">$600,000 in funding in 2009 and 2010</a> from the National Science Foundation’s Small Business Innovative Research grant program. In 2011, the company <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/3167">received $1.1 million from several private investors</a>, led by Runa Capital.</p>

        <h3><img src="/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/ttl_expertsondemand.png" alt="" height="17" width="242" /><span class="accessibility">Experts on Demand</span></h3> 
        <p class="kicker">UC Riverside has dozens of experts in virtually every field you can imagine.</p> 
        <p> Whether it's the latest fad in Hollywood, the latest must-have gadget or the latest row in Washington, UCR has a vast pool of staff and faculty who can present a unique view right now. </p> 
        <p> These interactions can take place in person, electronically, through a campus ISDN line, or even via video through our Digital Production Studio, which can put a UCR expert on the air anywhere in the world through AT&amp;T's regional fiber network hub. </p>

      <div class="figure_main vert"> 
       <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/20879/anirbanbanerjee-31"><img width="374" height="448" src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/AnirbanBanerjee-31-374x448.jpg" class="attachment-feature-vert" alt="head shot of Anirban Banerjee" /></a>
       <p>Anirban Banerjee, co-founder of StopTheHacker</p> 
      </div> 
      <p>RIVERSIDE, Calif. (www.ucr.edu) — <a href="https://www.stopthehacker.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.stopthehacker.com/']);">StopTheHacker</a>, an anti-malware company created by a graduate of the University of California, Riverside <a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.engr.ucr.edu/']);">Bourns College of Engineering</a>, <a href="http://blog.cloudflare.com/cloudflare-acquires-stopthehacker" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://blog.cloudflare.com/cloudflare-acquires-stopthehacker']);">has been bought</a> by <a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.cloudflare.com/']);">CloudFlare</a>, the web performance and security company who protects more than 1.5 million web sites and sees five percent of Internet traffic worldwide.</p> 
      <p>StopTheHacker was co-founded by <a href="http://www.stopthehacker.com/about-us/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.stopthehacker.com/about-us/']);">Anirban Banerjee</a> shortly after he received his Ph.D. from UC Riverside in 2008. CloudFlare, which is based in San Francisco, is valued at $1 billion by venture capital firms, <a href="http://graphics.wsj.com/billion-dollar-club/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://graphics.wsj.com/billion-dollar-club/']);">according to an analysis by the Wall Street Journal</a>.</p> 
      <p>Banerjee, now a systems engineer at CloudFlare in charge of integrating the technology developed by StopTheHacker into CloudFlare’s system, said the acquisition is the exact path he wanted StopTheHacker to take.</p> 
      <p>“This deal validates that we went into the right market,” he said. “It shows that, yes, people do value what we are doing.”</p> 
      <p>Financial details of the acquisition are not being disclosed.</p> 
      <p>The acquisition addresses a problem CloudFlare faced. While the company is good at stopping new malware infections, it needed a way to ensure that sites that just signed up for its service weren’t already infected.</p> 
      <p>Over a period of several months CloudFlare talked to about six malware scanning companies, including StopTheHacker, whose malware scanning service has been available for two years in the CloudFlare Apps Marketplace, said <a href="http://www.cloudflare.com/people" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.cloudflare.com/people']);">Matthew Prince</a>, CEO and co-founder of CloudFlare.</p> 
      <p>CloudFlare opted for StopTheHacker because of positive reviews from its customers that used the StopTheHacker app and because of the company’s focus on engineering and technology, Prince said.</p> 
      <p>“We respected the work that StopTheHacker had done and saw them as a leader in the field of anti-malware remediation and identification,” Prince said.</p> 
      <p>Other large technology companies have made similar investments recently to ensure they are not being used to spread malware. For example, Google recently acquired VirusTotal and Twitter acquired Dasient.</p> 
      <p>StopTheHacker was based in Riverside until moving to San Francisco in 2011. Banerjee, who came to the United States from India in 2004 to pursue his Ph.D. at UC Riverside, co-founded the company with Michalis Faloutsos, a former computer science and engineering professor at UC Riverside who advised Banerjee. Faloutsos is now a professor and chair of the computer science department at The University of New Mexico.</p> 
      <p>StopTheHacker got started with <a href="http://newsroom.ucr.edu/2141">$600,000 in funding in 2009 and 2010</a> from the National Science Foundation’s Small Business Innovative Research grant program. In 2011, the company <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/3167">received $1.1 million from several private investors</a>, led by Runa Capital.</p>

      <p>RIVERSIDE, Calif. — The human brain is a complex organ containing billions of neurons and capable of storing a lifetime of memories.&nbsp; So used to our brains are we that one is hardly pressed to pay attention to its intricate workings on a minute-by-minute basis.</p> 
      <p>That may change, at least on Wednesday, March 12, from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m., when the University of California, Riverside will mark <a href="http://ngsa.ucr.edu/">Brain Awareness Day</a>.&nbsp; The free event, which will take place in the <a href="http://campusmap.ucr.edu/imap/index.html?loc=HUB">Highlander Union Building</a>, Room 302, is intended to help the inland Southern California community of all ages understand how the brain works.</p> 
      <div id="attachment_20967" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 344px">
       <img class="size-full wp-image-20967" alt="Image shows human brain." src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Human_brain_NIH.jpg" width="344" height="250" />
       <p class="wp-caption-text"><b>NIH image of human brain.</b></p>
      </div> 
      <p>A part of <a href="http://www.dana.org/BAW/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.dana.org/BAW/']);">Brain Awareness Week</a>, the event will feature a ‘brain bazaar’ from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m., involving research by graduate students, demos that explain the brain’s intricate workings, and information on the brain that the public can take home.&nbsp; As a bonus, visitors will be offered free burritos for lunch.</p> 
      <p>“Brain Awareness Week&nbsp;is a global campaign to increase public awareness of the progress and benefits of brain research,” said <a href="http://cbns.ucr.edu/faculty/curras-collazo.html">Margarita Curras-Collazo</a>, an associate professor of <a href="http://cbns.ucr.edu/">neuroscience</a> and one of the faculty organizers of Brain Awareness Day. &nbsp;“We, the&nbsp;neuroscience community at UC Riverside,&nbsp;which includes faculty, graduate and undergraduate students, postdoctoral scientists and staff, would like to do our part to promote this awareness to our neighbors.”</p> 
      <p>Last year’s event attracted nearly 600 people from all walks of life.</p> 
      <p>“Brain Awareness Day is part community outreach — it is open to the public and we have invited local sixth graders and ninth graders,” said <a href="http://neuro.ucr.edu/gradstudents.html">Zoe Thompson</a>, the outreach coordinator of the <a href="http://ngsa.ucr.edu/">Neuroscience Graduate Student Association</a>, which has planned the event. “It is also part on-campus outreach — reaching out to undergraduates, graduate students and faculty in other departments who might want to collaborate or would like to know what we are doing.”</p> 
      <p>Activities planned this year include: the ability to get an electroencephalogram from a <a href="http://emotiv.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://emotiv.com/']);">wireless EEG machine</a> (an EEG records the electrical activity in the brain resulting from the firing of neurons); and a <a href="https://backyardbrains.com/products/spikerbox" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://backyardbrains.com/products/spikerbox']);">SpikerBox</a>, which can record neurons firing in invertebrates using a provided electrode and a smartphone.</p> 
      <p>“We will have sheep brains, a cow heart, and some creatures — planaria, anemones, a scorpion and crayfish — that&nbsp;show the progressive complexity of the nervous system,” Thompson said.</p> 
      <p>A number of booths will be set up to showcase different aspects of the brain.&nbsp; For example, in one booth visitors will understand how they can optimize napping to improve memory.&nbsp; In yet another booth, researchers will explain how a species of parasitoid wasps is able to take over the central nervous system of cockroaches.&nbsp; Yet another booth will demonstrate how the brain localizes sounds.&nbsp; Graduate students from the Neuroscience Graduate Program and Psychology Department will present posters of their research.</p> 
      <p>Three faculty members will give 20-minute talks starting at 1 p.m.&nbsp; They are: <a href="http://www.psych.ucr.edu/faculty/hickmott/index.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.psych.ucr.edu/faculty/hickmott/index.html']);">Peter Hickmott</a>, who works on cortical plasticity; <a href="http://neuro.ucr.edu/faculty/dahanukar.html">Anupama Dahanukar</a>, who works on molecular and genetic analysis of the fruit fly gustatory (taste) system; and <a href="http://www.psych.ucr.edu/faculty/seitz/index.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.psych.ucr.edu/faculty/seitz/index.html']);">Aaron Seitz</a>, who works on visual processing and learning.</p> 
      <p>The event is funded by the Department of Cell Biology &amp; Neuroscience, the Neuroscience Graduate Program, the Psychology Department, the Office of the Chancellor, the Graduate Division, the Center for Glial-Neuronal Interactions/School of Medicine, and the Graduate Student Association.</p> 
      <p>Parking on campus costs $6.</p>

            <td> <h4 class="BulletIntro">Launch of UCnet</h4> <p class="BulletIntro">On February 6, 2014, <a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a>, a new systemwide website for faculty, staff, and retirees was launched as part of an initiative to improve the online tools and resources offered to UC audiences. <a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a> is a place where UC faculty, staff and retirees can:</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li>Read timely news and interesting stories about the university and the people who work here</li> 
              <li>Find information about benefits and policies as well as a wealth of university tools and resources</li> 
              <li>Share views with colleagues from around the system</li> 
              <li>Learn about opportunities to get involved in UC community activities</li> 
             </ul> <p class="Body"><a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a> has replaced the At Your Service website. Due to this transition, certain links from the UCR Human Resources website that formerly pointed to the At Your Service website are now being redirected to the <a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a> home page, and not necessarily to the content expected. The Human Resources office is working to update these links to point directly to the specific content on <a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a> as they did previously to the At Your Service website. <a class="external" href="http://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/" target="_blank">UCnet</a> does feature a robust search tool that should prove helpful during this transitional phase and in the future to assist in navigating to needed information.</p> <p>Please note that <a class="external" href="https://atyourserviceonline.ucop.edu/ayso/" target="_blank">At Your Service Online</a>, the password-protected portal used to access personal pay and benefits information, still exists.</p> </td> 
           </tr>

In addition, we will continue to provide a computer kiosk for job applicants and a phone for visitors to use to contact HR/LR employees directly.</p> <p><strong>Our full service office hours are now</strong>:<br />Monday – Friday: 8:00 a.m. – 5:00 p.m.</p> </td>

While the campus made every effort to provide information regarding the new options, it is possible that there may be faculty and staff who wish to change their current election. If you conclude you are in the wrong plan and decide you want to make a change, please contact the Benefits Office at <a href="mailto:benefits@ucr.edu?subject=2014%20Medical%20Plan">benefits@ucr.edu</a> or by phone at (951) 827 -4766 as soon as possible, but no later than <em><strong>March 31, 2014</strong></em>.</p> </td>
