      <h3>About the Office of Alumni &amp; Constituent Relations</h3> 
      <p>The Office of Alumni &amp; Constituent Relations serves to preserve and promote the relationships between UCR, its alumni, students and many external constituencies. Through its various services and programs, the office engages UCR's alumni, students and external constituencies in support of the university and its tradition of excellence.</p> 
      <p>The office's general objectives are to:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>Promote the interests of the University of California, Riverside and strengthen its base of support.</li> 
       <li>Encourage an ongoing commitment to UCR among its alumni, students, parents, emeriti faculty and staff retirees.</li> 
       <li>Ensure timely and effective communication between UCR and its alumni and external constituencies.</li> 
       <li>Encourage greater involvement in <a href="http://alumni.ucr.edu/">UCR's Alumni Association</a>, <a href="http://www.ucr.edu/parents/parents.html">Parents Association</a>, <a href="http://alumni.ucr.edu/Page.aspx?pid=320">Student Alumni Association</a>, Emeriti Association and Retirees Association.</li> 
       <li>Provide services and programs to benefit alumni, students, parents and other constituencies.</li> 
      </ul>

      <h3>Graduation Application Deadlines</h3> 
      <p>In order to graduate, undergraduate students must submit a graduation application. Every college uses the same application deadline, but the format and how you submit it will vary. Contact your college directly to find out where and how to submit your graduation application. Graduate students should consult the <a href="http://graduate.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Graduate Division</a>.</p>

     The University of California, Riverside (
    <a href="http://www.ucr.edu/">www.ucr.edu</a>) is a doctoral research university, a living laboratory for groundbreaking exploration of issues critical to Inland Southern California, the state and communities around the world.
