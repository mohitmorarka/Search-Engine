     <h3>UCR has a session for everyone!</h3> 
     <p>The&nbsp;Summer Timeline contains important dates and deadlines which are subject to change&nbsp;without notice.&nbsp;Please click on the session your course is in and <strong>review these dates frequently to ensure that&nbsp;your classes are added/dropped within&nbsp;the allowed&nbsp;time frame. </strong></p> 
     <p>UCR will be celebrating <strong>Independence Day on July 4<sup>th</sup>, and Labor Day on September 1<sup>st</sup>. </strong>There will be no instruction on these two days.</p> 
     <p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <a title="Session 1" href="#Session 1">SESSION 1 (6/23-7/26)</a>&nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <a title="Session 2" href="#Session 2">SESSION 2 (7/28-8/30)</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <a title="Session 3" href="#Session 3">SESSION 3 (6/23-7/12) </a>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <a title="Session 4" href="#Session 4">SESSION 4 (7/14-8/2)</a></p> 
     <p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <a title="Session 5" href="#Session 5">SESSION 5 (8/4-8/23)</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <a title="Session 6" href="#Session 6">SESSION 6 (6/23-8/9)</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp; <a title="Session 7" href="#Session 7">SESSION 7 (7/28-9/13)</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <a title="Session 8" href="#Session 8">SESSION 8 (6/23-8/30)</a></p> 
     <p class="attention">Please keep in mind when registering for UCR summer courses:<br /> ?You are responsible for the grades and fees associated with each course in which you have enrolled.<br /> ?It is your responsibility to drop any unwanted course(s) or section(s), including waitlist enrollments, prior to the published drop deadline(s).<br /> ?To ensure enrollment in a course, you must pay for your fees in full by the end of the business day<em><sup>1 </sup></em>on the same day on which you enroll. Any balance due on your 'Summer Fees' screen that is not paid in full by the end of the business day<em><sup>1</sup></em> will result in some or all of your classes being dropped. <span style="color: #365f91;"><a title="Payment Information" href="/payments.html">Click here for more information about the pay-as-you-go system.</a><br /><br /></span><em><sup>1</sup> Payments are accepted at the Cashier’s office during open hours; online payments can be made up to 9:30 PM (a non-refundable convenience fee is assessed for using online payment methods).</em></p>

      <h3>Air Quality</h3> 
      <p>07/12/09 (12:29 pm) The <a href="ftp://ftp.aqmd.gov/pub/globalist/FcstMsg.pdf">South Coast Air Quality Management District</a> indicated that tomorrow the Metro Riverside Area will experience an air quality level that is &quot;Unhealthy for sensitive groups&quot;. Sensitive or susceptible persons, such as those with heart or lung disease, should minimize outdoor activity.</p> 
      <p>12/09/08 (10:59 am): The South Coast Air Quality Management District indicated that tomorrow the Metro Riverside Area will experience an air quality level that is &quot;Unhealthy for sensitive groups&quot;. In areas directly impacted by windblown dust, everyone should avoid any vigorous outdoor or indoor exertion; people with respiratory or heart disease, the elderly, and children should remain indoors.</p> 
      <p>09/07/08 (12:25 pm): The South Coast Air Quality Management District indicated that tomorrow the Metro Riverside Area will experience an air quality level that is &quot;Unhealthy for sensitive groups&quot; (AQI=135).</p> 
      <p>07/14/08 (9:00 am): The South Coast Air Quality Management District indicated that the Metro Riverside Area will experience an air quality level that is &quot;Moderate&quot; (AQI=97).</p> 
      <p>07/11/08 (1:00 pm): The South Coast Air Quality Management District indicated that the Metro Riverside Area will experience an air quality level that is &quot;Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups&quot; (AQI=135), with a forecast of the same for tomorrow 07/12/08 (AQI=135).</p> 
      <p>07/09/08 (1:30 pm): The South Coast Air Quality Management District indicated that the Metro Riverside Area will experience an air quality level that is &quot;Unhealthy&quot; (AQI=172), with a forecast of the same for tomorrow 07/10/08 (AQI=156).</p> 
      <p>07/08/08 (1:30 pm): The South Coast Air Quality Management District indicated that the Metro Riverside Area will experience an air quality level that is &quot;Unhealthy&quot; (AQI=174).</p> 
      <p>05/19/08 (5:41 am): National Weather Service in San Diego released a &quot;Hazardous Weather Outlook&quot; for the Riverside County mountain area. The hot weather will be near record breaking high temperatures tonight. Beginning tomorrow through Sunday, strong onshore flow will bring winds near advisory strength.</p> 
      <p>05/18/08 (2:30 pm): The South Coast Air Quality Management District indicated that the Metro Riverside Area will experience an air quality level that is &quot;Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups&quot; (AQI=114).</p> 
      <p>04/30/08 (1:30 pm)</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>A dust advisory is in effect for Coachella Valley (Area 30). Strong winds and blowing dust are expected to last throughout the day. Air quality is expected to reach the <strong>Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups </strong>level or higher in areas of the Coachella Valley impacted by windblown dust (see table below for specific meaning of this health concern).</li> 
       <li>A smoke advisory is in effect for Coachella Valley (Area 30) and the Anza Area (Area 27) in Riverside County. Smoke and ash from the Apache fire currently burning in the San Jacinto Mountains west of Idyllwild is expected. Air quality is expected to reach the <strong>Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups </strong>level or higher in areas directly impacted by the wildfire smoke and ash.</li> 
       <li>Everyone should avoid any vigorous outdoor or indoor exertion in smoke impacted areas; people with respiratory or heart disase, the elderly, and children should remain indoors. Keep your windows and doors closed unless it is extermely hot inside. In these cases, seek alternate shelter. Run your air conditioner if you have one. Keep the fresh air intake closed and the filter clean to prevent bringing additional smoke inside.</li> 
      </ul>
