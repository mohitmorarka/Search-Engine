     <h3>What is the SCS Kiosk?</h3> 
     <p>The SCS Kiosk is a computer kiosk located in many of the SCS computer labs, as well as Bear Help, that allows students to manage their UCR NetID's or request general assistance.</p> 
     <p>If you need to use an SCS kiosk, please visit Bear Help in the HUB or one of the computer labs in Watkins 2111, Sproul or Statistics.</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li><strong>Look Up Account or Set Password<br /></strong> 
       <div class="xpander"> 
        <p>This tool will allow you to look up your UCR Net ID and email addresses, as well as reset your password.&nbsp; You will need your Student ID Number (SID) and PermPIN. If you do not know your PermPIN you should go to the Registrar's Office with a picture ID and they will give it to you. Your UCR Net ID will allow you access to the SCS computer labs, email, VPN, the wireless network, and your iLearn account.</p> 
        <p>This function is also available online: <a href="http://studentusername.ucr.edu">Online Net ID and email address lookup</a></p> 
       </div> </li> 
      <li><strong>Signing up for an Extension computer lab account</strong> 
       <div class="xpander"> 
        <p>Use this option if you are an Extension student who is concurrently enrolled in a class at UCR that requires the use of a computer. In order to sign up for an Extension computer lab account you will need your full name, your Student ID Number, email address where you can be contacted, and the class or classes that you are taking.</p> 
       </div> </li> 
      <li><strong>Signing up for an Alumni account</strong> 
       <div class="xpander"> 
        <p>Use this option if you are a former UCR student and a current member of the Alumni Association who would like to be able to use the SCS computer labs. In order to sign up for an Alumni account you will need your full name, email address where you can be contacted, and the date when your Alumni Association membership expires.</p> 
       </div> </li> 
      <li><strong>Register your clicker</strong> 
       <div class="xpander"> 
        <p>If you are a concurrently enrolled Extension student you can use this tool to manually register your clicker.</p> 
        <p>Alternately, you can go to the <a href="http://clickers.ucr.edu" target="_blank">Clickers website</a> and use the form on the main page.</p> 
       </div> </li> 
      <li><strong>Requesting assistance with miscellaneous account problems</strong> 
       <div class="xpander"> 
        <p>Use this option if you have an account or password problem that doesn't fit under one of the other options or you have already tried one of the other options and it did not work. You will need your full name, email address where you can be contacted, login name, Student ID Number (SID), and a description of the problem (please include what steps you have taken to solve it).</p> 
        <p>Alternately, you can <a href="mailto:helpesk@student.ucr.edu">email the Student Help Desk</a></p> 
       </div> </li> 
     </ul>

     <h3><img class="img_L" title="Vanessa Lopez" src="/images/vanessa_lopez_ucr.jpg" alt="Vanessa Lopez" width="150" height="182" />Vanessa Lopez<br /><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;">Graduate Student Researcher<br />Phone: (951) 827-4360<br /></span><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: x-small;"><span style="color: #25558c; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18px; font-weight: bold;"><a href="mailto:vlope006@ucr.edu">vlope006@ucr.edu</a></span></span></h3> 
     <p>Vanessa Lopez is a Ph.D. candidate in the Entomology Department who joined the Hoddle lab in September 2009. She is working on developing a classical biological control program for the invasive goldspotted oak borer, <em>Agrilus auroguttatus</em>, (GSOB) which includes identifying the native range of GSOB and surveying for co-evolved natural enemies of this pest. Vanessa is also conducting research on the biology and life history of GSOB to understand important traits such as fecundity, longevity, and dispersal potential.</p> 
     <p>While an undergraduate at the University of California, Riverside (UCR), Vanessa studied overseas via the University of California’s Education Abroad Program. Through this program, she had the opportunity to study tropical conservation and biology in Monteverde, Costa Rica. The Tropical Conservation and Biology program focused strongly on research, and throughout the quarter she participated in several group and individual research projects. These projects encompassed many facets of biology such as behavior, diversity, and ecology. Examples of these research projects include examining species composition at different levels within an intertidal zone, and studying insect diversity in pasture, primary, and secondary forests.</p> 
     <p>Vanessa's individual study project in Costa Rica examined hitchhiking behavior and load selection in the leaf-cutter ant, <em>Atta cephalotes</em>. In leaf-cutter ants, small workers often ride or “hitchhike” on leaf fragments carried back to the nest by larger foragers. This study focused primarily on the behavior of the small workers, or minima, in <em>Atta cephalotes</em> to determine if there is a correlation between minima activity and the presence of parasitoids (Phoridae).</p> 
     <p><span style="color: #2668b2; font-size: 18px; line-height: 24px;">Degrees</span></p> 
     <p>B.S. Biological Sciences: Conservation Biology (2008), University of California, Riverside</p>

     <h3>Join the School of Business Administration Family</h3> 
     <p>The people who make up the staff and faculty of the School of Business Administration (SoBA) at the University of California, Riverside are a diverse group who share a passion for research and education in the vast fields of business administration.</p> 
     <p>SoBA is the only business school in Inland Southern California that is affiliated with a major research university. We have the largest undergraduate business program in the 10-campus UC system and are continuing to grow our offerings in graduate studies, adding Master of Professional Accountancy (MPAc) and Ph.D. programs to our already well-established MBA program.</p> 
     <p>We invite you to learn more about the faculty and staff opportunities at the School of Business Administration. Check back often to see new postings.</p>
