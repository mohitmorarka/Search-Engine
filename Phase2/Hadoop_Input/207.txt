      <h4><img src="/images/candidates.jpg" alt="candidates" width="484" height="190" /><br /><br />Celebrate UCR's 60<sup>th</sup>Commencement</h4> 
      <p>Welcome to the official Web site for UC Riverside's 60th Commencement to be held June 13 - 16, 2014.</p> 
      <p>If you are a student about to graduate, congratulations on your achievement – you have worked hard to get to this point of your life!</p> 
      <p>Making this day a memorable occasion for you and your proud family members and friends is very important to UC Riverside, and we've developed this Web site to provide you with information on the ceremonies, what to expect, where to go, and what to do. Additional details will be posted to this site as they become available, so please check back periodically.</p> 
      <div class="nota-bene"> 
       <p>Students who expect to graduate Fall 2013, Winter 2014 or Spring 2014 are eligible to participate in Commencement 2014. Graduation application deadlines and additional information can be found on the following web sites:</p> 
       <h4>Bachelor's Degree Candidates</h4> 
       <p><a href="http://student.engr.ucr.edu/">Bourns College of Engineering </a>(BCOE)</p> 
       <p><a href="http://chassstudentaffairs.ucr.edu/graduation_ceremony_info/index.html">College of Humanities, Arts and Social Sciences </a>(CHASS)</p> 
       <p><a href="http://cnasstudent.ucr.edu/graduation.html"> College of Natural and Agricultural Sciences </a>(CNAS)</p> 
       <p><a href="http://undergradbusiness.ucr.edu/undergraduate_advising/">School of Business Administration </a>(SOBA)</p> 
       <h4>Advanced Degree Candidates</h4> 
       <p>For specific eligibility information and application deadlines contact <a href="mailto:kara.oswood@ucr.edu">Kara Oswood</a> in the Graduate Division Student Affairs Office.</p> 
       <h4>Teaching Credential Candidates</h4> 
       <p>For specific eligibility information and application deadlines contact your <a href="mailto:leticia.singh@ucr.edu">Leticia Singh</a>.</p>

      <p style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="color: black;"><span style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: medium;">Dear UCR Faculty, Staff and Students,</span></span></p> 
      <p style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="color: black;"><span style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: medium;">&nbsp;</span></span></p> 
      <p style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: Calibri;"><span style="font-size: medium;"><span style="color: black;">Computing &amp; Communications will be performing an application version update and a database upgrade to our learning management system, iLearn.      <h3>The Collections and Archives that Make Up the Eaton Collection</h3> 
     <p>The Eaton Collection is the largest publicly-accessible collection of science fiction and fantasy literature in the world, consisting of over 300,000 items. The collection begins with the 1517 edition of Thomas More's <em>Utopia</em> and includes first editions of many seminal works including Mary Shelley's <em>Frankenstein</em> (1818), Bram Stoker's <em>Dracula (1897)</em> and H.G. Wells' <em>The Time Machine</em> (1895).&nbsp;</p> 
     <div class="nota-bene l_black_box"> 
      <h4>Manuscripts, Studies and Ephemera</h4> 
      <p>The collection also includes more than 50,000 science fiction-themed comic books as well as the most expansive collection of scholarly studies on science fiction and fantasy in the United States</p> 
      <div class="column2R l_image_block"> 
       <img src="/images/eaton/inside_pages/about_thumbnail1.jpg" alt="Book cover of Pigs &amp; Eagles" /> 
       <img src="/images/eaton/inside_pages/about_thumbnail2.jpg" alt="Sci-Fi book covers" /> 
      </div> 
      <ul> 
       <li> <h5><a href="/selected.html">Archives of Authors' Papers</a></h5> Eaton holds the archives of dozens of prominent science fiction writers and artists. <a href="/selected.html">A complete list of the authors and a basic description of the holdings are available.</a></li> 
       <li> <h5><a href="/fanzines.html">Fanzine and Comic Book Collections</a></h5> Eaton holds the largest collection of fanzines at a public institution. There are over 75,000 fanzines,&nbsp; with fans continuing to donate to its holdings weekly. <a href="http://library.ucr.edu/?l=fanzine">Search the fanzine collection now.</a></li> 
       <li> <h5><a href="/othercollection.html">Other Significant Collections</a></h5> The Eaton Collection features additional collections of SF scripts, multimedia collections, fan conventions, newsletters, ephemera and collectibles.</li> 
      </ul> 
     </div> 
     <div class="l_overflow_block"> 
      <div class="column3 l_find_book"> 
       <h4><img src="/images/eaton/find-a-book_title.png" alt="Find a book or publication" /></h4> 
       <p>The collection may be searched online by using the <a href="http://scotty.ucr.edu/">library's online catalog, SCOTTY</a>.</p> 
       <img src="/images/eaton/find-a-book_bottom.png" title="footer image" /> 
      </div> 
      <h4>How to Access the Eaton Collection</h4> 
      <p>The Eaton Collection is located in the UCR Libraries' Special Collections &amp; University Archives&nbsp; on the fourth floor of Tom&aacute;s Rivera Library on the campus of UC Riverside. It is open to the public Monday to Friday from 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.</p> 
      <p>If you would like to work extensively with one of our unique archival collections we recommend that you&nbsp;<a href="mailto:specialcollections@ucr.edu" target="_blank">contact us in advance</a> so that the requested materials will be ready upon your arrival.</p> 
      <p>The collection may be searched online by using the library's online catalog, SCOTTY. You can limit your search to Eaton's holdings by choosing &quot;Eaton Science Fiction Collection&quot; on the drop-down menu.</p> 
     </div> 
     <div class="nota-bene  l_black_box"> 
      <h4>&nbsp;</h4> 
      <h4>&nbsp;</h4> 
      <h4>Restrictions on the Collection</h4> 
      <p>There are several restrictions and requirements for individuals accessing the Eaton Collection.</p> 
      <ul class="standout"> 
       <li>Materials may only be used in the Reading Room. They cannot be checked out.</li> 
       <li>A valid photo ID (e.g. driver's license, student I.D., passport) must be presented and will be held at the information desk during your visit.</li> 
       <li>Pencils and laptop computers are permitted in the reading room. All other personal items, including cell phones, purses and backpacks, must be stored. PENS ARE NOT PERMITTED UNDER ANY CIRCUMSTANCES.</li> 
       <li>Researchers may use digital cameras after signing the <a href="http://library.ucr.edu/content/collections/spcol/pdf/forms_camera_use_policy.pdf">Guidelines for Camera Use</a> form. No flash photography is permitted.</li> 
       <li>No food or drink is permitted in the reading room.</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p>For more information on restrictions, please visit our UCR Libraries <a href="http://library.ucr.edu/view/collections/spcol/using.html">&quot;Using our Collections&quot;</a> web page.</p>
