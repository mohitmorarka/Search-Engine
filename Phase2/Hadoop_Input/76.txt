      <div class="span12"> 
       <h1 class="page-header"> Initiatives </h1> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <!-- Example row of columns --> 
     <div class="row"> 
      <div class="span4 sidebar categories"> 
       <div class="row"> 
        <div class="span4 blustripe"> 
         <h5 class="dotted-bottom">Initiatives</h5> 
         <ul class="dotted-list"> 
          <li class="active"><a href="index.html">All initiatives</a></li> 
          <li class=""><a href="institutional-initiatives/index.html">Institutional initiatives</a></li> 
          <li class=""><a href="educational-initiatives/index.html">Educational initiatives</a></li> 
          <li class=""><a href="access-affordability-initiatives/index.html">Access &amp; affordability initiatives</a></li> 
         </ul> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
      <div class="span8 dotted-top" role="main"> 
       <p>How do we strive to ensure that the University of California remains the best public university system in the world? By looking hard at every aspect of the enterprise and asking: Can we do better? Can we be more cost-effective? More sustainable? Where can we capitalize on the power of our collective size to work together?</p> 
       <p>Here's a sampling of initiatives led by the Office of the President that will help keep UC vital for the next 150 years.</p> 
       <div class="dotted-cols"> 
        <div class="row-fluid"> 
         <div class="span6"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://www.universityofcalifornia.edu/accountability/">Accountability <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>The annual Accountability Report assesses the university's progress in meeting key teaching, research and public service goals.</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="span6 even"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/paying-for-uc/glossary/blue-and-gold/index.html">Blue and Gold Opportunity Plan <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>UC's Blue and Gold Opportunity Plan will cover educational and student services fees for California residents whose families earn less than $80,000 a year and qualify for financial aid.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="row-fluid"> 
         <div class="span6"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://campusclimate.ucop.edu/">Campus climate <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>The Office of the President is leading an effort to conduct a comprehensive assessment of campus climate, gathering a pool of data to help build environments where people feel respected and valued.</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="span6 even"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://campusprotestreport.universityofcalifornia.edu">Civil Disobedience Initiative <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>CDI is working to implement the recommendations of the Robinson Edley Report, which identified practices that facilitate free expression and encourage lawful protest activity while protecting the safety of the UC community.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="row-fluid"> 
         <div class="span6"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://Ucfuture.universityofcalifornia.edu">Commission on the Future <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>How can UC maintain access, quality and affordability in the face of severely limited state resources? The commission's November 2010 report addressing that question is shaping UC’s actions.</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="span6 even"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://workingsmarter.universityofcalifornia.edu/projects/data-improvement-project/overview/">Data Improvement Project <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>By creating an integrated system that corrals systemwide data, the Office of the President is able to identify opportunities for efficiencies, spot trends, and support better business decisions.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="row-fluid"> 
         <div class="span6"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="/enterprise-risk-management/index.html">Enterprise Risk Management</a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>The ERM program, which aims to protect people and prevent loss to the system, has already reduced the university’s overall premium costs by more than $80 million in 2010-11.</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="span6 even"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="/academic-affairs/innovative-learning-technology-initiative/index.html">Innovative Learning Technology Initiative (ILTI)</a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>ILTI aims to expand the use of innovative and high-quality online instruction to improve student access to high-demand courses.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="row-fluid"> 
         <div class="span6"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://youcan.universityofcalifornia.edu/">Project You Can <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>Ten campuses, four years, $1 billion: That’s the Project You Can goal, a systemwide fundraising effort to grow the pool of scholarships and fellowships that help keep UC affordable and accessible.</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="span6 even"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://calteach.universityofcalifornia.edu">Science and Mathematics Teacher Initiative (CalTeach) <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>Through CalTeach, UC recruits and prepares mathematics and science majors for teaching careers by providing special coursework and field experiences in K-12 schools.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="row-fluid"> 
         <div class="span6"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://sustainability.universityofcalifornia.edu/">Sustainability <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>UC is aggressively moving to reduce its impact on the environment -- from energy-saving buildings to cutting waste in food service.</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="span6 even"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="/graduate-studies/initiatives-outreach/uc-hbcu-program/index.html">UC-HBCU Initiative</a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>Seeks to improve the representation of African Americans/Blacks in UC graduate programs by investing in relationships and efforts between UC faculty and Historically Black Colleges and Universities.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="row-fluid"> 
         <div class="span6"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="/uconline/index.html">UC Online</a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>UC Online serves as a resource to assist faculty, campuses and programs in developing high-quality online offerings.</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="span6 even"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://workingsmarter.universityofcalifornia.edu/projects/ucpath/overview/">UCPath <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>UCPath is an ambitious effort to transition the entire UC system to a single payroll and human resources system – a move that is expected to save the university as much as $100 million a year.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="row-fluid"> 
         <div class="span6"> 
          <h3 class="blue"> <a href="http://workingsmarter.universityofcalifornia.edu/">Working Smarter <span class="icon new-win">Link</span></a> </h3> 
          <cite>
           <metatitle /></cite> 
          <p>By cutting costs and generating revenue through a series of focused new initiatives, UC is on track to save $500 million over five years.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div>

      <h3>Ensure Your FERPA Compliance</h3> 
     </div> 
     <div class="span-9"> 
      <span class="warning">Important:</span> 
      <ul class="orange-bullets"> 
       <li>For definitions of what constitutes &quot;personally identifiable information&quot; or &quot;educational records,&quot; see <a href="http://www.ucop.edu/general-counsel/_files/ed-affairs/ferpa101.pdf" target="_blank">UC FERPA 101</a>.</li> 
       <li><a href="/registrar/help-desk/default.aspx">Our office</a> handles all official transcripts and subpoena requests.</li> 
      </ul> 
      <h3>Your Access</h3> 
      <p>You have access to a student's educational records for the sole purpose of performing your job responsibly. You are granted this access as a school official with &quot;legitimate educational interest.&quot; This is defined as:</p> 
      <ul class="orange-bullets wide"> 
       <li>Performing a task specified in a position description or contract</li> 
       <li>Performing a task related to a student’s education or discipline</li> 
       <li>Providing a service to a student</li> 
       <li>Maintaining safety and security on campus</li> 
      </ul> 
     </div> 
     <div class="span-9 last"> 
      <div class="sidebar"> 
       <img src="/Images/folder.png" style="float: right; margin: 4px;" /> 
       <div class="title">
         FERPA Resources: 
       </div> 
       <ul> 
        <li><a href="http://www.ucop.edu/general-counsel/_files/ed-affairs/ferpa101.pdf" target="_new">UC FERPA 101</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://www.ed.gov/policy/gen/guid/fpco/ferpa/index.html" target="_new">US Department of Education and FERPA</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://policy.ucop.edu/doc/2710533/PACAOS-130" target="_new">UC FERPA Policy</a></li> 
        <li><a href="../privacy-ferpa/ucr-ferpa-policy.aspx">UCR FERPA Policy</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://www.dol.gov/dol/ppii.htm" target="_new">US Protection of Personal Information Policies</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://www2.ed.gov/policy/gen/guid/fpco/index.html" target="_new">US Department of Education Family Policy Compliance Office</a></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <div class="span-18 last"> 
      <h3>Your Responsibilities</h3> 
      <ul class="orange-bullets wide"> 
       <li>You have a responsibility to protect the confidentiality of a student’s educational records, regardless of the medium in which those records are presented.</li> 
       <li>Posting grades using the student's name, student ID number or any portion of the social security number violates FERPA.</li> 
       <li>You may not disclose personally identifiable information about a student nor permit inspection of a student’s educational records without that student’s written consent (e.g., a signed department-specific release form).</li> 
       <li>It is your responsibility to verify that student directory information is not restricted before releasing it. You can view which information a student has restricted on screen 011 in the Student Information System.</li> 
       <li>Reference letters written on behalf of a student do not give you authorization to disclose that student’s records or performance details without that student’s written consent (e.g., a signed department-specific release form.</li> 
       <li>In general, student information may only be shared with the student’s consent, in a health or safety emergency, or pursuant to another FERPA exception.</li> 
      </ul> 
      <h3>Required FERPA Training (Subject to Change)</h3> 
      <p>UCR employees (and student employees) who want to access the Student Information System (SIS) must complete special training and sign a statement of FERPA understanding. <a href="http://emsystems.ucr.edu/Pages/SISTraining.aspx" target="_blank">You can do both online here.</a></p> 
      <h3>How Can Students Release Records?</h3> 
      <p>Students can customize their public directory information, authorize Growl users (e.g., family) and sign department-specific release forms. See <a href="/registrar/privacy-ferpa/disclosure-policies.aspx">Disclosure Policies</a> for more details.</p> 
      <h3>About Department-Specific Release Forms</h3> 
      <p>Your department may modify this <a href="/content/pdfs/permission_to_release_educational_records.pdf" target="_blank">Permission to Release Educational Records</a> form. It’s a way for students to grant your department permission to release their records to third parties. It must be completed prior to the disclosure of any private information. </p> 
      <h3>Questions?</h3> 
      <p>FERPA questions should be directed to the Campus FERPA Officer at 951-827-3427 or via email (<a href="mailto:bracken.dailey@ucr.edu">bracken.dailey@ucr.edu</a>). </p>
