     <div id="tab3"> 
      <div class="texttab-column"> 
       <ul class="alt3"> 
        <li><strong>September 10, 2013</strong><br /> American Chemical Society issues guidelines for safer research laboratories entitled <em><a href="http://www.acs.org/content/dam/acsorg/about/governance/committees/chemicalsafety/identifying-and-evaluating-hazards-in-research-laboratories-draft.pdf">Identifying and&nbsp;Evaluating Hazards in Research Laboratories</a>.</em>.</li> 
        <li><strong>June 12, 2013</strong><br /> <a href="/laboratory/policies.html">Laboratory Safety Policies</a> were approved by UC Office of the President involving: 1) Training, 2) Personal Protective Equipment, and 3) Minors in Laboratories and Shops.</li> 
        <li><strong>September 10, 2012</strong><br /> <a href="http://ehs.ucr.edu/laboratory/ucregentsagreement/laboratorysafetymanual.html">Laboratory Safety Manual</a> and Chemical Hygiene Plan was revised and made available to the campus.</li> 
        <li><strong>July 27, 2012</strong><br /> <a href="http://www.ehs.ucr.edu/laboratory/ucregentsagreement">UC Regents Agreement</a> was signed accepting responsibility for charges filed by the Los Angeles District Attorney in association with the death of UCLA research associate Sheherbano (Sheri) Sangji.</li> 
        <li><strong>June 8, 2012</strong><br /> <a href="http://www.ehs.ucr.edu/news/20120606risksummit.html">President's Award for Excellence in Environmental Health &amp; Safety (EH&amp;S)</a> was accepted by Russell Vernon during the Risk Summit 2012 conference in Oakland, CA.</li> 
        <li><strong>June 8, 2012</strong><br /> <a href="http://www.cdc.gov/Features/EcoliInfection/">E. coli outbreak</a> investigation by Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). A total of 14 ill persons infected with STEC O145 had been reported in 6 states. Based on interviews conducted to date, a source for these infections has not been identified. If a specific source is identified, public health officials will advise the public and take steps to prevent additional illnesses.</li> 
        <li><strong>May 2012</strong><br /> NISOH publishes the <em><a href="http://www.cdc.gov/niosh/docs/2012-147/?goback=%2Egde_4036726_member_272139485#%21">General Safe Practices for Working with Engineered Nanomaterials in&nbsp;Research Laboratories</a>.</em></li> 
        <li><strong>February 6, 2012</strong><br /> <a href="/news/thermadynecylinders.pdf">Notice to Stop Sale and Distribution</a> of Thermadyne/TurboTorch branded cynlinder products (14.1 oz MAP-Pro, 14.1 oz Propylene, and 16 oz MAPP cylinders).</li> 
        <li><strong>January 23, 2012</strong><br /> <a href="http://www.ehs.ucr.edu/news/oshareport2012.pdf">Summary of Work-Related Injuries &amp; Illnesses</a> (OSHA Form 300A) log reported on behalf of the Vice Chancellor of Business &amp; Administrative Services.</li> 
        <li><strong>November 16, 2011</strong><br /> <a href="http://ucanr.org/sites/ucehs/Safety_Spotlight">Risk Services Today</a> newsletter published, courtesy of University of California Office of the President.</li> 
        <li><strong>November 3, 2011</strong><br /> <a href="http://ucanr.org/sites/ucehs/Safety_Spotlight">UC Safety Spotlight Newsletter</a> published with the theme &quot;Fire Prevention&quot;.</li> 
        <li><strong>November 2, 2011</strong><br /> Health Alert: <a href="http://newsroom.ucr.edu/announcements/2011-11-01tb.html">Tuberculosis (TB) case at UC Riverside</a>.</li> 
        <li><strong>October 20, 2011</strong><br /> Chemical Safety Board releases their <a href="http://www.csb.gov/videoroom/detail.aspx?VID=61">Experimenting with Danger</a> video highlighting the tragic laboratory safety accidents at UCLA, Dartmouth, and Texas Tech University.</li> 
        <li><strong>October 12, 2011</strong><br /> Health Alert: <a href="http://www.ehs.ucr.edu/news/mumps.html">Mumps Outbreak at UC Berkeley</a>.</li> 
        <li><strong>September 26, 2011</strong><br /> <a href="http://ehs.ucr.edu/laboratory/audits">Laboratory Audit Form</a> was updated with new links, guidance, and user-friendly questions.</li> 
        <li><strong>September 23, 2011</strong><br /> <a href="http://rside.ucr.edu/blockparty/">Block Party</a> was a success! EH&amp;S kept food from over 40 organizations safe, removed fire and life safety hazards, and managed emergency operations for the campus</li> 
        <li><strong>September 14, 2011</strong><br /> <a href="http://pplant.ucr.edu">Physical Plant</a> to retrofit all laboratories for earthquake safety. For more information, contact <a href="mailto:john.prine@ucr.edu">John Prine</a> (951) 827-2033.</li> 
        <li><strong>September 1, 2011</strong><br /> <a href="http://ucanr.org/sites/ucehs/Safety_Spotlight">UC Safety Spotlight Newsletter</a> published with the theme &quot;Laboratory Safety&quot;.</li> 
        <li><strong>June 23, 2011</strong><br /> <b>EVC/Provost</b> Dallas Rabenstein releases a &quot;<a href="http://ehs.ucr.edu/news/safetyfirst20110623.pdf">Safety First: A Collective and Individual Responsibility</a>&quot; message to faculty.</li> 
        <li><strong>March 11, 2011</strong><br /> <a href="http://www.ehs.ucr.edu/news/20110315japanreactorcrisis.html">Earthquake in Japan initiated a reactor crisis</a></li> 
        <li><strong>June 9, 2010</strong><br /> <a href="http://www.ehs.ucr.edu/news/20100609risksummit.html">Russell Vernon, Ph.D. honored with the &quot;Power of 10&quot; award.</a></li> 
       </ul>

      <h3>eForms Background</h3> 
      <p>During the past decade, the University of California, Riverside (UCR) has deployed many online systems that have automated previously paper-intensive, manual business processes. These systems include the online travel planning and expense reporting system (iTravel), the electronic campus extramural fund approval form (eCAF), and the web based campus vehicle request and reservation system.</p> 
      <p>Despite this wonderful progress, many paper based processes still exist on campus, several of which require both departmental and control point approvals prior to processing and completion of service requests.</p> 
      <h4>eForms - A Campus-wide Approach to Form Automation and Management</h4> 
      <p>The eForms system is designed to automate various paper based processes via a standard, campus-wide approach. eForms is tightly integrated with the Enterprise Access Control System (EACS), and uses a common method for creating, routing, approving, and processing of a wide variety of campus forms and manual systems.</p> 
      <p>eForms are created by Computing and Communications (C&amp;C) in close partnership with campus service providers (ranging from C&amp;C itself, to Accounting, Academic Planning and Budget, to any department / unit wishing to automate a paper based business process).</p> 
      <h4>Using the eForm system, service requests are electronically routed and processed using the following eForms roles:</h4> 
      <h5>eForms Departmental / Organizational Roles</h5> 
      <ul> 
       <li>eForms Transactor (creates requests for various campus services)</li> 
       <li>eForms Departmental Approver (submits requests or routes to Organizational Approver depending on eForm requirement)</li> 
       <li>Organizational Approver (submits request)</li> 
      </ul> 
      <h5>eForms Service Provider Roles</h5> 
      <ul> 
       <li>eForm Owner (approves service request)</li> 
       <li>eForm Coordinator (coordinates completion of service request)</li> 
       <li>eForm Analyst (completes and processes service request)</li> 
      </ul>
