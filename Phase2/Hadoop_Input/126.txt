        <p> <img src="images/biologo.jpg" alt="photo" border="0" align="center" /> </p> 
       </div> 
       <p>The Department of Biology at UCR conducts research, teaching, and service in many areas of the life sciences, including animal behavior, behavioral endocrinology, bioinformatics, biomechanics, cell biology, conservation biology, developmental biology, ecology, evolution, organismal biology, molecular biology, phylogenetics, physiology, population biology, and systematics.&nbsp; The undergraduate major is designed for students interested in a variety of careers, including basic and applied research, education, environmental science, medicine, and science policy.&nbsp; Undergraduate students are encouraged to participate in faculty-mentored research projects (please check <a href="http://www.biology.ucr.edu/people/faculty.html">this listing</a> and contact individual faculty with interests similar to your own). &nbsp;Our graduate program is named <a href="http://eeob.ucr.edu/">Evolution, Ecology, and Organismal Biology</a> (EEOB). Our faculty also participate in several interdepartmental graduate programs, including <a href="http://www.cell.ucr.edu/">Cell, Molecular, and Developmental Biology</a> (CMDB), <a href="http://www.ggb.ucr.edu/">Genetics, Genomics, and Bioinformatics</a> (GGB), <a href="http://biomed.ucr.edu/index.php?option=com_content&amp;task=view&amp;id=83&amp;Itemid=115">Biomedical Sciences</a>, and <a href="http://neuro.ucr.edu/">Neuroscience</a>.&nbsp; Please browse this site to discover more about our department. &nbsp;You may use the search function in the upper right of this page to find specific items on our web pages.</p>

         <ul class="dotted-list"> 
          <li class="active"><a href="index.html">Introduction</a></li>

      <div class="span8 dotted-top" role="main"> 
       <p>This collection of tools and resources is intended to help employees orient themselves to the UCOP community and navigate their way through various procedures and services.</p> 
       <p>Key contacts:</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li><strong>Business Resource Center | <a href="/business-resource-center/teams/index.html">Contact BRC</a><br /></strong>The Business Resource Center (BRC) handles general procurement, travel and entertainment, ledger reconciliation, payroll and event planning.</li> 
        <li><strong>IT Service Hub | <a href="https://ucop.service-now.com">Access IT Service Hub</a> </strong><strong>| <a href="mailto:servicedesk@ucop.edu">Email ServiceDesk</a></strong><br />Employees report IT incidents, make requests, check status, and provide feedback via this portal. If there is an emergency, you should call (510) 987-0457 or even stop by the ServiceDesk bullpen at 7116 Franklin.</li> 
        <li><strong>Security at Oakland-based office buildings</strong><br />If you have a security question or want to report a security issue, call the security desk in the Franklin Lobby at (510) 987-9790. At Kaiser, call (510) 272-0909. At Broadway or OSF (415-20th St), call Chief Building Manager Roman Starno at (510) 587-6100 or Work Management Center at (510) 987-0600.</li> 
        <li><strong>UCOP Local Human Resources | <a href="/local-human-resources/staff-contacts/index.html">Contact Local HR</a></strong><br />UCOP HR Business Partners, assigned by department, provide guidance and support to all managers, supervisors and employees regarding all HR services.</li> 
        <li><strong>Work Management Center | Franklin 6314 | (510) 987-0600 | <a href="mailto:buildingservices@ucop.edu">Email WMC</a><br /></strong>The Work Management Center (WMC) is responsible for managing and facilitating daily operational needs for all UCOP employees, such as building access, ID badges, iVisitor, iRequest, parking access and office space. </li> 
       </ul> 
       <p>We welcome any recommendations for revisions, additions or other improvements.&nbsp; Please send your ideas to&nbsp;<a href="mailto:Thuvan.Le@ucop.edu">Thuvan Le</a>.</p>

     <h3>NOTES: </h3> 
     <ul style="list-style: circle;"> 
      <li>When creating a new course, double-click in the <strong><u>blue fields</u>, enter information in the <u>white </u></strong><strong>fields, click in the boxes, and choose from the drop-down lists.</strong></li> 
      <li>Use the information on the <strong>Worksheet - Request for a New Course</strong> supplied by the faculty member to begin filling in the information on CRAMS.</li> 
      <li><strong>DO NOT</strong> put periods after titles, subtitles, prerequisites, grading statements that you type, or repeatability statements that you type.</li> 
      <li><strong>DO</strong> put periods after sentences in the description and in any other non-Catalog text.</li> 
      <li><strong>Save:</strong> You can save your work at any time while you are entering the information by clicking the Save Work button. You can access courses that you saved and exited by going to the CRAMS main menu and clicking Work-in-Progress Queue (WIPQ).</li> 
      <li><strong>Proofread and Print:</strong> Save your work and then click the Proofread button to proofread (and print) the course at any stage during creation or modification.</li> 
      <li><strong>If the course is to be cross-listed with an existing course:</strong> Do not use the instructions in this section to create a new course to be cross-listed with an existing course. Instead, modify the Cross-listed With field on the existing course (the modification will automatically create the new course).</li> 
      <li><strong>If the course is very similar to an existing course:</strong> If a new course is very similar to an existing course, you may save time and work if you follow the instructions in the Save as New Course section of the &quot;<a href="modify-a-course.aspx"><strong>Modify a Course Guide</strong></a>&quot; instead of the instructions in this section.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <h3>Steps<br /> </h3> 
     <table style="width: 100%" cellspacing="2" cellpadding="0"> 
      <tbody> 
       <tr> 
        <td valign="top" align="left"> 
         <ol> 
          <li>At the CRAMS main menu, click <strong>Create a New Course. </strong></li> 
         </ol> 
         <table style="width: 40px; height: 10px" border="0" cellspacing="2" cellpadding="0"> 
          <tbody> 
           <tr> 
            <td><strong> <br /> <img border="0" hspace="0" alt="" align="top" src="../../images/course-mgt/greenarrow.png" width="45" longdesc="#" height="24" /><br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> <br /> </strong></td> 
            <td><strong> <img border="0" alt="" src="../../images/course-mgt/chapter7one.jpg" /><br /> </strong></td> 
           </tr> 
          </tbody> 
         </table> <br /> <br /> 
         <ol start="2"> 
          <li>If necessary, at the <strong>College/Department tab</strong> change the college, department, and contact information.<br /> <br /> </li> 
         </ol> <p align="center"></p> <p align="center"> <img border="0" alt="" src="../../images/course-mgt/chapter7two1.jpg" /> </p> <p> <br /> <br /> </p> 
         <ol start="3"> 
          <li>Click the <strong>Course Identification tab</strong> and complete all applicable fields.</li> 
         </ol> <p align="center"> <br /> <br /> <img border="0" alt="" src="../../images/course-mgt/chapter7three1.jpg" /><br /> <br /> <br /> </p> <p align="center"> <br /> <br /> </p> 
         <ol style="list-style: lower-alpha; margin-left:15px;"> 
          <li><strong>Subject Area:</strong> Choose from the drop-down list (if no subject area appears, check that you have selected a college and department at the College/Department tab).</li> 
          <li><strong>Course Number</strong> and Course Suffix: Type in the fields. After you save the course (using the Save Work button), the course number and suffix will appear and remain in the blue bar at the very top of the screen.<br /> </li> 
          <li><strong>Course Title</strong> and <strong>E-Z Segment Subtitle</strong>: Double-click to enter this information.</li> 
          <li><strong>1st Effective Quarter and Year:</strong> Choose from the drop-down lists.</li> 
          <li><strong>Course Level and Course Type:</strong> Choose from the drop-down lists.</li> 
          <li><strong>Offered in summer sessions only:</strong> Click in the box.</li>

      <p>The process of accreditation has two phases, the Institutional Capacity phase and the Educational Effectiveness phase. The first phase involved demonstrating that UCR has well-defined goals and objectives and the resources, policies and procedures, commitment, and experience necessary to achieve these goals. These issues were addressed in the Preparatory Review Report submitted to WASC by UCR on December 20, 2007 (see Preparatory Review Report on the left). WASC sent a team of experts to the UCR campus March 12-14, 2008, to follow up on that report. The team met with many campus individuals and groups to determine the degree to which <abbr>UCR</abbr> has defined its educational objectives and has the capacity to achieve those objectives. <span style="font-size: x-small; font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial;">For the written report of the team, see <a href="/docs/WASC%20Visit%20Report%20March%2008%20Final.pdf"><span style="color: #2668b2;">Visiting Team Capacity Report</span></a>.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></span></p> 
      <p>The <a href="http://wasc.ucr.edu/eer.html">Educational Effectiveness Report</a> was submitted to WASC&nbsp;on July 12, 2009, and&nbsp;was followed by a WASC team visit October 27-29, 2009.&nbsp;&nbsp;The team met with many campus individuals and groups to determine the degree to which UCR has demonstrated its educational effectiveness.&nbsp; For the written report of the team, see <a href="/docs/WASC Visit Report October 2009 Final.pdf">Visiting Team Educational Effectiveness Report</a>.</p>
