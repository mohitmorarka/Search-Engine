      <h3>Protecting your privacy is very important to us.</h3> 
      <div>
       &nbsp;
      </div> 
      <div>
       All of the information we collect will be used only as described.
      </div> 
      <div>
       Site activity data are collected throughout the www.alumni.ucr.edu web site. These data include information on domains, browsers, operating systems, and pages accessed. This information is used only in aggregate, and only to better understand general site usage patterns, improve site usability, and add services which will better serve our visitors' needs and interests. No attempt is made to track individuals or preserve any information relating to identified users. 
       <br />
       <br />UCR maintains a campus database of your name, address, phone number, email, college and year of graduation to allow us to stay in touch with you (send you the UCR magazine), and to let your old classmates find you (if you so permit). This information may be used by University of California, Riverside for the development of invitation lists to university-oriented programs and events, as well as for university-affiliated marketing programs and benefit offerings. If you do not wish to have this information assigned to these purposes, please notify the Office of Alumni and Constituent Relations at (800) 426-ALUM or at 
       <a href="mailto:ucralum@ucr.edu">ucralum@ucr.edu</a>.
      </div> 
      <div>
       &nbsp;
      </div> 
      <div>
       SB569 - California State Senate Bill 569 became law on January 1, 2006. It allows public universities like UCR to continue to provide individual alumni cost-effective group rates and discounts for home, travel and financial programs through affinity partnerships. Such programs provide significant savings to California public university students and alumni. Resources from these partnerships enable the UCR Alumni Association to administer a comprehensive scholarship program for the benefit of our students. Alumni programs and services are jeopardized as the state’s general fund is increasingly challenged to meet the needs of higher education, so such affinity partnerships are an important source of university funding.
      </div> 
      <div>
       &nbsp;
      </div> 
      <div>
       Please note that the University of California Riverside and the UCR Alumni Association never sell or otherwise disclose personal information such as your social security number, academic records, etc. to any outside organizations, companies or individuals.
      </div> 
      <div>
       The new law requires public universities to provide alumni the option to not receive information on such cost-saving offers from affinity partners. 
       <a href="http://www.alumni.ucr.edu/partnermailings">If you prefer NOT to receive information on such discounts, you can click here to complete the online Privacy Notice Form</a>. (Please allow up to 45 days to remove your name from our partner lists.)
      </div> 
      <h3>Highlanders Online</h3> 
      <div>
       The Highlanders Online Community includes, among other services, an&nbsp;alumni search tool. Your information may be viewed by other UCR alumni through this alumni search tool. There is a registration process that an individual must complete to access the Highlanders Online. Only UCR alumni have access to the community. Occasionally, you may receive electronic email newsletters. You may remove yourself from this mailing list following the directions located at the footer of those messages.
      </div> 
      <div>
       &nbsp;
      </div> 
      <div>
       At any time, you can change your personal information in the alumni directory, or choose to have the information hidden.&nbsp; To do this, log in to the Highlanders Online.&nbsp; From there, click on Account Settings, then Profile and Privacy.&nbsp; On the Profile and Privacy page you can update, add or delete any information in the boxes provided.&nbsp; Also, choose whether or not to make each piece of information private by checking&nbsp;the boxes to the right of each field.&nbsp; Click &quot;Update&quot; when you have made all of the changes you would like to make.
      </div> 
      <div>
       &nbsp;
      </div> 
      <div>
       If at any time you have questions about how your information will be used, you may email us at 
       <a href="mailto:ucralum@ucr.edu">ucralum@ucr.edu</a>.
      </div> 
      <div>
       Thank you for visiting our Web site. We look forward to continuing to improve this site to exceed your expectations.
      </div>

     <p>As Department Chair, it is my pleasure to introduce you to one of the most politically dynamic, and intellectually creative departments in the University of California system.&nbsp; Beginning in 2009-2010, the inaugural year of the Ethnic Studies Ph.D. program, we have been committed to aggressively building this program so that it produces the highest quality scholars, and supports students whose work is guided by the deepest possible commitment to critical knowledge production and social transformation. Crucially, the initiation of our graduate program announced our significance as a space of rigorous, socially engaged intellectual work within the university setting.</p> 
     <p></p> 
     <p>The scholarly practices of Ethnic Studies are both practical and theoretical, addressing questions of power, social movement, freedom, liberation, community, culture, and history. These commitments reflect in the courses we teach, the books and essays we write, the events we organize, and the lectures, public interviews, and speeches we give.&nbsp; (You will find links to and direct downloads of some of our work <a href="publications_media/index.html">on this website</a>.)</p> 
     <p>Our academic and scholarly distinction lies in our critical, transformative approach to knowledge and teaching. We neither imitate nor replicate the paradigms and curricula of traditional academic departments. The political-intellectual work of Ethnic Studies is guided by deep engagements with the living histories of people’s struggles to survive and transform the legacies of racial colonialism, land conquest, hemispheric chattel slavery, genocide, imperial war, and other forms of institutionalized social and cultural violence.</p> 
     <p> In these pages you will find a description of who we are, how we think, and what we do. You can learn about our undergraduate majors and our new Ph.D. program, which represent some of the most innovative, socially engaged curricula in this or any university. We highlight a few of our recently graduated Ethnic Studies students, each of whom is making a significant impact in their areas of work and study, from grass roots activism to graduate and professional school. Finally, this website offers a sense of how our work reflects and carries forward a long history of collective struggles for freedom, respect, community, and intellectual self-determination.</p> 
     <p>An extended description of our vision of Ethnic Studies appears below, and a full outline of course offerings and requirements for our undergraduate and graduate programs is included in other sections of this website. I hope those of you who are current or aspiring graduate and undergraduate students will consider pursuing a degree in Ethnic Studies, whether an undergraduate major or Ph.D. I also invite our colleagues from other departments, schools, colleges, and universities to contact us with proposals for exchanging ideas and developing collaborative projects.</p> 
     <p>We are an inherently dynamic department:&nbsp; students, faculty, and staff are constantly involved in shaping and re-shaping the meaning and practice of Ethnic Studies. We hope you will participate in this exciting and necessary work.</p> 
     <p>&nbsp;</p>
