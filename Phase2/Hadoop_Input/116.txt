        <p>Assistant Professor of Computer Science and Engineering Philip Brisk has been awarded a prestigious National Science Foundation CAREER grant for his research to create design automation software for laboratories-on-a-chip.</p>

       <div class="l_news_list"> 
        <h4><a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/news/2014/2014-02-27b.html"><img alt="Elisa Franco" src="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/images/news/thumb_franco.jpg" />Franco and Research Team Discover New Behaviors of Synthetic Molecular Oscillators</a></h4> 
        <p>Assistant Professor of Mechanical Engineering Elisa Franco and a team of researchers have made important discoveries regarding the behavior of a synthetic molecular oscillator, which could serve as a timekeeping device to control artificial cells.</p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="l_news_list"> 
        <h4><a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/news/2014/2014-02-27a.html"><img alt="Alexander Khitun" src="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/images/news/thumb_khitun.jpg" />Researchers Demonstrate Holographic Memory Device That Could Improve Electronics</a></h4> 
        <p>A team of researchers at BCOE and the Russian Academy of Science have demonstrated a new type of holographic memory device that could provide unprecedented data storage capacity and data processing capabilities in electronic devices. The new memory device uses spin waves - a collective oscillation of spins in magnetic materials - instead of optical beams.</p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="l_news_list"> 
        <h4><a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/news/2014/2014-02-27.html"><img alt="Alexander Balandin" src="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/images/news/Thumb_balandin_new.jpg" />Balandin Elected Fellow of Materials Research Society</a></h4> 
        <p>Professor of Electrical Engineering Alexander A. Balandin has been elected a fellow of the Materials Research Society. He is recognized for &quot;pioneering contributions on the thermal properties of graphene and low-dimensional materials; seminal contributions to the study of quantum confinement effects in nanostructures; and leadership in materials education.&quot;</p> 
       </div>

        <p>Jake Lanphere, a graduate student in chemical and environmental engineering, has been awarded a 2014 Graduate Student Award in Environmental Chemistry by the American Chemical Society, it was announced recently.</p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="l_news_list">

        <p>Southern California Edison has provided $30,000 in support for the MESA (Mathematics, Engineering and Science Achievement) program at the Bourns College of Engineering, including funds for its outreach program at the Sherman Indian School in Riverside.</p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="l_news_list"> 
        <h4><a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/news/2014/2014-01-15.html"><img alt="Professor Bahman Anvari" src="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/images/news/thumb_anvari_aaas.jpg" />Anvari Named Fellow of SPIE</a></h4> 
        <p>Professor of Bioengineering Bahman Anvari has been named a fellow of the International Society for Optics and Photonics (SPIE) for achievements in phototherapy, optical nano-materials, and laser trapping methods for membrane mechanics studies. SPIE is a not-for-profit international society advancing an interdisciplinary approach to the science and application of light.</p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="l_news_list">

        <p>Mechanical engineering Ph.D. candidate Omid Khandan received a Best Poster Award in the Society-Wide Micro and Nano Technology Forum at the 2013 ASME International Mechanical Engineering Congress and Exposition in San Diego, Calif., Nov. 15-21, 2013.</p>

       <div class="l_news_list">

       <p>UC policies can help you &quot;blow the whistle&quot; on improper activities, and protect you from retaliation.</p> 
      </div> 
      <div class="span4">
       <p><span>The University of California has a responsibility to conduct its affairs ethically and in compliance with the law. If you suspect that a UC employee is engaged in&nbsp;</span><a href="faqs/faq-being-a-whistleblower.html#5">improper governmental activities</a><span>, you should know that UC has policies that can show you how to &quot;blow the whistle&quot; and can protect you from retaliation if the need arises. T<span>his website provides important information for employees and supervisors about the whistleblower policies.</span></span></p>

      <h3>Review and Approve Proposals</h3> 
      <p>All course proposals follow a specific approval route. When it's your turn to provide feedback, you will do so in CRAMS. Use this page to connect with guidelines, log in to CRAMS and track the proposal's location in the approval route.</p> 
      <a href="https://cramsportal.ucr.edu/crams/approver.login" target="_blank" class="ctaButton" style="width: 150px; display: block; margin: 40px auto 0 auto;">Approver Log-in</a>

       <li>Step 2.<b> <a class="noUnderline" href="/crams/course-proposal/syllabus.aspx">Review Syllabus Guidelines</a></b> to see how a course syllabus should be formatted.</li>

       <li>Step 3. When it's your turn, and the proposal is in your queue, <b><a class="noUnderline" href="https://cramsportal.ucr.edu/crams/approver.login" target="_blank">log in to CRAMS</a> </b>and provide feedback.</li> 
       <p></p> 
       <li>Step 4. If the proposal is not yet in your queue, you can find out where it is along the approval route by <b><a class="noUnderline" href="/crams/location-of-courses/default.aspx">tracking your proposal</a>.</b></li>
