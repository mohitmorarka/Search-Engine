        <p>Yunzeng Wang, dean of the School of Business Administration</p>

       <p>RIVERSIDE, Calif. (<a href="http://www.ucr.edu/">www.ucr.edu</a>) — <a href="http://soba.ucr.edu/directory/faculty.html?netid=yunzengw">Yunzeng Wang</a>, who has served an interim dean of the University of California, Riverside <a href="http://soba.ucr.edu/">School of Business Administration</a> since October 2011, has been named permanent dean.</p> 
       <p>“I’m excited to be able to appoint Yunzeng Wang as dean,” said Dallas Rabenstein, provost of UC Riverside. “He has done an admirable job as interim dean, and I look forward to his continued leadership of the school.”</p> 
       <p>Since being named interim dean, Wang has overseen the launch of three graduate business programs: a master’s degree in finance, a master’s degree in accounting and a flexible MBA program. The addition of these programs, along with growth of the traditional MBA program, has nearly doubled the number of graduate students at the school.</p> 
       <p>He has also significantly increased fundraising, worked to re-engage business school alumni and continued the annual <a href="http://soba.ucr.edu/news_events/forecast.html">regional economic forecast conference</a>, which drew record attendance and sponsorships in fall 2013.</p> 
       <div id="attachment_20263" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 356px">
        <a href="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/20261/32275054ucr" rel="attachment wp-att-20263"><img class="size-medium wp-image-20263" alt="Yunzeng Wang talks to students at the regional economic forecast conference in Oct. 2013" src="http://ucrtoday.ucr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/32275054UCR-356x236.jpg" width="356" height="236" /></a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">Yunzeng Wang talks to students at the regional economic forecast conference in Oct. 2013</p>
       </div> 
       <p>In addition, he has increased outreach to Inland Southern California. More than 200 area high school students attended the 2013 economic forecast conference. And, in the recent months, the business school has signed agreements with several local companies and public agencies to provide tuition-assistance to their employees.</p> 
       <p>Wang joined the School of Business Administration &nbsp;in 2008 as the Dean’s Distinguished Scholar in Supply Chain Management and professor of finance and management science. He also served as an associate dean in the school prior to becoming interim dean.</p> 
       <p>Prior to arriving at UC Riverside, Wang was a professor at the University of Texas at Dallas and a faculty member at the Weatherhead School of Management at Case Western Reserve University in Cleveland, Ohio.</p> 
       <p>Wang’s research interests include supply chain management, technology acquisition and adoption strategy, US-China economic development and trade, stochastic optimization and game theory.</p> 
       <p>He earned his Ph.D. in operations management at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania.&nbsp; He has a master’s degree in management sciences from the University of Waterloo in Canada and a master’s degree in engineering from the Harbin Institute of Technology in China. His undergraduate degree is in electrical engineering from Shandong University in China.</p> 
       <p>Established in 1972, the UC Riverside School of Business Administration, home of the A. Gary Anderson Graduate School of Management, is the first professional school of its kind in the region, and the only one at a research-based institution. Leveraging its location at the nexus of commerce, the UCR business school seeks to develop world-class leaders and scholars who are as diverse as the challenges they face and the enterprises they grow.</p>

      <p> <span class="start">UCR is committed to a policy of</span> nondiscrimination in personnel decisions and has initiated a number of procedures to implement that policy. In addition, UCR is committed to strategic action to produce desired increases in the numbers of under-represented minorities and women in the faculty and staff, as well as the disabled veterans (special disabled veterans, Vietnam-era veterans, and any other veterans who served on active duty during a war or in a campaign or expedition for which a campaign badge has been authorized). </p> 
      <p> <span class="start">The Office of Faculty and Staff Affirmative Action</span> works with campus units, create policies, programs, systems and infrastructure to achieve equal employment opportunities for women and minorities, and affirmative action in employment at UCR. The Office is also responsible for developing and maintaining an Affirmative Action Personnel Program Plan that outlines the ways in which UCR will work to achieve the most diverse pool of candidates for open recruitment that occurs on campus. As a federal contractor, UCR is required to have this plan. But beyond our legal requirements, UCR is dedicated to working towards achieving diversity in all areas of campus life including race, ethnicity, physical abilities, sexual identies, veteran statues, religious preference and other cultural characteristics. </p> 
      <p> <span class="start">Each academic year the Chancellor</span>, in conjunction with the Office of Faculty and Staff Affirmative Action, issues a statement to all members of the UCR community summarizing campus policy and objectives with commitment to A Campus of Respect and the UCR Principles of Community. </p> 
      <p> <span class="start">The recruitment and selection of a highly</span> qualified faculty and staff lies at the heart of the UCR's affirmative action program. The Campus Affirmative Action plan emphasizes safeguarding the excellence and integrity in our recruitment, selection, appointment processes and promotion.</p> 
      <p><span class="start">Affirmative Action Office Responsibilities are:</span></p> 
      <ol> 
       <li>Development, implementation, and maintenance of the Affirmative Action Plan, to ensure compliance with existing governmental and university regulations.</li> 
       <li>Advising faculty, administrators, and staff on: a) Current federal and state laws and related regulations on Affirmative Action. Also advising how the Campus may best comply with these governmental requirements. b) Recruitments of applicants; training, promotion, and retention of employees to achieve racial and gender diversity, as required by the Affirmative Action Plan. Also working with other UC campuses, and state and local communities to promote affirmative action objectives.</li> 
       <li>Monitoring campus progress and preparing reports on employee affirmative action. Maintaining statistics on labor force, employee work force, training, recruitment, promotion, separation, with breakdown by gender and ethnicity for reporting purposes.</li> 
       <li>Working with faculty, managers, supervisors, and staff employees, as necessary, to mediate grievances based upon alleged violations of affirmative action policies contained in the Affirmative Action Plan.</li> 
       <li>Advocating, and supporting campus activities which promote diversity, women's issues, minority issues, cultural awareness, career development, and other issues covered by the Affirmative Action Plan.</li> 
      </ol>
