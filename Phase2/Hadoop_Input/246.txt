     <div class="house"> 
      <p>Welcome to UC Riverside's resource center for international opportunities abroad! The Education Abroad Program and Opportunities Abroad Program Office caters to outbound students who have the desire and eligibility to study, intern, work, and volunteer in a foreign country through the Education abroad Program (EAP) and Opportunities Abroad Program (OAP). We invite you to explore our website and seek the program that is right for you! Once you've explored your options, go to MyUCR Abroad to attend an Information Session or make an appointment to speak with an advisor about the steps you need to take in order to study abroad.&nbsp;</p> 
     </div> 
     <div class="column2"> 
      <div class="linkbox">
       <center> 
        <h3>Education Abroad Program (EAP)</h3> 
       </center> 
       <p>EAP is the University of California's premier <a href="http://eap.ucop.edu/Pages/default.aspx" target="_blank">Education Abroad Program</a>. For nearly 50 years, EAP has offered the highest quality study abroad experiences tailored to meet your needs and interests as a UC student.</p> 
       <ul class="big_arrow"> 
        <li><strong>When you study abroad through EAP…</strong></li> 
       </ul> 
       <ul> 
        <li>You remain enrolled at UCR</li> 
        <li>You earn UC credits and grades automatically</li> 
        <li>You benefit from UC EAP's worldwide support infrastructure</li> 
       </ul> 
       <ul class="big_arrow"> 
        <li><strong>EAP Offers…</strong></li> 
       </ul> 
       <ul> 
        <li>Full year, single term, and Summer opportunities in a wide range of academic disciplines</li> 
        <li>Programs for sophomores, juniors, seniors, and graduates</li> 
        <li>Internships, field trips, field study, and research opportunities</li> 
        <li>Courses that satisfy UC breadth/general education and major requirements</li> 
       </ul> 
       <ul class="big_arrow"> 
        <li><strong>Cost</strong></li> 
       </ul> 
       <ul> 
        <li>The cost of study on EAP can be close to the cost of studying on campus and is typically far less than the cost of non-UC study abroad programs</li> 
        <li>You can take your UC financial aid abroad!!! If you do not currently receive UC financial aid, you may qualify for aid while on EAP</li> 
        <li>Each year, EAP students receive $1.3 million in study abroad grants and scholarships. For more information see the EAP website</li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <div class="column2R"> 
      <div class="linkbox">
       <center> 
        <h3>Opportunities Abroad Program (OAP)</h3> 
       </center> 
       <p>OAP is any type of abroad experience under the Planned Opportunities Abroad Agreements (POAA). POAA provides UCR students access to non-University of California EAP study abroad programs, permitting them to study abroad for transfer credit and to use eligible financial aid for direct enrollment or through a third party. It allows students more flexibility in GPA requirements, deadlines, program length, available countries, and universities around the globe because there are so many options. Resources….</p> 
       <ul class="standout"> 
        <li><a href="http://www.studyabroad.com/" target="_blank">http://www.studyabroad.com/</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://www.goabroad.com/" target="_blank">http://www.goabroad.com/</a></li> 
        <li><a href="http://www.iiepassport.org/" target="_blank">http://www.iiepassport.org/</a></li> 
       </ul> 
       <ul class="big_arrow"> 
        <li><strong>Financial Aid</strong></li> 
       </ul> 
       <p>Financial aid may be used based on the Financial Aid Office's set of guidelines. Please consult with a Study Abroad Advisor for requirement clarification.&nbsp;</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li>Students may be eligible to use these types of aid: Cal-Grant, Pell Grant, Federal Academic Competitiveness (ACG) Grant, Loans.</li> 
       </ul> 
       <ul class="big_arrow"> 
        <li><strong>Academic Credit Assessment</strong></li> 
       </ul> 
       <ul> 
        <li>Complete an Academic Planning Form which will be assessed by the Admissions Office, who will determine the transfer of credit based on UCR's transfer credit criteria.</li> 
        <li>Consult with your Academic Advisor and/or Foreign Language Advisor with regards to proposed coursework. They will determine whether or not proposed coursework may fulfill your major or minor requirements.</li> 
        <li>Consult with your College Dean with regards to graduation and residency requirements.</li> 
       </ul> 
      </div>

      <p>Computing and Communications, along with Purchasing has available to staff and faculty, a web based service for acquiring PCs and laptops. This service is conducted through a firm called KST Data Inc., a third party company providing a cost-effective and efficient procurement program for computer products. KST Data is a leading reseller of computers and related products for major manufactures (Dell, HP, IBM, ViewSonic, and others) and printers (Lexmark, Epson, Xerox, HP, and others), at substantial discounts. </p> 
      <p>This service is designed to allow one-stop shopping for staff and faculty members. A key component in this program include “Sweet Spot” pricing based on special discounts on select laptop and desktop configurations. Purchasers can add features and modify these preferred configurations as needed, without losing the Sweet Spot discounts.</p> 
      <p>Dell also has their Premier Website for UCR employees to configure and purchase computers on their own.&nbsp; Once registered through their site a staff or faculty member can navigate and select for personal or business purchases of computers and peripherals.</p>

      <h1>Trouble with the Index</h1> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">California Museum of Photography</h2> 
      <h2 class="spaced-out">Feb 1 - Jun 21, 2014</h2> 
      <h3 class="spaced-out">Opening Reception - Sat, Feb 1, 2014, 6pm - 9pm<br /></h3> 
      <br /> 
      <br /> 
      <p> </p>
      <div>
       <p class="p1">The truth of photographs has always been conflicted. Light hits sensitized paper to produce a picture of the world, capturing a moment in time. <i>This was here</i>, every photograph asserts. But the picture is flat and static, bound by edges and defined by depth of field, reproducible and variable in size: an artificial organization of a world that is spatial and temporal. Nevertheless, <i>this was here</i>. Since photography was invented, it has carried with it this elemental condition of “indexicality.” A photograph must exhibit some element of proof, some trace of reality, to be a photograph.</p>
       <p class="p1">&nbsp;</p>
       <p class="p1">Many artists using photography today are grappling with this complicated state of photographic truth in ways that confront the typical expectations we may have of photographs. It seems that photographs alone can no longer secure their truth claim simply by accurately depicting the world in two dimensions. Through additional maneuvers artists privilege the index over pictorialism, pushing their resulting pictures even to complete abstraction. The activity of the artist is registered loudly in each work; <i>This was here</i> is now <i>I was here</i>.</p>
       <p class="p2"><br /></p>
       <p class="p1"><i>Trouble with the Index</i> features works by Walead Beshty, Matthew Brandt, John Divola, Victoria Fu, Farrah Karapetian, Heather Rasmussen, James Welling, Jennifer West, and Mario Ybarra, Jr.</p>
       <p class="p2"><br /></p>
       <p class="p1"> </p>
       <p class="p1"><i>Trouble with the Index</i> is organized by the California Museum of Photography at UCR ARTSblock and is curated by CMP Curator of Exhibitions Joanna Szupinska-Myers.</p>
      </div>
